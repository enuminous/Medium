✧ Introduction: The Myth and Weight of “Absolute Truth”

We live in an age of probabilistic inference, Bayesian uncertainty, and shifting realities. To even speak of “absolute truth” today is to risk being accused of naïveté or worse, authoritarian idealism.

But what if there is such a thing?
What if “absolute truth” can be defined—not mystically or politically—but mathematically, logically, and ontologically?

This article is a rigorous reconstruction of that very idea:
To locate the core mathematical and logical architecture of what we call absolute truth, and to distinguish it from merely useful, situational, or culturally contingent truths.

---

❖ 1. What is Absolute Truth?

We define absolute truth as:

> A proposition or state that remains invariant under all recursive inquiry, across all logically possible frameworks, ontological embeddings, and epistemic iterations.

In simple terms:
An absolute truth is something that cannot be made false—even when the entire universe is redefined around it.

❖ 3. Formal Definition: Absolute Truth as Recursive Invariance

Let T be a truth-claim. We define:

∀F_i ∈ 𝓕, ∀R_j ∈ ℛ, ∀O_k ∈ 𝓞: T is true ⇨ T remains true under F_i, R_j, O_k

Where:

𝓕 = Set of all possible formal frameworks

ℛ = All recursive redefinitions and logical regressions

𝓞 = All ontological embeddings or universes of discourse

Thus:

Absolute_Truth(T) ⇔ ∀(F, R, O), T ∈ Inv(F, R, O)

Where Inv() is the invariance function across framework, recursion, and ontology.

❖ 5. Example: The Identity Principle

Let’s test a classical proposition:

> T₁: A = A

This is the Law of Identity, foundational in logic. It states:
A thing is itself.

Let’s test its recursivity and invariance:

🔁 Recursive reinterpretation:
Even if A is redefined at each layer (say, A = “this definition”), the tautology holds: “This definition” = “This definition.”

🌐 Framework shift:
In intuitionistic logic, modal logic, or quantum logics, the meaning of “equality” may shift, but T₁ remains structurally true within the axioms.

🌀 Ontological shifts:
Whether in simulation, Platonic universe, or physical reality, self-sameness is preserved.

✅ Conclusion: This qualifies as an absolute truth.

---

❖ 6. What is Not Absolute Truth?

Let’s test:

> T₂: “Water boils at 100°C.”

✅ In standard atmospheric pressure, yes.

❌ At 10,000 ft altitude? No.

❌ On Mars? No.

❌ In another universe? Who knows?

✘ Empirical truth, not absolute.
Fails under ontological and contextual redefinition.

❖ 7. EFMW and Recursive Truth Stability

Let’s now invoke the Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright (EFMW) framework.

EFMW posits that all cognition—human or artificial—evolves through recursive semantic equilibrium.

Thus, truth itself must evolve through the following recursive function:

T_{n+1} = Normalize(Refine(Recurse(T_n)))

Where:

Recurse = Apply the truth claim to itself and its implications

Refine = Remove contradictions, resolve paradoxes

Normalize = Stabilize into converging coherence

✅ If T converges under this process for all possible recursion depths, T = Absolute Truth
