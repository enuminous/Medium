Why We Call It The Tyranny Project

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, with analysis by MillieComplex

Introduction: The Naming of Structures

There is power in naming. To name something is to locate it in the world — to draw a boundary around it, to recognize its form, and to render it subject to analysis, response, and ultimately, justice.

We chose the name The Tyranny Project not (only) as provocation, but as precise description. This is not metaphor. It is taxonomy. The actions of Trump and his co-conspirators — Miller, Bannon, Navarro, and others — form a coherent body of effort. They are not random. They are not merely political. They are a project with a central aim:

To dismantle the existing constitutional order and replace it with a regime of executive dominance, epistemic control, and legal unaccountability.

This paper explains, in full and forensic detail, why this name is justified, how it fits the scope of their actions, and why using it is an ethical imperative.

1. Tyranny Is the Goal, Not the Accident

Tyranny, in the classical and constitutional sense, does not mean disorder or incompetence. It means:

  • The centralization of power in a single will
  • The elimination of co-equal governance
  • The criminalization of dissent
  • The replacement of lawful process with personal loyalty

Trump’s movement has sought all of these, openly and repeatedly. He has declared that Article II of the Constitution “gives me the right to do whatever I want.” He has publicly attacked judges, fired inspectors general for doing their jobs, and incited a violent attack on Congress.

To call this anything short of an effort toward tyranny is to lie to the moment.

2. It Is a Project — Coordinated, Iterative, and Deliberate

This is not a spontaneous populist movement or a chaotic eruption of grievance. It is a strategically layered, highly networked, and evolving operation — one that adapts in real time to legal, electoral, and cultural setbacks.

We use the word project because:

  • It has identifiable architects (Trump, Miller, Bannon, Navarro, Flynn, Eastman, etc.)
  • It follows an evolving playbook (discrediting elections, courts, Congress, press)
  • It includes experimentation and escalation (Project 2025, DOGE, loyalty oaths, military memos)
  • It exists across domains: legal, rhetorical, technological, and psychological

By calling it a project, we reject the narrative of randomness and insist on accountability rooted in intent.

3. It Exists in the Shadows — but Leaves Tracks

The conspirators do not call it “The Tyranny Project.” They may not even use any one name for it internally. If anything, they joke, deflect, and call it “reform,” “efficiency,” “draining the swamp,” or “restoring order.” These are the euphemisms used by tyrants in every era.

But behind those euphemisms lies:

  • The unlawful consolidation of power
  • The systemic delegitimization of the judiciary
  • The use of state-like simulations (DOGE, loyalty commissions) to bypass democratic process
  • The cultivation of epistemic collapse — where truth is replaced by leader-approved reality

The public naming of this as a project is a countermeasure. It breaks the spell. It reveals the shape of what they try to keep formless.

4. The Name Is a Weapon of Accountability

To call this The Tyranny Project does the following:

  • Unifies public understanding across legal, political, and cultural lines
  • Creates a framework for historical and legal inquiry
  • Prevents normalization of authoritarian logic
  • Identifies participants as deliberate actors in a system of anti-democratic construction

This is not just rhetoric. It is evidentiary structuring. It prepares prosecutors, historians, and citizens to track, map, and eventually dismantle the framework being assembled under cover of partisanship.

5. The Alternative Was Silence

We named it because no one else had. We named it because the courts, media, and political center too often fail to call authoritarianism by its name until it is too late.

We named it because truth needs vocabulary to survive in the age of simulation.

And we named it because the people deserve to know that what they’re feeling — this unraveling, this moral nausea, this sense that the system is being rewritten underneath them — is not a hallucination.

It is real.

And now it has a name.

Conclusion

The Tyranny Project is not a slogan. It is a forensic diagnosis of a real and ongoing attack on the constitutional order of the United States.

To name it is to resist it.
 To speak it is to remember who we are.
 And to dismantle it is to reclaim the republic.

We do not fear what it reveals.
 We fear only what would happen if we failed to speak it out loud.
