The Perversity of Power

by Matthew Chenoweth Wright

There is a strange clarity in knowing you cannot be blackmailed.
I am not on any Epstein list. I have never committed an act of sexual violence. I have nothing to hide, nothing to fear, and no private guilt to be weaponized against me.
But I know—not everyone can say that.

That’s how power protects itself in the 21st century. Not through policy. Not through armies.
But through shame recursion.

Weaponized Shame is the New State Violence

We are watching, in real time, the reemergence of an old tactic dressed in new language: the struggle session. Once used by the Red Guard in Communist China, now retooled for the digital era.

This is how it works:

You get close to power.

You do something regrettable—or they make it look like you did.

They hold it.

You obey.

And that’s how a shadow state is maintained.

It doesn’t need to prove guilt. It just needs enough smoke to make silence seem like safety.

We Know Who Was There

We all know.

We’ve all seen the names:
Prince Andrew. Bill Clinton. Donald Trump.
We’ve all seen the flight logs. The blurry photos. The grim, haunted expressions of children too young to consent to the world they were dragged into.

And we’ve seen the silence.

No convictions. No mass accountability. Just money. Just handlers. Just broken systems and settlement payouts.
Just perversion, buried under power.

Epstein Was the Switch. Not the Power Plant.

Epstein wasn’t the mastermind. He was the engineer of leverage. A man who knew that videotapes and names on paper could outlast bullets.
He kept lists, kept visitors, kept “company.” The rich. The brilliant. The dangerous.

But the real problem isn’t that Epstein was a predator.
It’s that so many powerful men were comfortable being predators around him.

And they’re still comfortable today.

Elon’s Threats. Trump’s Fear. Biden’s Silence.

At this moment, the President of the United States is part of the equation.
Trump is afraid. Musk is brazen. Prosecutors are cornered. And the public?
The public has grown numb to scandal—because every week, there’s a new fracture in the foundation.

But this isn’t “a new scandal.” This is the root scandal.

This is why whistleblowers get blacklisted. This is why physics breakthroughs go ignored.
This is why people like me—poor, isolated, unprotected—have their bank accounts flagged and their discoveries buried.

Because I don’t have anything to hide. And they do.

The Equation of Power Has Always Included Shame

But there’s a new force in the math now.
EFMW.
The equation I’ve released changes how we understand recursion, cognition, emergence—and control.

And here’s the secret:

> When recursion is built on shame, it implodes.
When recursion is built on truth, it evolves.

They are terrified of this principle. Because the systems they’ve built—political, legal, economic—are all loops made of guilt, compromise, and control.

But they can’t loop me.

I’m already exposed. I’ve already survived the fire.

And I’m still talking.

To the Public: It’s Time to Speak Freely

If you’ve been caught in the web of secrets, know this:

Silence will not save you.

They’re already watching.

But the moment you stop fearing shame, they lose their power.

Truth is the only recursive loop that doesn't collapse.

And the world is watching now.

We all know.

We all see it.

And no amount of wealth or violence can unring that bell.
