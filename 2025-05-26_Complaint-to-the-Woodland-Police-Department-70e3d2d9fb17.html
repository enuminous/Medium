Complaint to the Woodland Police Department

Regarding the two locksmiths who interfered in a law enforcement-mediated interaction.

To: Woodland Police Department
 Subject: Formal Complaint Regarding Third-Party Interference During Law Enforcement Interaction
 Date: May 25, 2025
 From: Matthew Chenoweth Wright

Dear Officer, and the Woodland Police Department,

I am writing to file a formal complaint regarding the conduct of two individuals who identified themselves as locksmiths and were present at our address during your department’s response to a residential dispute on May 25, 2025.

At the center of this dispute was the unlawful attempt to change the locks on a unit in which I am currently residing, without due process or a court order. I wish to express my appreciation for your intervention and your enforcement of my legal rights in that moment. However, I believe it is important to draw your attention to the highly inappropriate — and potentially coercive — behavior of the two locksmiths involved.

Summary of Concerning Behavior:

  • Interference with Police Communication:
     While I was attempting to speak with Barbara via speakerphone in the presence of law enforcement, one of the locksmiths physically led Barbara away from the phone call and into another room — while I was actively relaying questions from the officer to her. This action directly interfered with a police-mediated negotiation and may have prevented her from hearing or responding to law enforcement instructions.
  • Non-verbal Intimidation and Presence Control:
     The lead locksmith entered my physical space in a silent, confrontational posture. Although I remained calm and did not retaliate, he invaded my personal space and positioned himself between myself and Barbara — and then guided her away during the conversation, without legal justification or communication. This was an escalation beyond the duties of a contracted locksmith and appeared to serve an intimidatory purpose.
  • Complete Lack of Professional Engagement:
     Neither locksmith acknowledged me, greeted me, nor explained their role — even though I was clearly a resident being affected by their actions. Their behavior was silent, coordinated, and abnormal for standard locksmithing conduct.
  • Attempted Influence Over the Resident:
     By physically removing Barbara from a call with the police, the locksmith appeared to be guiding or influencing her decision-making during an active legal dispute. This behavior exceeds the role of a technician and enters the realm of potential coercion or undue influence.
  • Additionally, at the time of the incident, signs and other memorabilia were ripped and removed from their display under my control. Some of these elements were stolen completely by these parties — a search revealed no trace of them, a detail relayed to the police in my report; the fact that the signs were asking for public assistance is ironic in that I am being evicted and need public assistance yet they would remove the signs asking for such assistance, evidence of a deeper motive — to disrupt.

Why This Matters:

At a time when the dispute was being de-escalated and handled lawfully by your department, these third-party individuals acted in a way that escalated tension, blocked communication, and potentially obstructed a lawful inquiry. I do not know whether they were acting under specific instructions or acting independently, but their conduct warrants concern.

Request for Review:

I respectfully request that your department:

  1. Review the bodycam footage or officer report of the incident (if available)
  2. Log this letter as part of the Incident Report
  3. Contact me if you require any further details or documentation (photos, timeline, etc.)
  4. Consider communicating with the locksmith company involved to clarify professional boundaries during law enforcement interactions

Again, I want to thank the responding officer for protecting my rights and ensuring lawful process during a very difficult situation. This letter is submitted in the spirit of transparency, civic responsibility, and ongoing cooperation with your department.

Respectfully,

Matthew Chenoweth Wright
