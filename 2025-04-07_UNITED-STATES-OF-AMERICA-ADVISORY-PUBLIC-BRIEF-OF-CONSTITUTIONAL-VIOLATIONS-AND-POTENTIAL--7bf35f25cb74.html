UNITED STATES OF AMERICA
ADVISORY PUBLIC BRIEF OF CONSTITUTIONAL VIOLATIONS AND POTENTIAL INDICTMENTS
Re: United States v. Donald J. Trump and Pamela Bondi (Advisory Only)

Prepared by: Concerned Citizens and Constitutional Observers
Date: April 7, 2025
Jurisdictional Scope: Federal Constitutional Law, Criminal Code, Ethical Conduct of Government Officials

---

I. INTRODUCTION

This brief is submitted as a public legal advisory to identify and document apparent criminal, ethical, and constitutional violations arising from the unlawful deportation of Kilmar Abrego Garcia and the subsequent retaliatory actions by Attorney General Pam Bondi and former President Donald J. Trump against Department of Justice attorneys who complied with their constitutional duty to the court.

This document is not a legal filing but serves as a public call to action and a roadmap for prosecutors, lawmakers, and watchdogs.

---

II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

1. Kilmar Abrego Garcia, a legal resident of the United States with credible fear of persecution, was deported by DHS in March 2025 in violation of a 2019 immigration court order and federal law.

2. DOJ Attorney Erez Reuveni, in proceedings before U.S. District Judge Paula Xinis, acknowledged that the government had no legal basis for the deportation and that the removal was unlawful.

3. Following this admission, Pam Bondi placed Reuveni and his supervisor on administrative leave, citing “failure to vigorously defend the administration.”

4. Bondi’s action, reportedly authorized by Donald J. Trump, appears to be direct retaliation for lawful compliance with federal court procedures and ethical obligations.

5. As of 11:59 PM ET on April 7, 2025, the Trump Administration is expected to fail to comply with Judge Xinis’s lawful order to return Mr. Garcia, further compounding the constitutional violations.

---

III. IDENTIFIED VIOLATIONS

A. Obstruction of Justice (18 U.S.C. § 1503)

The President and Attorney General have engaged in conduct likely to be construed as corrupt interference with a federal judicial proceeding, including retaliating against attorneys who provided truthful statements to a judge.

B. Retaliation Against a Federal Employee (5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(8))

Federal law prohibits retaliation against employees for disclosing violations of law or gross mismanagement. DOJ attorneys are protected in fulfilling duties to the court and upholding lawful orders.

C. Violation of the Right to Due Process (U.S. CONST. Amend. V)

The unlawful deportation of Mr. Garcia, in defiance of an immigration court's protection order, constitutes a gross violation of substantive and procedural due process rights under the Fifth Amendment.

D. Contempt of Court (Inherent Judicial Authority)

Failure to return Mr. Garcia by the court-imposed deadline may result in civil or criminal contempt findings against DHS leadership and those who directly interfered with the judge’s order.

E. Abuse of Power / Malfeasance in Office

Using the Department of Justice as a tool for political enforcement rather than the impartial application of law amounts to executive overreach, a violation of the separation of powers doctrine.

---

IV. CONSTITUTIONAL IMPLICATIONS

This incident marks a potential constitutional crisis:

The Executive Branch is attempting to override the authority of the Judiciary, in clear violation of the checks and balances that preserve democratic governance.

DOJ independence is being undermined to force compliance with illegal immigration practices.

The pattern is consistent with autocratic behavior and may justify emergency Congressional review, special counsel appointment, or federal grand jury inquiry.

---

V. RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Immediate Congressional Oversight Hearings

House and Senate Judiciary Committees should issue subpoenas for documentation, including internal memos authorizing retaliatory action.

2. Special Counsel Appointment

A neutral, independent federal prosecutor should be appointed to investigate obstruction, abuse of power, and civil rights violations.

3. Ethics Review by OPR and OIG

The DOJ’s Office of Professional Responsibility (OPR) and the Inspector General must open formal inquiries into Pam Bondi’s conduct.

4. Public Reporting Mechanism

A secure whistleblower channel should be offered to any DOJ or DHS personnel with knowledge of this incident.

---

VI. CONCLUSION

The integrity of the Justice Department and the separation of powers are under active threat. The actions of Donald Trump and Pam Bondi have crossed beyond partisan maneuvering and into clear violations of law and the Constitution. The American people, Congress, and the courts must now determine how much longer this erosion of rule of law will be tolerated.

Submitted respectfully for public review and immediate response.
