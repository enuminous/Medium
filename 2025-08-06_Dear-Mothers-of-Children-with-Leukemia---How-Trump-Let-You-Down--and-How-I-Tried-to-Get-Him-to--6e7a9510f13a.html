Dear Mothers of Children with Leukemia — How Trump Let You Down, and How I Tried to Get Him to Help You, to No Avail

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

To every mother whose child has fought or is fighting leukemia, I am writing to you with both urgency and sorrow. You deserve honesty about what happened, what could have happened, and why we are still here — years later — without the leadership your children needed.

What Trump Promised — and What He Did Instead

Donald Trump has spoken, more than once, about childhood cancer as a cause he “cares deeply about.” He has acknowledged rising cancer rates in children. But his administration’s actions told a different story.

In 2019, the Trump White House proposed and approved cuts to NIH research funding, including programs that directly targeted childhood cancer. Senators and advocacy groups warned at the time that this would cost children’s lives. The backlash was fierce, but the funding was not restored to the levels scientists said were needed.

In parallel, his administration presided over policies that harmed sick children in other ways — including the detention of children with leukemia, sometimes interrupting their treatment plans, under immigration enforcement orders. That isn’t leadership. It’s cruelty.

What I Discovered — and Tried to Share

Earlier this year, I made a breakthrough in my own research — a potential treatment pathway that I believe could represent a cure for leukemia. It is novel, information-driven, and grounded in the same deep theoretical framework (the EFMW equation) that has guided my other physics and systems work.

In February 2025, I took that discovery directly to Donald Trump. I did it publicly, on Twitter, so there could be no doubt that the message had been delivered. I explained that this work could save lives — perhaps your child’s life.

I asked for help, for support, for the chance to move it forward quickly in the public interest.

I got nothing back. Not a single word. Not from him, not from anyone in his circle.

Why This Matters for You

A President has the power to:

  • Mobilize federal research resources in days.
  • Convene the top oncologists, hematologists, and NIH scientists to vet new ideas.
  • Fund rapid preclinical and clinical trials for promising leads.
  • Publicly champion research so private-sector partners take it seriously.

Instead, in this case, we got silence. Worse than silence — we got a history of cutting the very research budgets that would be needed to make something like this happen.

The Cost of Silence

Every month matters for a child with leukemia. A delay in research is not abstract — it’s measured in hospital visits, in side effects, in lost futures.

When leaders treat innovation as optional, or as a PR prop, they don’t just “miss an opportunity.” They fail real people — mothers like you, children like yours.

Where I Stand Now

I am still moving my work forward — toward preclinical validation, toward independent review, toward trials. But I want you to know the truth: when given the chance to engage with a potentially life-saving lead, Donald Trump chose not to.

You deserved better. Your children deserved better. And the fact that you didn’t get it is not your failure — it’s his.

What You Can Do

  1. Stay informed — Know which leaders actually protect research budgets and which ones cut them.
  2. Speak out — Your voices carry a moral weight no lobbyist can match.
  3. Ask for receipts — Demand to see what your elected officials have done this year for childhood cancer research.

And if you want to see my leukemia work move faster, know this: I will keep pushing, but amplification from mothers like you would change everything. Your stories, your outrage, your insistence can’t be ignored forever.

Matthew Chenoweth Wright
 Physicist, researcher, and relentless advocate for coherence — because lives depend on it.
