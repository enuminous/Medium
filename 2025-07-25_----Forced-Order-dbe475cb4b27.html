A. Erosion of Civil Liberties

Communities see disproportionate detainment of the unhoused, mentally ill, and minority populations. Everyday behaviors (e.g., resting in parks) become grounds for state removal.

B. Breakdown of Due Process

Without court oversight or consent, detainees may be confined indefinitely, without legal recourse or external review.

If adopted nationally, the executive order could create a shadow network of forced institutionalization, eroding constitutional protections and deepening systemic inequality. Rather than restoring order, it risks institutionalizing control over those most vulnerable.
