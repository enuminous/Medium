Donald Trump Is Worried About the Purity of His Precious Bodily Fluids

A Cinematic Descent into Liquid Obsession

The man is drenched with anxiety—a visceral dread that his own internal water isn’t as pristine as he imagines. He has long lived inside a narrative of purity, rituals of containment, and symbolic cleansing, yet his mind is increasingly awash in contradiction and guilt.

Trump’s rhetoric around immigrants “poisoning the blood of our country” echoes declarations of xenophobic purity, metaphors of bodily sanctity weaponized as political armor . Psychologists have noted that disgust and germ phobia are primal filters in his worldview—magnifying his repulsion at bodily fluids, contaminants, external intrusion .

Inside the Mind: Fluid Logic and Fear

Imagine a Kubrickian frame: cold fluorescent light, endless corridors. Here we glimpse Trump, pacing in the Oval Office of his own mind, haunted by an internal weather report:

Every misstep, every public crisis feels like a rupture in his vessel,

He imagines forbidden elements entering—betrayal, scandal, indictment—tarnishing his self-image,

He whispers about invisible impurities contaminating him.

Yet when asked if he has spoken to the families of Manhattan shooting victims, his response was a dry vacuum:
"I don’t want to tell you that." No empathy. No acknowledgment. Only retreat. The internal boundary is impenetrable to compassion, even as chaos spills everywhere.

Wetness by the Drop: The Fluid Anxiety Index

Region of Inner Monologue - State of Fluid Concern

Corpus Callosum - Flickers of denial pooling at margins
Amygdala — Surging with fear of corrosion, of impurity
Prefrontal Cortex - Slick with insecurities about legacy and loyalty
Inner Narrative Stream - A constant loop of purification fantasies

He imagines his blood, his sweat, even his saliva tainted—every fluid a battleground, every utterance a test of purity. It’s not literal fear of infection—it’s existential terror of contamination. The skidding edge of his cognition is drenched, trembling, unable to hold still.

Cinematic Tropes at Work

Monochrome close-ups of blood counts rising in tabloids, headlines about lawsuits and indictments reflecting red stains everywhere.

Slow, deliberate zooms into his face as he speaks of “blood purity,” his pupils dilating with fear.

Echoing voices in empty halls: those around him know what he refuses to accept—that he is intoxicated by his own disturbances.

Forecast: A Storm of Internal Discharge

Over the coming months:

Expect more liquid metaphors in his speech—references to “poison,” to “blood,” to “purity,” to “staining,” especially around immigration or loyalty.

As pressure mounts, his emotional water table will rise—he’ll appear flooded, explosive, unpredictable.

His sycophants will mirror his fluid anxiety, reinforcing a delusion of untainted rectitude despite the stains accumulating around them.

The Final Cut: Trump’s Precious Fluids Are Leaking

Kubrick once framed his characters at edges—amid immense architecture, dwarfed, disintegrating. Trump is that figure now, standing in his own fortress of pristine narrative, disconnected from reality. And yet the fortress leaks.

He’s terrified not of touch, but of being touched—of infiltration, of weakness, of loss of symbolic purity. And he doesn’t see the slow erosion happening all around him.

This essay is the overhead drone shot: the building intact, the man alone, rain tapping at the windows, damping walls he thought were impermeable.
