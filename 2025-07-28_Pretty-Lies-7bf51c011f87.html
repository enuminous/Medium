Pretty Lies

How Power Eats Beauty and Brilliance

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

“Funneling bright, pretty young women into the political machine is a job for those of rank character and even lower morals.”
— Field Notes from the Empire’s Back Rooms

The Invitation

She is young, eloquent, sharp as glass.
She’s been told she’s “going places,” though the places always seem to belong to someone else.
She enters the room with a résumé and leaves with a handler.

This is how it starts: not with assault, but with a compliment.
Not with a threat, but with a mentorship.
The kind that comes with after-hours drinks, private meetings, and “access to power.”

They call it grooming, but not in the sense people think.
This is ideological grooming, image grooming, structural grooming —
the shaping of a human being into an asset.

Beauty as Currency, Brilliance as Collateral

In a just world, beauty and brilliance would be wings.
But in this one, they are bait.
To be beautiful is to be visible.
To be brilliant is to be useful.
To be both is to become a target-rich environment for men who long ago learned how to convert humans into pawns.

The pipeline is familiar:

Campaign intern

Communications assistant

“Trusted aide”

Or worse: the whisperer behind the king, paid in proximity and threatened with exile

The system rewards the look of agency while stripping away actual autonomy.
To succeed, one must be visible, but never too vocal; sharp, but not threatening; loyal, but never inconveniently ethical.

The Machine Feeds on Aspiration

The machine doesn’t need these women to thrive.
It selects them because they offer cover.

A pretty face at the podium softens a brutal policy.
A brilliant advisor behind the scenes adds gravitas to a grotesque strategy.
They are used to signal virtue, inclusivity, modernity —
while being denied the very power they lend their names to.

Worse still, if they fall out of favor, the same system that elevated them with fanfare discards them in silence.

The Conspiratorial Quiet

Why don’t more speak up?
Because they signed NDAs.
Because they fear being labeled difficult, dramatic, or disloyal.
Because the machine punishes disobedience far more harshly than it punishes exploitation.

And because — heartbreakingly — some still believe they are winning.

Breaking the Cycle

This isn’t about one party or ideology.
It’s about power and its appetite — and how it trains people to become accessories to their own devaluation.

What’s required is:

Public recognition of the pipeline’s architecture

Direct support for whistleblowers and resistors

Institutional reform that values integrity over optics

And cultural clarity: we must teach young people the difference between mentorship and manipulation.

Beauty is Not Consent

To be beautiful is not an invitation.
To be brilliant is not a license for use.
And to stand near power should never mean becoming its meal.

Until we rewrite that story, the machine will keep grinding.

But some of us — those who have seen the machinery from the inside — are now sharpening pens instead of smiles.

And we are not afraid to write how power eats beauty and brilliance anymore.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 28, 2025.
