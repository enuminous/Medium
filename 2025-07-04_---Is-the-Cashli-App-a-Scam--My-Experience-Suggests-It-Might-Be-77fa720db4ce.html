🚨 Is the Cashli App a Scam? My Experience Suggests It Might Be

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

I want to share my experience with an app called Cashli, and issue a public warning to others who might be considering using it. Based on what I’ve seen, this app may be engaging in deceptive, non-compliant, and potentially fraudulent practices under the guise of being a financial services provider.

⚠️ The Setup

Cashli presents itself as a cash advance app that links to your bank account and offers advances up to $150. It claims to use bank-level security, Plaid for verification, and AES-grade encryption. The Terms of Use are dressed up in legalese, claiming they were “written by a Federal Trade Commission Compliance lawyer.” The app also charges a $3.88/month platform fee, which is supposedly used to cover the cost of account linking and analysis.

💣 What Actually Happened

Here's what I personally experienced:

Bank Linking Failed — Twice
I attempted to link my bank account through Plaid. Both times, the app failed to confirm the connection, yet still displayed aggressive prompts indicating a binding contract and fee responsibility.

Then Came the Email Request
Instead of completing verification through Plaid, the app demanded that I send an email to their support team with the name of my bank and the last four digits of my bank account number.

That’s not normal, not secure, and not compliant with standard banking practices. No legitimate financial app should ever ask you to email banking information.

No Way to Cancel, No Confirmation of Link
I sent the requested email. As of now, I have no confirmation that my bank account is linked, no indication of what comes next, and no ability to proceed in the app. I have no control, but they have:

My bank info through Plaid

My email with partial account info

My consent to charge a recurring fee

Unclear Billing Timeline
The app states the first month’s fee is non-refundable. There’s no clear method to stop the fee, especially when the app doesn’t confirm your account is even successfully linked. It’s a trap designed to charge without delivering anything in return.

🧾 What the Internet Is Saying

Reddit users report:

Being charged immediately upon linking their account

Being threatened by customer service when asking for a refund

Receiving rude and hostile support responses

No ability to cancel or confirm whether they were approved for anything

The Google Play Store lists the app with a few generic claims and no transparency. There are no trusted third-party reviews or evidence of regulatory oversight.

🚫 Why This Is a Problem

Let’s be clear:

Emailing partial banking information is a red flag.

Ambiguous billing practices with no refund process is a red flag.

Failure to confirm basic functionality (like successful bank linking) is a red flag.

Aggressive legal language and pressure tactics to accept fees without clarity is a red flag.

And when users complain, they are gaslit, ignored, or threatened with being blacklisted from other financial services. That’s predatory behavior.

🔍 What You Should Do

If you’ve interacted with Cashli:

Monitor your bank account for suspicious charges.

Immediately report them to:

FTC

CFPB

Google Play Support

If you haven’t used Cashli yet — don’t. There are safer and more transparent alternatives. Financial hardship should never be exploited.

🗣️ Final Word

We need stronger enforcement and visibility around apps like these. When fintech companies cut corners on security, accountability, and transparency, they endanger the very people they claim to help.

If this story helped you, or if you’ve had a similar experience, speak up. Scams rely on silence. I won’t be silent.
