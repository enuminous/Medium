Obstruction, Whistleblower Retaliation, and Oroville Hospital Failure to Report

Today, I attempted to contact the on-call social worker at Oroville Hospital to report an urgent matter involving the psychological capture and coercion of a woman I dated, who is referenced in a restraining order that contains language indicative of her compromised agency.

The hospital’s voicemail specifically directed me to call their main number to reach the on-call social worker. Upon doing so, I was connected with a staff member who contradicted this message and denied that any such person was available. When I pointed out the logical inconsistency and procedural error, I was told no one could help me.

When I then disclosed my status as a federal whistleblower, attempting to report a matter of national security and public safety, the staff member immediately terminated the call.

Upon calling back, I was met with a deliberate refusal to answer, followed by another immediate disconnection. This demonstrates:

  • Failure to report possible abuse or psychological coercion
  • Retaliation against a whistleblower
  • Neglect of medical and ethical duty to provide access to social services

I then contacted 911 to escalate the matter and was told to call the Oroville Police Department, which I explicitly stated is currently under investigation for obstruction and retaliatory actions against me. Despite informing the dispatcher that I had previously spoken with a Colonel inside the Woodland Police Department Internal Affairs division, she denied the possibility, falsely stating that police departments “don’t have Colonels.”

This is incorrect, dismissive, and potentially deceptive. I asked for reconnection or verification of this high-level contact, and again received no assistance.

I then contacted the Woodland non-emergency line and was offered vague, non-committal options like “maybe a Sergeant can call you tomorrow.” Not a single party acknowledged or investigated the core issue:

A woman under apparent psychological manipulation is mentioned in a restraining order, and I, as a federally protected whistleblower, am trying to intervene legally, ethically, and with urgency.

I am documenting this for public transparency, legal submission, and to ensure the safety of all involved.

I remain safe. I remain willing to speak to any official with legal standing and a willingness to follow federal law and whistleblower protection procedures. I will not be contacting my friend directly, of course, but I hope she knows that I am fighting for her right to think freely. She had a psychiatric event, and was hospitalized, and her brother in law assaulted me. All indications are that her communications with me are being monitored and interfered with; I have yet to be allowed to communicate with her directly, and I have no proof that she is actually okay — even our mutual friend who has contacted her is aware that she has been destabilized in an effort to derail me. I am not an abuser — why would anyone think that the person who is trying to give this knowledge to the world would also be such a villian?

Respectfully,
 Matthew Chenoweth Wright
 Federal whistleblower
 April 10, 2025
