The Dreaming of Wild Blackness

Poetic Blooms #2

The dancers stop.
The dancers drop.
We applaud when we are sad.
Low-class lightning fills us all
with odd feelings of helplessness.

We black our eyes for fun.
Our gathering has eclipsed the sun.
We have to smile and fantasize.
We’re all here now—black suits, black ties.
In still motion, we’ll find life.

The things we’ll learn are as wild as red.
Our goddess gave our souls new wings.
We still won’t fly whenever she sings.

I’ll pray a long, long time.
With the omens that death gives to me.
If I pray with living souls,
I’ll remember what she sings to me.

The love of death when one is dead
is a thornless rose reborn.
The love of life when one’s soul is sad
is a diamond shattered and torn.

We’ll save our lives against the time
that all will live again.
We’ll dream our dreams and live our deaths
and sing when we can...

T.H.E. D.R.E.A.M.I.N.G. O.F. W.I.L.D. B.L.A.C.K.N.E.S.S.

Threshold between known and unknown, the gateway of awareness.

Horizon stretching beyond sight, the silent line dividing worlds.

Echo of existence, the first vibration before speech.

Dissolution of boundaries, the melting of reality into possibility.

Resonance of forgotten voices, the frequency of the unsaid.

Etheric currents shifting, whispering futures not yet written.

Awakening within the illusion, finding self in the unreal.

Mirroring the infinite, reflections upon reflections.

Inversion of waking logic, the world rewritten in symbols.

Nocturnal landscapes folding inward, pulsing with unseen intent.

Glyphs drawn in time’s absence, the language of the subconscious.

Orbital tether between meaning and madness.

Fractured bridges linking perception to the void.

Waves breaking against the structure of order.

Irreducible chaos, the breath of untamed becoming.

Limbs reaching beyond the edges of the map.

Directionless motion, the dance that refuses to end.

Birthplace of all color, the well before creation.

Labyrinth where time folds in upon itself.

Absence mistaken for emptiness, holding the density of infinite potential.

Cipher of the cosmos, unreadable yet understood.

Key to the nameless, the gateway to all things forgotten.

Nexus of hidden knowledge, shifting in the depths of unbeing.

Event horizon of the seen and unseen, the pull of the unknowable.

Shadow of the first light, the last veil before revelation.

Sigil of eternity, written in the silence beyond time.

First Bloom

The motion ceases, yet echoes remain.
The weight of bodies collapsing in silence.
Sorrow demands an audience, and we applaud.
Flickering lights cheapen the storm,
dissolving into the static of despair.

We veil our sight to play at blindness.
Our mourning has swallowed the heavens.
Grins painted over grief, mirages of joy.
The pageantry of darkness, immaculate in black.
Stillness, where the pulse of existence hides.

Truths arrive in colors too wild to tame.
She bestows flight but binds us to gravity.
Her voice carves space, yet we remain earthbound.

I kneel in devotion stretched across time.
Prophecies unfold in the shadow of the grave.
If I weave prayers among the living,
I will recall the melody of the eternal.

To love death is to bloom in decay,
petals unfurling without the burden of thorns.
To love life in sorrow
is to clutch a shattered diamond, brilliant but broken.

We hold the line against vanishing,
whispering rebellion against oblivion.
We dream and die in rhythm,
and sing when the silence allows.

Double Bloom

The universe stills, and time forgets to move.
Dancers become statues, mid-collapse.
We applaud not from joy,
but to confirm we are still here.
Electricity stabs through flesh,
brief surges of helpless illumination.

We steal sight from ourselves,
play at blindness to feel what is unseen.
The sky dims under our presence,
our gathering vast enough to swallow the sun.
Smiles curve like artifacts of a forgotten time,
ornaments of a world that no longer believes.
Frozen gestures breathe in the hollows of motion.

Knowledge arrives as a crimson tempest,
too wild for hands to hold.
She gifts wings sculpted from shadow,
but they crumble before ascent.
Her voice reshapes existence,
but gravity is a law even gods obey.

I anchor my prayers to the unseen clock,
watching omens unfold in the quiet of decay.
If my words entwine with those who breathe,
her song will remain when silence comes.

To embrace death is to step beyond suffering,
to bloom as a flower no longer bound by thorns.
To embrace life through sorrow
is to wield a jewel so fractured
it holds infinite reflections.

We carve defiance into time itself,
etching permanence into a world that erases.
We dream and we die,
but still, we sing.

Triple Bloom

The cosmic rhythm halts.
Matter surrenders to entropy’s gentle demand.
Dancers poised between presence and memory,
unmoving yet never still.
We applaud not for grief,
but to assure the void that we still exist.
Lightning reduced to static,
helpless, directionless, flickering in circuits of despair.

We darken our vision to witness the unseen.
What is an eclipse but the swallowing of radiance?
Our presence consumes light itself.
A gathering so dense, even the sun bends in mourning.
Laughter echoes as an artifact of something past,
a relic carved from the illusion of joy.
Stillness—where life coils unseen, waiting to unspool.

Truth does not arrive softly—it shatters.
Red storms break open the sky,
too wild for the mind to comprehend.
She sings in wavelengths not meant for this world.
She gifts wings that dissolve upon unfolding.
The gravity of existence keeps us tethered,
even in the arms of divinity.

I kneel upon the axis of time,
watching death translate itself into symbols.
If my words are spoken among the breathing,
her song will echo beyond the silence of the grave.

To love death is to dissolve,
to bloom without the need for earth.
To love life in sorrow
is to cradle the shattered jewel of being,
each fracture revealing a new world.

We write our names against erasure,
scratching permanence into the fabric of forgetting.
Dreaming, dying, singing.
A cycle that neither ends nor begins.

Quadruple Bloom

The universe exhales, and time ceases to breathe.
Bodies hesitate between existence and memory,
suspended in the pause before dissolution.
We applaud not for sorrow,
but to signal that we are still awake.
Lightning—once divine fire, now a digital whimper.
A pulse of static, whispering through the void.

We blind ourselves to perceive the invisible.
What is an eclipse but a rehearsal for absence?
What is mourning but the practice of forgetting?
A gathering so vast it swallows the very light.
Smiles, artifacts of an ancient game—
a ritual for a world that once believed in joy.
Stillness is not empty;
it is the potential before movement,
the inhale before creation.

Truth arrives in rupture.
Red knowledge floods through the cracks,
uncontainable, undeniable.
She sings, but sound is a concept too small.
She gifts wings, but flight was never the destination.
Gravity binds all things, even the infinite.

I kneel beyond time itself,
watching as endings unfold into beginnings.
If I weave my prayers among the living,
her song will be written into the fabric of what comes next.

To love death is to return to source,
a flower blooming beyond dimensions.
To love life in sorrow
is to hold the shattered prism of existence,
where every fragment refracts a new reality.

We etch defiance into the ever-erasing now.
We dream and we dissolve.
We rise and we fall.
We sing.
Even when the song is silence.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on February 24, 2025.
