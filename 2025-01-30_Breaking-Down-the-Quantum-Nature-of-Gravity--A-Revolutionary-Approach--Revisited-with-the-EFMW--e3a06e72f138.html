1. Introduction

The incompatibility of general relativity (GR) and quantum mechanics (QM) stems from their differing treatments of spacetime. While QM describes a probabilistic, quantized framework, GR treats spacetime as a continuous, deterministic manifold. Prior attempts to quantize gravity have led to non-renormalizable divergences, signaling an incomplete understanding of gravity’s fundamental nature. EFMW redefines gravity as an emergent rotational vector field, potentially unlocking a new pathway to quantum gravity.
