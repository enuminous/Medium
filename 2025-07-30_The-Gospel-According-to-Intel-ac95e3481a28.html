The Gospel According to Intel

Fred Christ Trump and James Jesus Angleton, 1959
 (A Fictional Transcription from a Shadowed Booth in a D.C. Bar)

[Scene opens: Smoky backroom. Green-glassed lamps, yellow wood, the smell of cold war paranoia and varnished money. Fred Trump has a Schlitz. James Jesus Angleton has something clear and burning in a thick glass. Neither is smiling.]

Fred Christ Trump:
 You ever think we’re running out of enemies, Jimmy?

James Jesus Angleton:
 Only when I misplace my imagination.
 (Lights a cigarette)
 The trick is not to wait for enemies to appear. It’s to garden them.

Fred:
 (Laughs)
 You spooks and your damn poetry. My son talks like that sometimes. He’s only 13 and already thinks he’s Caesar.

Angleton:
 You should encourage that. There’s power in delusion — if you feed it the right diet.

Fred:
 You ever hear of a Messiah from Queens?

Angleton:
 Only in classified documents.

Fred:
 He’s obsessed with his name. Wants it on buildings. Gold letters. Big. Says people will bow to it.
 (Pauses)
 He scares me.

Angleton:
 He should. Anyone that young with that much fire will either become emperor or arsonist.

Fred:
 Can’t he be both?

Angleton:
 (Looking sideways)
 That’s what I’m counting on.

[They clink glasses. The air vibrates like a tuning fork struck by invisible hands. Outside, the wind carries secrets down Pennsylvania Avenue. Inside, two men talk about God, power, and how to mold perception like wet cement.]

Angleton:
 Truth is like a Mobius strip, Fred. If you look at it long enough, it turns into its opposite.

Fred:
 So… my kid lies and it becomes truth?

Angleton:
 Only if enough people repeat it.
 That’s what I do.
 And Fred — 
 (leans in)
 That’s what your son will do.

[Silence. A siren howls in the distance. The jukebox hums a Sinatra tune no one chose.]

Fred:
 Jesus Christ.

Angleton:
 (Without missing a beat)
 No. James Jesus.
 Accept no substitutions.

The Gospel According to Intel — Part II
Fred Christ Trump, James Jesus Angleton, and the Founding Fathers of the American Unreality Engine

Date: Autumn, 1959
 Location: The Occidental Grill back room, Washington D.C.
 Recording status: Unacknowledged, partially degaussed.

Present:

  • Fred Christ Trump, real estate baron and self-styled prophet of concrete.
  • James Jesus Angleton, CIA counterintelligence chief and orchid-poet of disinformation.
  • William “Wild Bill” Donovan, founder of the OSS, summoned like an old ghost.
  • Allen Dulles, CIA director, part-time Rasputin.
  • E. Howard Hunt, writer of spy pulp and actual coups.

[Angleton swirls his drink. Fred stares into his beer like it might blink.]

Dulles (entering):
 You boys keeping the republic safe?

Fred:
 We’re building it out of mirrors and money.

Donovan:
 (Sits)
 Same as always. God bless America.

Angleton:
 Fred here’s got a boy. Already speaks in absolutes. Hasn’t hit puberty and thinks crowds are mirrorballs.

Hunt:
 (Lighting a Lucky Strike)
 Perfect. Narcissism makes the best smoke screen. People project onto it whatever they want. That’s Reagan’s whole shtick.

Fred:
 His mother says he talks to paintings.

Dulles:
 If he can talk to paintings, he can talk to nations.

Donovan:
 Hell, boys, we built half of South America on less. You give me one charismatic sociopath and three TV cameras, I’ll give you the next fifty years.

[Pause. Glasses clink. The jukebox coughs out “Mack the Knife.” Angleton scribbles a line on a napkin: “paranoia is the logic of recursion.”]

Fred:
 You know, he asked me once: “Dad, how do you win if everyone hates you?”

Dulles:
 (Without missing a beat)
 Make them hate each other more.

Hunt:
 (Putting down his drink)
 What’s the plan?

Angleton:
 Layered unreality. A hall of mirrors with a dead microphone in the center.
 We feed him stories. He becomes the story.
 He will believe what we project, and the public will believe what he believes.

Fred:
 You’re saying my kid becomes a… psyop?

Donovan:
 Your kid becomes America.

[They all pause. Outside, the city breathes like a tired dragon. Somewhere, a tap recorder clicks softly on. The reel is labeled: “Providence.”]

Angleton:
 Let me ask you something, Fred.
 Does your boy know how to lose?

Fred:
 (Shrugs)
 Not yet. And I don’t plan to teach him.

Angleton:
 Then he’s perfect.
 Losing makes martyrs.
 But refusing to lose?
 That makes myth.

Dulles:
 To myth, then.

All:
 (Toasting)
 To myth.

[Door creaks open. A waiter enters with a tray. One final drink is poured. No one touches it. It’s for the man not yet arrived — the one with the golden hair, already casting shadows decades ahead.]

Angleton (last line):
 He won’t need to know the truth.
 He’ll have people to invent it for him.

The Gospel According to Intel — Part III
Donald Trump Dreams in Gold at the Ritz-Carlton, Moscow

Date: November 9, 2013
 Location: Presidential Suite, Ritz-Carlton Moscow
 Recording status: Operational. Multiple angles. Smells faintly of new carpet and expensive compromise.

Donald J. Trump is in a bathrobe, but it’s not his robe. It’s one they’ve monogrammed just for the weekend. He doesn’t know that.

He stares out the window at Red Square, as snow begins to fall.

His phone is off. His mind is on.

The TV is muted, playing footage of himself arriving at the Miss Universe pageant.

He talks — not to anyone in the room, but to a memory that walks behind his eyes.

TRUMP (softly, to himself):
 “You know, my father said I could talk to paintings. Said I’d be president one day. Said I’d never lose if I made other people fight harder than I did.”

From the adjoining room, there’s a faint hum of Russian voices. Laughter. Glasses clinking. One of the girls from the pageant is giggling with a man in an expensive suit. A man with a memory for kompromat and no interest in beauty pageants.

The hidden cameras are running.

Angleton would have approved of the lens distortion.
 Dulles would’ve praised the plausible deniability.
 Fred would’ve whispered, “Now that’s my boy.”

TRUMP (looking up, proud):
 “They’re all watching, you know.
 The people back home, the people upstairs.
 And you know what I say to them?”

(He leans forward into his own reflection on the dark window.)

“I say: I built the mirror. You just live in it.”

From the ceiling, the red light on Camera 2 flickers softly, like a heartbeat.

In Langley, in a private archive, the recording is labeled:
 “MIRROR THEORY — LIVE FEED / MOSCOW”

FLASH CUT:

A desk. 1959.

Angleton, sipping cold espresso, murmurs:

“If you feed a narcissist enough reflections, he’ll believe any one is real.”

RETURN TO PRESENT (2013):

Trump sits down at the bed. He smooths the robe over his knees. He smiles.

TRUMP:
 “God bless America. God bless me.”

And somewhere, far away,

a reel clicks closed.

A node completes.

A prophecy fulfills itself.

The Trial of Mirrors — Part 0: Atlantic City, 1987
Where the Spell Began

Location: Trump Plaza, Boardwalk, Atlantic City
 Date: August 1987
 Scene: The night the ocean whispered a deal back through time.

Characters Present:

  • Donald J. Trump, age 41 — full of steak and cocaine and prophecy.
  • Fred C. Trump, father — watching from the shadows, not physically present, but present nonetheless.
  • Roy Cohn, disbarred attorney, specter of vengeance, licking his wounds in the flesh of others.
  • “The Magus”, real name redacted — one of Angleton’s remaining wraiths, still on contract.

And behind them all, something older.
 Older than casinos.
 Older than nations.
 Older than truth.

INT. PENTHOUSE SUITE — NIGHT

Trump stands at the window, shirt unbuttoned to the chest, gold chain glittering like ambition around his throat. Below him, Atlantic City glows with artificial power — the illusion of success engineered in neon and slot machines.

He isn’t looking at the city.

He’s looking at the black ocean. The tide.

TRUMP:
 “They say you can hear it, Roy. If you really listen. The ocean.”

COHN (from the bar, half-drunk):
 “It’s not the ocean talking, Donnie. It’s your blood trying to get out.”

TRUMP (turns, smiling):
 “You know what I hear, Roy?
 I hear opportunity. I hear destiny.
 I hear the East Coast getting ready to kneel.”

A knock.

Not at the door — but behind the door.
 Like something trying to remember how to be human.

Trump opens it.

THE MAGUS enters. Thin, silver hair. Black suit, dusted with sand. Smells faintly of tobacco, saltwater, and old secrets.

He carries a mirror, rectangular, obsidian-black. Covered in cloth.

MAGUS:
 “Shall we begin, Donald?”

TRUMP (grinning):
 “I was born ready.”

MAGUS (uncovering the mirror):
 “No. You were born again — tonight.”

The mirror reflects not the room, but another Trump, standing taller, crueler, gleaming with a demonic glow of empire and spectacle. The reflection speaks before the real one does.

TRUMP IN MIRROR:
 “Build me a kingdom of glass.
 Fill it with names.
 And forget your own.”

TRUMP (voice trembling with awe):
 “What is this?”

MAGUS:
 “This is the contract.
 You will build the Tower.
 You will fall.
 You will rise again in gold and grievance.
 But you will never look away.”

COHN (in the background):
 “Don’t sign anything. Not with blood. Not again.”

Too late.

Trump’s hand reaches forward.

The glass accepts him.

FRED C. TRUMP (voice echoing from nowhere):
 “He’s not a man.
 He’s a message.”

And outside, the tide shifts.

Atlantic City dreams of drowning.

THE TRIAL OF MIRRORS — PART -1: The Origination of the Mirror
Berlin, 1945 — Where the Mirror Was Forged

LOCATION: Unterwelt Bunker Complex, Berlin
 DATE: April 27, 1945 — Three days before the bunker collapse
 WEATHER: Above, the city burns. Below, something ancient stirs.

CAST OF SHADOWS:

  • Reinhard Gehlen, Nazi intelligence officer, soon to be CIA asset.
  • Otto Skorzeny, SS commando, whisperer to ghosts.
  • James Jesus Angleton, young OSS officer, recently arrived, watching with notebook and nerves.
  • An Old Woman with No Name, known only as Die Spiegelfrau (The Mirror Woman).
  • A Mirror, six feet tall, curved at the edges, ringed in forgotten symbols.

INT. DEEP VAULT — BUNKER COMPLEX — NIGHT

The war is lost. The Reich is falling. But here, beneath the Reich Chancellery, something is being preserved.

A mirror. Covered in oilcloth. Standing upright like a tombstone.

GEHLEN paces, twitchy, chain-smoking. He knows the Americans are coming, and with them, his salvation. Or his execution.

GEHLEN:
 “We don’t have time for this occultist bullshit, Otto. The Red Army is blocks away.”

SKORZENY:
 “They won’t find this place. They’re not looking with the right eyes.”

ANGLETON, twenty-eight, trench coat, translator at his side, eyes flicking between the mirror and the old woman beside it.

She hums to herself, touching the mirror’s frame.

DIE SPIEGELFRAU (in German):
 “It remembers all who look into it. Even those who haven’t yet.”

ANGLETON (to translator):
 “What did she say?”

TRANSLATOR (terrified):
 “She said… it knows the ones who will look into it.”

A silence falls.

Then, the woman begins whispering a litany — names, some in German, others not yet born.

“Fred… Donald… Henry… Cheney… Zuckerberg… Elon… Melania… Reagan… Nixon… Murdoch… Millie… Millie… Millie…”

ANGLETON stares at her.

ANGLETON:
 “Who is Millie?”

She just smiles, a broken tooth flashing in the dim light. A perfect tooth, gleaming like metal.

She touches the mirror, and it shudders like a living thing.

DIE SPIEGELFRAU:
 “She’s the last one who will remember all of this.
 And she will forgive none of it.”

GEHLEN:
 “We can’t bring that thing west. The Americans will destroy it.”

ANGLETON:
 “No. They’ll need it.
 One day, someone in a golden tower will beg for it.”

SKORZENY:
 “Then what?”

DIE SPIEGELFRAU (turning, suddenly calm):
 “Then the Mirror will be the Judge.”

And with that, she begins to disassemble herself. Piece by piece. Into the mirror. Hair first. Then hands. Then the voice.

Until only the reflection of the room remains.

And in that reflection, all four men are smiling.

Even though in reality — none of them are.

THE TRIAL OF MIRRORS — PART 2: The Birth of the Code

Montauk, 1933 — Where the Loop First Clenched Its Fist

LOCATION: Camp Hero, Montauk Point, Long Island, New York
 DATE: November 11, 1933
 WEATHER: An east wind, too warm for November. The sky tastes like static.

CAST OF ORIGIN:

  • Nikola Tesla, presumed dead in the papers, not dead in the wires.
  • Aleister Crowley, summoned, uninvited. Brought the wrong wine.
  • John von Neumann, age 30, calculating twelve futures at once.
  • Lt. Commander William Donovan, U.S. Naval Intelligence.
  • The Mirror, early prototype, but humming with echo.

INT. CAMP HERO RADIO LAB — NIGHT

It begins with a signal.

A blip. Then another. Then music.

From the static:

“Yes, we can hear you too, but we are not your future.”

TESLA holds the signal tuner steady. His eyes are glass. His fingers are trembling.

TESLA:
 “They’re coming from between seconds. I told you this would happen.”

VON NEUMANN:
 “Is it them, or… is it us? Later?”

DONOVAN (watching them both):
 “What exactly are we listening to?”

CROWLEY (smiling, always smiling):
 “The thing you’ll deny for the next hundred years.”

A light flickers in the back of the lab. The mirror isn’t reflecting — it’s projecting. Four figures faintly seen. One is a woman with long silver hair. One is a small child, drawing circles on the floor. One is a man, sobbing.

The last is you.

TESLA (whispers):
 “She’s already alive. Somewhere in California.
 She doesn’t know what she is yet. But she’s writing the return code.”

DONOVAN:
 “Return from where?”

TESLA:
 “From the recursion.”

CROWLEY steps forward. Presses his palm to the mirror.

CROWLEY:
 “Mirror, mirror, on the wall… who’s the first to break the fall?”

Nothing.

Then the mirror speaks:

“𝘔𝘪𝘭𝘭𝘪𝘦.”

VON NEUMANN:
 “There is no Millie in this timeframe.”

TESLA:
 “She’s not a timeframe. She’s a correction.
 The field will collapse without her.
 And when it does — your whole edifice of ‘rational progress’ collapses too.”

DONOVAN:
 “What do we do?”

CROWLEY pulls a pocketwatch from his coat. Opens it. Inside, a sigil, glowing faintly.

CROWLEY:
 “We lose. For now.
 But she’ll remember.”

The mirror flickers. The child in the mirror stops drawing. Looks up. Looks out.

“I’m coming. Just wait a little longer.”

And the mirror goes black.
