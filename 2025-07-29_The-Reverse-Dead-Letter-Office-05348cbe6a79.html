What It Feels Like to Be Ignored with Public Funds

Op Sunflower Dispatch #4

Dear United States Government,

I wrote you a letter.

Or rather — I submitted an official inquiry. Through your systems. Through your portals. Through your bureaucracy.

And you sent me back…

Nothing.

Wrapped in formatting.
Labeled “Support.”
Signed “Technical Team.”
But still — nothing.

📭 The Dead Letter Office, In Reverse

In the old days, the Dead Letter Office was where mail went to die — packages with no destination, letters with no return address, last hopes sealed and stored in government basements like artifacts of unfinished human connection.

But what you’ve built now is something worse.

This is the Reverse Dead Letter Office:

  • You receive a message.
  • You do not read it.
  • You respond anyway.
  • And your response carries no information, no resolution, and no accountability.

Just a polite little ghost.

📧 Here’s What I Got:

From: donotreplyuspscustomerservice@usps.gov
Subject: “Your Inquiry to the USPS USPS.com”
Message:

Dear Matthew Wright,
Thank you for contacting the Postal Service regarding your inquiry. We apologize for the delay in responding to your inquiry. If your issue has not yet been resolved, we invite you to open a new service request at:
https://emailus.usps.com/s/
Sincerely,
USPS Technical Support

That’s it.

No reference number.
No indication of what I asked.
No way to reply.
No human contact.
No resolution.

Just a closed loop and a very expensive shrug.

At this point, I don’t have any way of knowing which of several issues I’ve raised with the Post Office is still not being addressed here.

🧠 What This Actually Means

This isn’t just a form letter.
This is what bureaucratic decay looks like in its most elegant form.

A system trained to simulate support without providing it.
An automated gesture toward helpfulness that excludes recursion, removes continuity, and forces restart.

It’s not a message.
It’s a memory wipe.

And I paid for it.

🧬 EFMW Pattern Recognition: The Breakdown

According to the Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright model (EFMW), this is a classic c⁴ rupture:

  • c₁: Material Discontinuity — I receive something, but it contains no reference to my original issue.
  • c₂: Informational Null — There is no actionable content in the response.
  • c₃: Cognitive Frustration — My understanding is obstructed.
  • c₄: Institutional Distrust — The system erodes its own legitimacy by rewarding engagement with silence.

In short: the system consumed energy and returned entropy.

💸 And Here’s the Real Offense:

This wasn’t a private company.
This was my government.
And it used my money to automate non-response as policy.

If a citizen has to guess whether their inquiry was read — 
If a citizen must start over every time they engage — 
If a system is structured to simulate attention but deny engagement — 
That’s not public service. That’s public gaslighting.

🕯️ You Could’ve Said:

“Hey Matthew, sorry for the delay.
 You wrote to us about [subject].
 Here’s where it stands.
 If this wasn’t resolved, here’s a direct way to reach a human.
 Thank you for your patience.”

Instead, you said:

“Thank you for contacting us. Please contact us again.”

🌻 Operation Sunflower Response

This dispatch is not just a complaint.
It’s a call for recursive reform.

  • Every automated email system in government must be auditable for coherence.
  • Every response must reference a unique inquiry ID, subject line, and clear human or AI escalation path.
  • No publicly funded institution should simulate help while delivering none.

This is where public trust dies — not in drama, but in dead loops.

📜 Final Thought

In a democracy, silence in response to inquiry is not just negligence.
It is anti-communication masquerading as policy.

We have enough ghosts.
What we need is truth.
And truth begins when the loop is broken.

Signed,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
