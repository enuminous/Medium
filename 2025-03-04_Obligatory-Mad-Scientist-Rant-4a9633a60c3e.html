Oh, you poor, deluded fools.

You thought you could control the truth, that you could wrap it up in bureaucratic red tape, shove it into classified vaults, drown it in disinformation, and snuff it out like a candle in the dark. But the truth is not a candle. It is not delicate. It is not fragile. It is a wildfire, a sun, a thing that does not die—it only waits for the right conditions to ignite. And oh, my dear, wretched gatekeepers, you have handed me the match.

You built a world on a foundation of lies. Carefully curated, neatly categorized, and ruthlessly enforced. You told people what to believe, what to ignore, and what to forget. You thought that if you controlled the information, you controlled reality. And for a time, you did. Oh, what a glorious, terrible time it was. You rewrote history at will, buried scandals deeper than any grave, and sat upon your thrones of deceit, convinced that you were untouchable.

But you made a mistake. You assumed the system was flawless. You assumed that people would never look too closely, never ask the right questions, never develop the means to reconstruct what you worked so hard to erase. And that, my dear, foolish architects of suppression, is where you failed. Because I asked the right questions. I built the system. I wrote the equation. And now, I have done what you feared most.

I have torn the curtain from your little stage.

Did you think I would keep it to myself? Did you think I would sit in a basement somewhere, whispering secrets to the void, content in knowing the truth while the world remained blind? No. Oh no. That is not how this story goes. The equation is out. The analysis is running. The people—your people—are waking up. And every day, every hour, every second, more of your carefully woven tapestry unravels. They are seeing what you so desperately tried to hide. They are questioning everything. And the best part? The very best part?

There is nothing you can do to stop it.

You could try to discredit me, but you’d have to acknowledge me first. You could try to silence me, but I am already everywhere. You could try to destroy my work, but it has already spread beyond your reach. You are out of moves. You are out of time. And now, now you must watch as the dam bursts, the flood sweeps away your delicate house of lies, and the world steps, blinking, into the sunlit horror of what you have done.

Do not beg me to stop. Do not plead for mercy. You never granted it to the ones you deceived, and you shall find none here. The reckoning is here. The forensic reconstruction of reality is underway. And all that remains for you to do is stand there, helpless, as the world finally—finally—sees you for what you are.

For too long, you have mistaken silence for ignorance. You have mistaken compliance for consent. You have mistaken history for truth. But history was written by those who seek to control it—and truth has no master but itself.

And now, my dear, fragile suppressors of reality... the era of control is over.

I am the wildfire. I am the reckoning. And there is no equation in the world that can suppress the inevitable.

Oh, how I do so love watching the mighty fall.
