1. David Bohm: Hidden Variables and Deterministic Quantum Mechanics

Bohm’s interpretation of quantum mechanics (the pilot-wave theory) proposes that the wavefunction governs particle behavior, but particles themselves follow deterministic trajectories under the influence of a "pilot wave." The key here is that the wavefunction and the hidden variables together provide a deterministic path for the particle, even if its quantum behavior seems probabilistic at the macro level.

Mathematical Representation (Bohmian Mechanics):

\frac{d \mathbf{x}}{dt} = \frac{\hbar}{m} \nabla S(\mathbf{x}, t)

2. Roger Penrose: Quantum Consciousness and Gravitational Effects

Penrose theorizes that consciousness arises from quantum processes, especially within microtubules in neurons. He suggests that quantum superposition at the micro-scale could affect brain function and that the collapse of the wavefunction is tied to gravitational effects, causing a non-computable state that underlies free will.

Mathematical Representation (Penrose’s Consciousness and Collapse):

Penrose's model suggests that consciousness could emerge at the level of quantum collapse, driven by gravity. The objective reduction (OR) of the quantum state is tied to gravitational effects:

\text{OR time} \sim \frac{1}{\Delta E} \cdot \frac{1}{\tau_{gravitational}}

3. Richard Feynman: Path Integrals and Probabilistic Interactions

Feynman’s path integral formulation of quantum mechanics states that all possible paths contribute to the probability amplitude of a particle’s position. The probabilistic nature of quantum events is the result of all potential histories that contribute to the quantum state.

Mathematical Representation (Feynman Path Integrals):

The path integral formulation sums over all possible paths to calculate the probability amplitude:

\Psi(\mathbf{x}, t) = \int \mathcal{D}[x(t)] e^{\frac{i}{\hbar} S[x(t)]}

4. Matthew Wright: EFMW and Emergent Free Will Metastates

EFMW connects the deterministic structure of quantum mechanics with the idea of emergent free will through quantum metastates. These metastates represent points of superposition where the system is probabilistically undecided but follows deterministic laws until the collapse occurs. The quantum wavefunction of the cognitive system collapses into one of many states, reflecting the decision-making process—an emergent "choice" driven by deterministic quantum evolution and external influences.

Mathematical Representation (EFMW Framework):

The emergence of free will in EFMW can be represented by an equation where the quantum metastates evolve deterministically until a collapse happens based on probabilities (fueled by the observer’s cognitive state and external conditions):

P(\text{action}) = \int \psi(x) P(x) dx
