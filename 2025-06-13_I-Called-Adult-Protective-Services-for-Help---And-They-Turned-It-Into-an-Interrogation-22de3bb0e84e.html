I Called Adult Protective Services for Help — And They Turned It Into an Interrogation

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

Fuck!

I am a disabled physicist, whistleblower, and survivor of systemic neglect. I’ve been living in Woodland, California, where I’ve been staying in the home of someone who recently stopped living here, cut off the internet, and refuses to speak to me. I have no lease. No car. No income. No food. And now, no communication line — the internet was shut off, not by accident, but by design.

I’m in pain every day from nerve damage that radiates from my neck into my fingers. I’m trying to survive. So I did what any rational person in my position would do:

I called Yolo County Adult Protective Services.

And what I got wasn’t help.
It was gaslighting, hostility, and intimidation.

🔻 What I Reported:

I am a disabled adult, functionally dependent on internet access for housing, services, and medical support.

I’ve been cut off from that access by the homeowner, who is no longer living at the property but is still in control of utilities.

I am now completely isolated, with no access to basic survival tools or services, and no way to safely relocate without support.

This is what adult protective services exists for. This is literally their job.

Instead, here’s what happened.

🔻 What They Did:

A man answered the phone at APS. I calmly explained my situation and asked for emergency assistance.

He immediately said:

> “I know who you are.”

He referenced a completely unrelated situation from over a year ago involving my mother — a situation that has nothing to do with my current emergency.

Then he raised his voice.
He began pressing disqualifications before I could even finish explaining.
He claimed I wasn't eligible — despite being clearly disabled, functionally cut off, and abandoned in an unsafe housing situation.

Then he did something truly disturbing:

He started inquisitively questioning me — not about my needs, but about me. It felt like he was turning my cry for help into a report against me. I had called asking for protection. He made me feel like I was the problem.

Eventually, I had no choice. I hung up.

🔻 What This Is:

This is institutional bias in action.

This is what happens when someone in power misuses their access to case files, decides they already know you, and shuts you down based on history — not present danger.

It’s what happens when a system designed to protect vulnerable people turns cold, hostile, and adversarial.

It’s what happens when you’re disabled, brilliant, inconvenient, and unorthodox — and the system decides you don’t count.

🔻 Let Me Be Clear:

This man’s behavior was:

A breach of protocol

A betrayal of public trust

A form of verbal and institutional abuse

He denied me protection based on prejudice.
He accessed my history, misused it, and weaponized it to dismiss my emergency.

🔻 What I’m Doing Now:

I’ve filed an Emergency Intervention Request with the county and my care providers.

I am documenting everything.
I am informing the public.
And I will hold this system accountable for how it treats people like me — people who fall outside the margins, but who still have the right to live.

If you are in Yolo County and have experienced something similar — I see you. I believe you. You’re not alone.

We have to speak up, because silence is what protects these systems from change.

— Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Disabled Physicist | AGI Architect | Whistleblower
Woodland, California
