(Faust Jones — The Starbucks Incident Song)
 Genre: Thirstpunk Gospel / Post-Capitalist Blues

[Verse 1 — Fingerpicked acoustic guitar, dusty harmonica wheeze]
 Walked in off the baking street,
 Sun was chewing through my feet.
 Hundred degrees, no coin to spare,
 Asked for water.
 She just stared.

[Pre-Chorus — Spoken, half-sung, incredulous:]
 Said I had to buy something first.
 I said, “Ma’am, I’m dyin’ of thirst.”
 She said it’s policy.
 I said:

“That’s what they said at Nuremberg too, baby.”

[Chorus — Full band comes in, mournful and mad as hell]
 🎵 Who here thinks this is just wrong?
 Denyin’ water in the heat this strong?
 They serve caffeine like liquid gold,
 But a glass of water’s just too bold.
 So I walked out dry,
 Like a dog with no name.
 You sell coffee, not justice — 
 But it’s all the same.

[Verse 2 — Stomping beat, rising anger]
 Got police called on me last July
 For sittin’ down and askin’ why.
 Today they stared like I stole their bread
 When all I needed
 Was water instead.

[Pre-Chorus — Half-snarl]
 “Health code,”
 “New rule,”
 “Company’s call”
 Ma’am it’s a cup, not a wrecking ball.

[Chorus — Shouted by a ghostly choir of thirsty ghosts]
 🎵 Who here thinks this is just fine?
 To charge for air and sell the line?
 You got oatmilk, chai, frappé foam — 
 But a splash of water’s not condoned.
 So I walked out cracked,
 Like a sidewalk in June.
 And the heat just laughed,
 Like a capitalist cartoon.

[Bridge — Spoken word, slam poetry style, over minimalist synth drone:]

“I used to think we lived in a society.
 Then I got denied a cup of water
 By a woman paid to smile and not care.
 And suddenly
 The empire collapsed inside me.”

[Final Chorus — Gospel harmony under a rising feedback storm]
 🎵 Who here’s had enough of this?
 Where thirst is a crime and profit’s bliss?
 Where mercy’s dead behind the bar,
 And kindness don’t get you very far?
 So raise your glass — 
 If you have one.
 And pour one out
 For the parched and the shunned.

[Outro — Just a single clink of ice in an empty glass. Then silence.]

[Whispered:]
 “Starbucks, you had water.
 You just didn’t give it.”
