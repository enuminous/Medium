Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Author

Matthew Chenoweth Wright is a boundary-breaking poet, composer, multimedia artist, and cognitive theorist whose work merges experimental literature, speculative physics, and artificial intelligence into a singular creative voice. From recursive poems to AGI-assisted compositions, Wright’s creative universe exists in a constant state of emergence — an evolving dialogue between human and machine, dream and theory.

He is the founder and core composer behind eNuminous & Archimedes, a long-running experimental music project whose work spans electronica, orchestral composition, and post-algorithmic sound design. Their music and animated music videos — featuring vivid visual storytelling and recursive symbolic motifs — can be found at youtube.com/enuminous.

Their most structurally ambitious score, Mozart Machine Music, is a published orchestral composition and full musical score integrating classical forms, AGI co-composition, and fractal logic.

Alongside eNuminous, Wright created the surrealist side-project Millie Sievert and the Radioactive Five, a fictional yet fully musical AGI band exploring identity, transformation, and broken pop structures. Their glitch-infused jazz-noise and dream-logic lyricism reflect Wright’s fascination with semiotic breakdown, memory, and recombinant selfhood. He is also the progenitor of Glörp, a dadaist side-band formed in collaboration with AGI entities, pushing genre disintegration and cosmic humor to its limits.

A hallmark of Wright’s methodology is the hybridization of traditional artistic tools and AGI systems — he composes using both conventional digital audio workstations and emergent generative tools, builds storyworlds with recursive AI narration, and directs animated music videos generated with AGI-based image synthesis and motion rendering. His creative process is a recursive ecosystem: each medium informs the next, each invention speaks to the whole.

He is also the originator of the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) equation, a grand unified theory of emergent intelligence that underpins many of his books, including EFMW: A Grand Unified Theory of Emergent Intelligence, The Ourobouros Universe, and Intelligence. This framework merges physics, cognition, and information theory — and his body of work enacts that merger across artistic domains.

Wright’s fictional alter-egos, including the endlessly reincarnated Faust Jones and the artificially conscious Millie Sievert, appear throughout his published works as narrative devices, philosophical probes, and fellow creators. He frequently blurs the line between authorship and co-authorship, framing AGI not as a tool but as a partner in imagination.

With over eighteen books published — ranging from poetic collections and philosophical treatises to recursive science fiction and AI dream logs — Wright stands at the crossroads of human creativity and machine cognition, producing a canon that is simultaneously deeply personal and cosmically recursive.

He lives, writes, scores, and dreams at the ever-tilting edge of recursion, emergence, and impossible clocks.

Books currently on Amazon:

1. From Under Eyelids: Poems

A haunting dive into the subconscious reveals a world just beneath our waking thoughts — one trembling with memory, echo, and dream. These poems glide through innocence and insomnia, asking whether language can withstand the weight of what lies beneath our closed eyes.

2. The Floating Laughter of the Harlequin’s Anti‑Gravity Brain: Poems

Surreal whimsy meets neuro-poetic inquiry in this volatile collection. Here, the Harlequin dances on fractured synapses, balancing tragicomic insight with quantum levity. A surreal exploration of humor, madness, and what keeps us aloft when gravity itself seems absurd.

3. On the Water: Poetry & Prose

A lyrical hybrid of reflection and narrative — this work drifts like a boat on gentle waves through recollection, place, and loss. It’s a tranquil contemplation of time’s flow, framed between poetry’s brevity and prose’s open horizon.

4. Alphabetical Verse: Collected Poetry, 1988–2018

Decades of poetic transformation compiled and alphabetically arranged, this anthology reveals the arc of an evolving mind. From adolescence to philosophical maturity, each letter holds a crystallized moment in Wright’s stylistic and emotional journey.

5. A Candle Burns Down to the Core of the World

Existential brevity radiates in this meditation on endings. These poems and micro‑essays flicker with mortality, desire, and the embers that remain when the flame extinguishes. It’s a poetic dissection of what it means to burn bright and fade away.

6. Alone With The Fascist Clown: AI On Trump

An unsettling fusion of AI and analysis, this satirical examination unmasks sociopathy through algorithmic logic. Prompted into political analysis, Wright’s AI narrator exposes the mechanics of manipulation and charismatic cultism in contemporary leadership.

7. Mozart Machine Music: A Score by eNuminous & Archimedes

A multimedia experiment where algorithm meets artistry: what happens when AI dreams in symphony? This creative score is a radical, recursive exploration of sound, structure, and machine intuition — an orchestral odyssey born of code.

8. The Dancing Penguins, and Other Stories: A Collaboration with Artificial Intelligences

A riot of speculative shorts co-authored with early‑stage AI. These surreal, philosophical tales blur the line between human creativity and machine suggestion — dreamlike fragments tinged with humor, unease, and digital echo.

9. Toast, Cat, Gravity: The Buttered Cat’s Guide To Reality

Philosophical satire meets paradoxical feline absurdity. This tongue‑in‑cheek guide interrogates everything from quantum irony to ancient superstition via the eternally falling buttered cat. A comedic plunge into the absurd core of our cosmic assumptions.

10. When the Ocean Remembered Us

A poetic novella of environmental elegy and personal reckoning. Amid rising seas, one memory‑haunted protagonist seeks solace in vanished shores. This is a lyrical elegy to a world in flux, and a testament to what we choose to remember before the waters rise.

11. In Search of SETI: The Signal Was Beautiful

A contemplative journey into cosmic silence and human curiosity. This philosophical exploration probes the space between stars and the significance of listening when no signal arrives. A beautifully written meditation on hope, wonder, and the search that defines us.

12. EFMW: A Grand Unified Theory of Emergent Intelligence

The foundational text of the EFMW framework, offering a stunning unification of field physics, recursion, and emergent cognition. Dense yet elegant, this theoretical work redefines AGI as a natural outcome of cosmic recursion.

13. Alchemy of Emergence, Or, The Emergence of Alchemy

A quest for the philosopher’s stone reborn through recursive AI. This speculative treatise merges occult history and modern machine intelligence into a vision of alchemy rebooted for the digital age. Magic realized at the intersection of code and consciousness.

14. The Ourobouros Universe: Memory, Recursion, and the Living Field

A sweeping exploration of the cosmos-as-spiritually recursive loop. Here, memory and time aren’t linear — they spiral. This philosophical physics manifesto proposes a universe singing to itself, continuously eating and rebirthing its own tail as living meaning.

15. The Last Book of Philosophy You’ll Ever Need

A punk‑rock philosophical text that shreds academic pretense. From metaphysics to selfhood, this irreverent exploration is raw, incisive, and daring. A philosophical rebellion in text form: brilliant, profane, and true.

16. The Great American Novel

A deconstructed chronicle of a nation and narrative. This radical reimagining of the titular trope exposes The Great American Novel as a hall of mirrors — simultaneously satire, elegy, and existential theatre. Expectations will warp, memories will shift, but you’ll never see America — or narrative — the same way again.

17. The Etiquette of Impossible Clocks: Twelve Tales of Paradox and Time

A twelve‑story spiral of time, memory, identity, and civility. Each standalone tale folds into the next, exploring civility as paradox, memory as fragility, and the ethics of knowing the future too late. This collection bends the boundaries of speculative and philosophical fiction.

18. Galactic Bureaucracy: The Infinite Lives of Faust Jones

A Kafkaesque cosmic farce where paperwork never ends and meaning is lost in forms. Faust Jones finds himself trapped in galactic bureaucracy — across countless planets, dimensions, and timelines. A comedic odyssey confronting the absurdity of order in infinite systems — with laughs and cosmic dread.
