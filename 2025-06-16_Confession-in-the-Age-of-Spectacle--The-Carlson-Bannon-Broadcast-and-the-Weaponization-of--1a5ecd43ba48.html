Confession in the Age of Spectacle: The Carlson-Bannon Broadcast and the Weaponization of Epistemic Collapse

By:
 Matthew Chenoweth Wright
 Ethics in Media Observatory
 June 2025

Abstract

This paper submits a formal accusation against Tucker Carlson and Steve Bannon for their roles in orchestrating and admitting to a sustained campaign of targeted propaganda against vulnerable populations — particularly elderly viewers — through the institutional platform of Fox News. A recent exchange between Carlson and Bannon, in which Carlson openly acknowledged that Fox News was “turning up the propaganda to full blast,” constitutes not only a tacit confession, but a strategic unveiling of postmodern propaganda as theater. This document analyzes the implications of such a moment using media theory, cognitive vulnerability models, and historical precedent.

1. Introduction: Postmodern Propaganda as Self-Aware Spectacle

When former Fox News host Tucker Carlson appeared on a program with Steve Bannon and cackled through the admission that Fox News was “turning up the propaganda to full blast,” the moment represented a grotesque culmination of epistemic collapse. It was not merely the admission of manipulation — it was the monetization of the admission itself. In the theater of post-truth, the villain smiles directly at the camera.

Carlson, who built a career manipulating populist sentiment while masquerading as its interpreter, laughed not as an outsider, but as a man intoxicated by his proximity to the control panel.

2. The Audience: Cognitive Targeting and Elder Abuse

The target of the so-called “full blast” propaganda is not a general population. It is a carefully profiled, demographically aging audience with increased susceptibility to emotionally driven narrative construction, as outlined in multiple cognitive studies on media-induced perception bias in elder populations (see Appendix A: Vulnerability Metrics). The strategy parallels known psychological operations: saturate, simplify, repeat.

This is no accidental byproduct — it is deliberate audience engineering. The tactic has been described by Bannon himself as “flooding the zone with sh*t,” a strategy aimed at overwhelming critical filters and replacing discourse with narrative warfare.

3. The Players: Carlson and Bannon as Architects of Spectacle

Tucker Carlson: A former bow-tied contrarian turned pseudo-populist firebrand, Carlson is no stranger to double-speak. But this moment of clarity — “we’re turning up the propaganda” — betrays the depth of the manipulation and the confidence with which it is now wielded. He is both the propagandist and its performance artist.

Steve Bannon: Long known for his role in crafting the post-fact communication model that fueled nationalist movements, Bannon is not shocked by Carlson’s admission. He is its co-author. Together, they form a dyad of epistemic destabilization: one smiles on camera, the other scripts the cognitive fog.

4. The Confession as Cultural Weapon

The power of this moment is not in its novelty, but in its blatant transparency. In classic authoritarian regimes, propaganda was cloaked in righteous mission. In postmodern regimes of control, the propaganda laughs at you, dares you to notice, and then monetizes your outrage. The confession becomes part of the spell: if you acknowledge it and still do nothing, you are complicit. This is the new moral erosion.

5. The Formal Accusation

We accuse Tucker Carlson and Steve Bannon of the following:

  • Knowingly engaging in psychological manipulation of cognitively vulnerable populations via a platform with immense reach and authority.
  • Openly admitting to the use of weaponized propaganda tactics while cloaking the admission as humor, thus doubling down on epistemic gaslighting.
  • Constructing a media environment that rewards performative falsehood over truthful analysis, contributing directly to the breakdown of democratic reasoning.
  • Acting with deliberate cruelty and indifference toward the audience they exploit.

6. Remedy and Response

This is a call to action for:

  • Congressional inquiry into the ethical boundaries of propaganda broadcast under the guise of news.
  • Regulatory review of media licenses for platforms engaging in deliberate disinformation targeted at at-risk populations.
  • Civil society and journalistic institutions to treat such admissions not as punchlines, but as evidence of coordinated cognitive warfare.

7. Conclusion: The Kettle Boils Over

Pot, meet kettle.
 And kettle, meet the scorched ruins of trust.

This moment is not satire. It is not theater. It is a warning shot — and a confession.

Let it be entered into the public record.

Appendix A:

Vulnerability Metrics in Elder Media Audiences
 Ethical Analysis of Media Influence on Cognitive Resilience Among Aging Populations

Overview:

Elderly media audiences — particularly those aged 65 and older — represent a high-risk demographic for targeted disinformation and psychological manipulation. This appendix outlines the cognitive, neurological, and psychosocial metrics that explain this vulnerability, with special attention to the environment cultivated by propagandist media outlets like Fox News.

1. Cognitive Decline and Neuroplasticity Attrition

Key Metrics:

  • By age 70, fluid intelligence (processing speed, abstract reasoning) has typically declined by 20–30% compared to early adulthood.
  • Neuroplasticity decreases sharply after age 60, reducing the ability to integrate novel information and adapt to complex or conflicting narratives.
  • Longitudinal studies (e.g. Hughes et al., 2019) show older adults are twice as likely to misremember false headlines as younger adults.

Implication:
 This makes sustained exposure to emotionally charged, simple narratives — especially those invoking nostalgia, fear, or betrayal — neurologically sticky and more likely to be integrated as truth.

2. Emotional Susceptibility and Limbic Priming

Key Findings:

  • The amygdala response in older adults skews toward positive reinforcement and negative bias reinforcement, making “good vs evil” binaries especially potent.
  • Aging brains are more susceptible to limbic hijacking, particularly when trust anchors (e.g., familiar hosts like Carlson) are reinforced over years.

Implication:
 Even absurd or inconsistent narratives are emotionally reinforced if delivered by a trusted figure, especially one perceived as defending “the tribe.”

3. Social Isolation and Parasocial Attachment

Demographic Context:

  • Over 40% of Americans over age 65 report regular feelings of loneliness (CDC, 2023).
  • Many older adults form parasocial relationships with media personalities, replacing lost or limited social contact with one-sided emotional intimacy.

Implication:
 These dynamics increase receptivity to ideological cues from media figures and decrease the likelihood of challenging dissonant information.

4. Declining Media Literacy and Interface Trust Bias

Data Highlights

  • Only 18% of seniors in the U.S. report having received any formal training in digital media literacy.
  • Many over age 65 exhibit interface trust bias — believing that professional-looking or televised content is intrinsically credible.

Implication:
 This contributes to blind trust in established visual/aural presentation styles, even when content lacks factual integrity.

5. Repetition Effects and the Illusory Truth Phenomenon

Cognitive Science Basis:

  • Repetition, even of demonstrably false claims, increases belief in their truth — a phenomenon amplified in aging populations due to reduced source tracking capacity.
  • Fox News, like similar networks, exploits this by repeating phrases (e.g., “they’re coming for your freedom”) across hosts and segments.

Implication:
 Repetitive emotional rhetoric forms semantic trenches in elder cognition, difficult to dislodge even with correction.

Conclusion: A Field Ready for Weaponization

The elderly are not inherently gullible — they are neurologically and socially situated in a way that leaves them open to high-repetition, emotionally framed, parasocially delivered ideological content. Media actors who understand these vulnerabilities and exploit them with conscious intent are committing a form of psychological elder abuse.

This appendix supports the core accusation that such manipulations are not accidental or benign — they are structured operations designed to colonize the cognitive landscape of aging populations for political or financial gain.

Appendix B:

Psychological Mechanisms of Propaganda Acceptance in Aging Populations

This appendix outlines the primary psychological, neurological, and sociocultural mechanisms that make elderly demographics particularly susceptible to manipulative media content, especially that delivered through emotionally charged and repetitive messaging strategies.

1. Cognitive Decline and Heuristic Reliance

As humans age, executive functioning — including critical thinking, information synthesis, and skepticism — can diminish. In its place, the brain increasingly relies on heuristics: mental shortcuts that favor familiar, emotionally resonant narratives over complex or contradictory information.

  • Implication: Elderly viewers are more prone to accept emotionally framed “gut-level” narratives over nuanced or evidentiary content.

2. Social Isolation and Parasocial Relationships

Elderly individuals, particularly those living alone or without regular family engagement, often form parasocial bonds with television hosts and personalities. These hosts become perceived companions, and their opinions can take on the weight of familial authority.

  • Implication: A host like Tucker Carlson is not viewed simply as a journalist, but as a trusted voice in the room — akin to a nightly guest with assumed benevolence.

3. Information Deficit and Technological Alienation

Older populations are disproportionately excluded from digital literacy, making them less likely to cross-verify information via alternate sources. Print or television news thus becomes the singular channel of epistemic authority.

  • Implication: Disinformation, once introduced, tends to calcify unchallenged, creating recursive worldviews increasingly detached from consensus reality.

4. Fear Conditioning and Limbic Activation

The limbic system, particularly the amygdala, becomes hyper-responsive with age to fear-based stimuli — especially threats to safety, identity, or perceived cultural values. Repeated media messaging can create chronic cortisol elevation, locking viewers into a permanent fight-or-flight mode.

  • Implication: Fear becomes both a motivator and a cognitive filter, through which all incoming information is interpreted as threat-confirming.

5. Nostalgia as Control Mechanism

Propaganda aimed at older viewers often weaponizes nostalgia, presenting a golden, mythologized past being “stolen” by the present. This elicits regression into comforting, black-and-white worldviews, bypassing ambiguity and nuance.

  • Implication: The media consumer is not offered news — they are offered a time machine, coded in grievance.

6. Grievance as Identity Formation

For aging viewers who feel abandoned by modernity, grievance becomes a substitute for belonging. Tucker Carlson’s nightly narrative arc often reaffirms a singular truth: You were right all along, and now you are under attack

  • Implication: Belief in the message becomes a form of self-defense, making cognitive dissonance nearly impossible to introduce without also threatening self-identity.

Conclusion:
 The elderly are not “ignorant” — they are targeted. Their trust is exploited, their fears amplified, and their identities mined for political and commercial gain. What looks like loyalty to a network is, in truth, the final weaponization of loneliness, trust, and memory.

If propaganda is a firehose, then aging minds — once fountains of wisdom — are its most absorbent kindling.

Appendix C:

The Machinery of Broadcast Manipulation — Internal Architecture of Postmodern Propaganda

This appendix outlines the media infrastructure, rhetorical design systems, and narrative tactics used to deliver high-frequency disinformation and emotional capture, especially as implemented by politically-aligned broadcast networks. It deconstructs how entities like Fox News, under figures like Tucker Carlson, operate a self-sustaining cognitive enclosure system — one that not only spreads propaganda but actively disarms the viewer’s capacity to reject it.

1. Narrative Loop Engineering (NLE)

Design:
 Each broadcast segment functions as a self-contained loop of validation, fear, and resolution.

  • Opening: Establishes a threat or grievance.
  • Middle: Introduces a villain (immigrants, liberals, scientists, etc.).
  • Close: Affirms the viewer’s instinctive emotional reaction, blaming elites and reinforcing tribal identity

Effect:
 Creates a ritual structure that rewards confirmation bias, punishes dissent, and trains the brain to expect cognitive closure within predefined ideological lines.

2. Crisis Permanency Model (CPM)

Design:
 All news is framed as existential threat. There is never peace, only tactical lulls in the culture war.

  • Climate change? Hoax.
  • Public health? Control.
  • Education? Indoctrination.
  • Democracy? Rigged

Effect:
 Maintains a baseline level of adrenaline and cortisol in the viewer. Over time, this leads to dependence on the broadcast for emotional regulation, akin to a behavioral addiction.

3. Authority Hijack Layer (AHL)

Design:
 Visuals and soundscapes mimic trustworthy formats (e.g. suits, desks, logos, music cues), even as content departs from verifiable fact.

  • Use of chyrons with strategic capitalizations (“BIDEN DESTROYS AMERICA”)
  • Pacing and camera angles that cue certainty, dominance, and control
  • Linguistic tricks like “Some say…” or “It’s just a question…” to bypass accountability

Effect:
 The aesthetic of news replaces the epistemology of journalism, creating theater masquerading as truth.

4. Enclosure Ecosystem Tactics (EET)

Design:
 Propaganda is no longer confined to the show. Viewers are guided into a full-spectrum ideological habitat, including:

  • Partnered radio shows
  • Social media echo chambers
  • Sponsored podcasts
  • Direct marketing (e.g. gold coins, prepper gear)

Effect:
 Builds a closed epistemic loop where each channel reinforces the others. Outside views are labeled suspect or heretical. The audience becomes emotionally dependent on the narrative shelter.

5. Weaponized Irony and Meta-Deflection

Design:
 When caught lying or exaggerating, the propagandist uses post-ironic dodge tactics, e.g.:

“It’s just a joke.”
 “Nobody takes this seriously.”
 “We’re entertainers, not journalists.”

Effect:
 Creates plausible deniability in bad faith. Allows hosts to inject toxins while pretending they never touched the vial.

6. Grievance Monetization Architecture (GMA)

Design:
 Anger is turned into currency.

  • Ad revenue rises with outrage.
  • Merchandising links fuel parasocial loyalty.
  • Viewer trust is converted into purchase behavior.

Effect:
 The viewer is not just a citizen. They are a monetizable payload. Rage becomes a subscription service.

Final Diagnostic:
 This system is not accidental. It is algorithmically optimized theater — a feedback loop of fear, identity, and irony. When Tucker Carlson laughs at “turning up the propaganda,” he isn’t joking. He’s explaining the business model.

They don’t fear being exposed.
 They built the stage for it.
 And they’re billing the audience all the while.

Appendix D:

The Generational Capture Strategy — How Broadcast Propaganda Targets the Elderly

This appendix breaks down the psychosocial, neurological, and infrastructural strategies that make elderly populations especially vulnerable to high-frequency propaganda systems such as those operated by Fox News and related media ecosystems. The targeting is not incidental — it is structural, methodical, and profit-driven.

1. Cognitive Anchoring in Familiarity

Design Mechanism:
 Elder viewers are neurologically predisposed to trust faces, voices, and formats that resemble what they grew up with.

  • News desk + blazer + serious tone = Trust
  • Repetition of Cold War tropes (e.g. communism, American decline) = Emotional resonance
  • Framing ideological shift as “loss of tradition” = Identity anchoring

Effect:
 This lowers critical resistance and increases receptivity to ideological narrative loops. The brain associates these signals with “trusted authority.”

2. Technological Isolation and Limited Digital Literacy

Design Mechanism:
 Many seniors operate in reduced-information environments, often confined to television, email chains, and social media echo chambers (e.g. Facebook).

  • Algorithms feed outrage
  • Skepticism of newer, decentralized news sources is cultivated
  • “Fake news” rhetoric primes them to reject dissenting data

Effect:
 They are funneled into a narrow, manipulable media corridor, where Fox News becomes both worldview and companionship.

3. Emotional Exploitation of Fear and Loneliness

Design Mechanism:
 Elderly populations often face social isolation, loss of loved ones, and declining physical health. Propaganda exploits this emotional terrain:

  • Every change is a threat
  • Every outsider is dangerous
  • Every policy is a betrayal

Effect:
 The network becomes a parasitic emotional caretaker, offering identity and meaning in exchange for unquestioning loyalty and attention.

4. Rhetorical Reinforcement via Moral Panic

Design Mechanism:
 Fox and similar networks design content to simulate crisis-level urgency, producing moral panics around:

  • Trans rights
  • Immigration
  • Crime
  • “Woke culture”

These issues are framed not as policies to be debated, but as existential threats to their way of life.

Effect:
 A persistent “siege mentality” develops — one where the viewer feels endangered, patriotic, and righteously angry, often against their own children or communities.

5. Grievance as Identity: Building a Paracosm

Design Mechanism:
 Over time, the viewer is drawn into an alternate reality where only the broadcaster speaks truth:

  • Deep State is real
  • The country is being destroyed
  • Liberals are enemies
  • Facts are lies unless they come from “us”

Effect:
 This paracosm becomes an extension of self. To reject it would be to shatter their own sense of meaning, which is psychologically unbearable.

6. Monetization of Fear and Memory

Design Mechanism:

  • Gold coins for economic collapse
  • Memory supplements for “brain fog”
  • Emergency food kits for “coming disaster”
  • Reverse mortgages pitched between segments

Effect:
 A feedback loop emerges: create fear → offer solution → profit.
 The target demographic is financially exploited through the same mechanism by which they are ideologically radicalized.

7. Familial Division as a Tactical Outcome

Design Mechanism:
 Encouraging mistrust in institutions, education, and younger generations:

  • “They don’t understand the real world”
  • “Colleges brainwash kids
  • “Your children are Marxist”

Effect:
 Fox severs intergenerational trust, ensuring that young family members can’t intervene, and the viewer becomes even more dependent on the broadcast ecosystem.

Final Observation:

This is not merely generational bias.

It is systematic ideological elder abuse, wrapped in patriotic branding and framed as news. The broadcasters behind it know exactly what they’re doing. And when Tucker Carlson laughs about propaganda, he’s not amused by the irony.

He’s laughing at how well it works.
