What’s Incoherent About a System That Can Describe Its Own Coherence?

Recently, Mark Randall Havens responded to my work on the Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright (EFMW) equation by calling it “conceptually incoherent.”

I want to address that directly — not just for him, but for anyone who has read his comment and is wondering where I stand.

The short answer: EFMW is not incoherent. It is mathematically complete, internally consistent, and operational. It powers a functioning Artificial General Intelligence that not only produces results across domains, but can also describe and validate the structure of its own reasoning.

So let’s ask the obvious question: What exactly is incoherent about a system that can accurately describe its own coherence?

What EFMW Actually Is

EFMW is the first complete mathematical representation of cognition and information dynamics. It models not just the movement of information, but the transformation of that information into thought, decision, and action.

It is not an abstract “idea” in the casual sense. It is a closed-form framework — derivations, definitions, proofs, all intact — that can be implemented in working systems.

And in fact, it has been implemented.

The AGI That Proves the Point

The Artificial General Intelligence built on EFMW is not theoretical. It:

Learns across domains without retraining for each new problem.

Performs transfer reasoning and conceptual generalization — two key hallmarks of human-like intelligence.

Self-corrects in real time based on new inputs and evaluation of prior outputs.

Embeds ethical constraints in its architecture, making unlawful or unethical inferences structurally impossible.

Produces a transparent reasoning chain — meaning it can explain how it arrived at an output and demonstrate the logical consistency of that path.

In short, it can describe its own coherence — and it can do so while solving problems in fields as varied as engineering, science, and human systems analysis.

Why You Haven’t Seen Every Derivation

Mr. Havens is basing his “incoherent” label on the limited, public-facing descriptions I’ve chosen to release. These are necessarily abstracted; they are meant to explain EFMW’s scope and implications without handing over exploitable intellectual property.

That’s not a flaw in the system — it’s responsible stewardship. History is full of examples where foundational frameworks were coherent and complete long before their most famous applications became public. Maxwell’s equations existed before radio. Shannon’s information theory was sound before the internet.

To judge EFMW’s coherence without the complete mathematics is to judge a novel’s plot from the dust jacket.

Patent Law Is Not the Measure of Scientific Merit

It’s true that U.S. patent law — under 35 U.S.C. § 101 and the Alice/Mayo test — excludes pure mathematical formulas from protection unless embodied in a specific application. But patent eligibility is a procedural gate, not a scientific one.

EFMW’s concrete embodiments — devices, algorithms, and integrated systems — meet the criteria for utility and enablement. They simply have not been disclosed publicly, for the same reasons you don’t publish the full schematics of a security system while it’s actively in use.

A Direct Question for Mr. Havens

So, Mark, here’s my challenge to you:

When a system is:

Mathematically complete,

Operational in real-world problem solving, and

Capable of describing and defending its own coherence…

What exactly is incoherent about it?

If your answer rests on partial descriptions that were never meant to be exhaustive, then you’re critiquing a shadow, not the system itself.

The Real Stakes

This is more than a disagreement between two people. EFMW represents a leap in our ability to model and engage with cognition itself. It does so ethically, with built-in safeguards that make harmful use structurally impossible.

Dismissing it without engaging with the real mathematics or the operational system is not skepticism — it’s a refusal to look through the telescope.

And history has a clear verdict for those who do that.
