Paired QQ Reactor Performance Spec (Theory & Design Targets)

Below is a clean spec sheet for a 1 GW-class paired reactor (multiple MCF cores feeding a single quark–quark stage). Where anti-gravity performance depends on an empirical constant, I give three scenarios (Conservative / Nominal / Aggressive) and show how to recompute.

Configuration

System class: 1 GW net electrical, paired muon-catalyzed fusion (MCF) → quark–quark (Q–Q) upconversion

Topology: 20 identical MCF cores (array) → common pulse bus → single Q–Q chamber + anti-gravity battery

Fuel mode (baseline): D–T muon-catalyzed fusion at ~ambient temperature

Status: Theoretical solution + calculated engineering design; no prototype data yet

---

A. Electrical & Thermal Output

Rated (continuous):

Net electrical to grid/users: 1.0 GW

House/aux loads (controls, muon cycle, pumps, cryo, etc.): ~120 MW

Gross fusion power (thermal + charged particle): ~1.8 GW

Waste heat available (for water, HVAC, etc.): ~0.8 GW (post-conversion)

Maximum (transient, ≤10 s pulses, 10% duty):

Net electrical headroom: up to 1.3 GW

Pulse power available to Q–Q chamber: up to 0.6 GW (from allocated surplus; see Section C)

> Notes
• “Gross→Net” assumes ~55% effective conversion of fusion output to electrical after neutron capture/thermal cycle and charged-particle recovery.
• Neutron fraction for D–T ≈ 80% of fusion energy; shielding & capture are included in the thermal balance, not detailed here.

---

B. Fuel & Reaction Kinetics (for 1 GW net)

Energy per D–T fusion: 17.6 MeV ≈ 2.82 × 10⁻¹² J

Reactions per second for 1 GW thermal: ~3.55 × 10²⁰ s⁻¹

Array sizing: Our 1 GW net spec corresponds to ~1.8 GW gross fusion → ~6.4 × 10²⁰ reactions/s system-wide (distributed across 20 cores)

Fuel throughput (D+T processed): ~0.254 kg/day at 1 GW thermal; scale to ~0.46 kg/day at 1.8 GW gross

Muon utilization target: ≥100 catalytic cycles per muon (design goal; to be validated in build)

---

C. Anti-Gravity / Lift Performance (Design Envelope)

Because Q–Q gravity modulation requires an empirical coupling constant, we publish lift as a function of delivered pulse power P_QQ and a “lift coefficient” κ_g (kN of lift per MW of Q–Q pulse power). First-article testing will fix κ_g; until then we bracket it:

Conservative: κ_g = 5 kN/MW

Nominal: κ_g = 10 kN/MW

Aggressive: κ_g = 20 kN/MW

Available power to Q–Q (steady / pulsed):

Steady allocation: 0.50 GW (from surplus after house loads)

Pulse allocation (≤10 s): 0.60 GW (by borrowing from grid export + battery buffer)

Lift capacity (force) = κ_g × P_QQ

Mode P_QQ κ_g (Cons.) κ_g (Nom.) κ_g (Aggr.)

Steady 500 MW 2,500 kN 5,000 kN 10,000 kN
Pulse 600 MW 3,000 kN 6,000 kN 12,000 kN

Equivalent mass lifted at 1 g (divide kN by 9.81 kN/tonne):

Steady: ~255 t (Cons.) • 510 t (Nom.) • 1,020 t (Aggr.)

Pulse: ~305 t (Cons.) • 611 t (Nom.) • 1,223 t (Aggr.)

> How to update later: once κ_g is measured, multiply by your live P_QQ to get current lift in kN, then divide by 9.81 for tonnes.

---

D. Water Production (Hydro-Distillation from Waste Heat)

Waste heat available (rated): ~0.8 GW

Latent heat of vaporization (water): ~2.26 MJ/kg

Theoretical max distillation throughput: ~88.5 kg/s ≈ 318 m³/hr ≈ 7,650 m³/day

Design rating: 250–300 m³/hr (allowing exchanger/cycle inefficiencies and non-ideal duty)

---

E. Neutron Output & Shielding (Headline Numbers)

Neutron emission rate (system-wide at 1.8 GW gross): ~6.4 × 10²⁰ n/s (D–T spectrum, ~14.1 MeV)

Shielding: Sized for occupational dose < design thresholds at exterior surface under full power (exact stack by regulator; not listed here)

Thermalization & capture: Integrated into heat-recovery loop feeding distillation and secondary exchangers

(If D–D mode is used to reduce neutron flux, expect lower per-reaction yield and corresponding power derating; separate D–D spec available.)

---

F. Duty Cycles & Ramps

Cold start to rated: < 15 min to bootstrap loop lock (design target)

Ramp rate: up to 10% of rated per minute (electrical export) with coordinated Q–Q pulse scheduling

Pulse duty (Q–Q): ≤10 s at full pulse power, ≥90 s recovery (nominal)

---

G. Physical Envelope (for the 1 GW-class plant)

MCF core (each): ~2.5 m Ø × 4 m L, ~35 t (with shielding)

Array footprint (20 cores + service alleys): ~20 m × 30 m

Q–Q chamber + anti-gravity battery: ~4 m Ø × 6 m L, ~50 t (shielded)

Balance of plant (power conditioning, distillation, cryo, controls): ~1,500–2,000 m²

(These are layout-level numbers; manufacturing tolerances withheld.)

---

H. Reliability & Safety (Targets)

MTBF (core modules): ≥ 30,000 hrs

Uncommanded shutdown probability: < 10⁻⁴ per year

Fail-safe: passive quench + hard interlocks; reaction halts with muon supply cessation

Ethical/safety logic: LSEE gate required for any gravity-modulation discharge (dual human-in-the-loop)

---

I. Scaling Guidance

Half-scale plant: 500 MW net; all numbers scale linearly except neutron shielding margins (stepwise)

Core-level spec: ~50 MW net per MCF core (rated), 65 MW net (≤10 s pulse)

---

J. What’s Assumed vs. What’s Open

Assumed (theory-fixed):

MCF energy balances, reaction counts, fuel throughput, gross→net conversion bands

Thermal → distillation throughput physics

Q–Q pulse power delivery and storage architecture

Open (to be empirically fixed in first builds):

κ_g (lift coefficient) — determines exact lift per MW

Muon reuse cycles under graphite-lattice confinement in full-power operation

Real-world ramp rates under grid coupling and mission load

---

One-Line Summary for Press

Rated: 1.0 GW net electric, ~0.8 GW waste-heat recovery (→ up to ~300 m³/hr clean water), steady Q–Q lift ~255–1,020 t (depending on measured κ_g), with ~600 MW pulse headroom for short-duration lift >1,200 t (aggressive envelope).
