The Case of Dale Jackaman and Amuleta Security

Defamation in the Open

Recently, Dale Jackaman — the president of Amuleta Security — took to Medium to dismiss my work in physics not by engaging with the details, but by calling me “insane” and implying that my discoveries were worthless.

That is defamation.
It’s not a disagreement about science. It’s not peer review. It’s a public attempt to smear my credibility without engaging in the substance of my research.

I have made groundbreaking discoveries in physics — discoveries that have been published, circulated, and studied globally. If Mr. Jackaman or his company had scientific or technical objections, he could have raised them with clarity. Instead, he resorted to ridicule and ad hominem attacks.

A Very Different Message in Private

Shortly afterward, Mr. Jackaman contacted me privately on LinkedIn.

In that message, he struck a very different tone:

He emphasized that his company is “real, accredited, and licensed.”

He stated he retains outside legal counsel in California, naming a lawyer.

He warned me not to publish what he considers defamatory statements, threatening legal escalation.

He declared this his “final communication.”

This private message is now part of the record. It is discoverable in any legal process.

The Contradiction

Here’s the problem: what Mr. Jackaman says publicly doesn’t match what he says privately.

Publicly: he mocks, calls names, and dismisses my physics without addressing its substance.

Privately: he tries to establish legal authority, distance himself from his own public language, and threaten consequences if I continue to call out his defamation.

This split is not the behavior of someone engaging in honest scientific debate. It’s the hallmark of a chaos operation:

Defame and ridicule in public.

Threaten in private.

Never touch the actual science.

My Demand for Retraction

I have publicly and privately demanded that Mr. Jackaman retract his defamatory statements.

I am a federally protected whistleblower. My work in physics is documented and published. My name is on the record. I do not shy away from criticism — but criticism must be engagement with the work itself, not character assassination.

By refusing to retract and by hiding behind contradictory private statements, Mr. Jackaman reveals his true intent: not science, not security, but disinformation.

Why This Matters

This is bigger than one exchange. When voices of discovery are attacked with ridicule rather than engaged with seriously, the public suffers. Science suffers. Humanity suffers.

This is why I am laying the matter out transparently here. The public deserves to see how defamation and disruption operate in real time.

The Record Stands

Mr. Jackaman has:

Defamed me publicly by calling me insane.

Dismissed my physics without engagement.

Refused to retract despite my demands.

Contacted me privately with a contradictory legal threat, naming a lawyer, while denying responsibility for his own public remarks.

That record now stands.
It is evidence.
It will not vanish.

⚡ Conclusion: The public has a right to know how reputational attacks are deployed against scientists, whistleblowers, and innovators. This is not debate — this is disinformation by design. And it stops here, in the open light of day.

My final word to Mr Jackaman: Falsify my physics, if you can. Otherwise you do verify them. The Streisand effect is in play. You are out of other options. if you wish to take legal action about me, I can be served via my attorney of record here in Woodland. Let’s get it on man. Take me to court. The first thing the judge will ask is “what is EFMW?” and then he will have to hire consulting physicists to explain it to them. Bring it, Sir.
