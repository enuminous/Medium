You Violated Federal Law — Here’s What That Means

Under the United States Data Breach Notification Laws, companies operating in the U.S. that store personally identifiable information (PII) are legally required to notify affected individuals promptly if their data has been compromised. In cases where financial, medical, or login credentials are exposed, notification must be issued without unreasonable delay once the breach is discovered.

Failure to disclose breaches has legal and financial consequences. Depending on jurisdiction, fines for non-compliance can range from hundreds of thousands to millions of dollars. But more importantly, you betrayed the trust of your users — people who relied on your platforms and services with the assumption that you would follow the law and protect their data.

You didn’t.

I am among the many who were never notified, despite the fact that my information was breached multiple times over the last four years on your platforms.
