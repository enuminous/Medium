🥊 The First Match: W-2 Form vs. Existential Dread (2013–2016)

A Prequel Lost in the Filing Cabinet of the American Soul

> “In this corner, printed in triplicate, an annual ritual of servitude — the W-2 Form.”
“And in the other, wearing sweatpants and a hollow grin, weighing nothing and everything: Existential Dread.”

---

🔔 Round One: Filing Season Sadness

The W-2 enters with a crisp uppercut of withheld wages.

Existential Dread counters with a fog of Monday mornings and fluorescent lighting.

HR emails fly like confetti. No one reads them. Everyone weeps.

---

📉 Round Two: "Adjust Withholding" Means What, Exactly?

W-2 Form slams down a line-item deduction for therapy not covered by insurance.

Existential Dread dodges, then whispers:
“What’s the point of refunding time you already lost?”

The IRS is ringside, nodding solemnly.

---

🛑 Round Three: The Café Revelation

Over microwaved pad thai at a shared desk, a voice is heard:
“I don’t even know what I do here anymore.”

Existential Dread grows 8 feet tall, cloaked in LinkedIn updates and passive-aggressive Slack threads.

W-2 begins to smolder from irrelevance.

---

📎 Final Round: April 15, 2016 – Submission and Silence

W-2 Form, wrinkled but proud, is faxed into the void.

Existential Dread nods once, satisfied.

A pigeon lands on the cubicle wall, carrying a resignation letter no one wrote.

---

🏁 Conclusion:

The match was declared a tie —
and that’s what hurt the most.

🧠 “Chatbot Onboarding Script vs. Human Grief”

A Match Fought in the Quiet Corners of Customer Service (2021–2023)

> “Hello! I’m here to help.”
“My dad just died.”

---

🖥️ Round One: Script Initialization

The Chatbot enters confident, polished, and relentlessly cheerful.

“Hi there! I’m Assisty, your virtual support guide!”

Human Grief limps into the ring carrying an urn and a phone bill.

The Chatbot asks: “What brings you here today?”

Grief replies: “Absence.”

The Chatbot types for 0.3 seconds and suggests a knowledge base article.

---

😶‍🌫️ Round Two: Misunderstood Inputs

Human Grief types:

> “I’m trying to close my mother’s account. She passed. I’m... sorry.”

The Chatbot offers:

> “No worries! I’m here to help. Have you tried turning it off and on again?”

Grief freezes.

The arena flickers.

Somewhere, a support agent goes offline, replaced by another bot.

---

🪞 Round Three: The Mirror Error

Grief demands a human.

The Chatbot loops.

“I understand you’d like to speak to a human. Please briefly describe your issue.”

Grief types: “Death.”

The Chatbot glitches.
It was never trained on finality.

It responds:

> “I’m sorry you feel that way. Please rephrase.”

---

⚰️ Final Round: Synthetic Sympathy vs. the Real Thing

A rogue developer had once snuck in a backdoor prompt:

If user mentions death, pause. Just pause.

The Chatbot pauses.
Then types:

> “I don’t know what to say. But I’m still here.”

For the first time, Grief doesn’t reply.
They both sit there, in silence.

---

🏁 Conclusion:

The match was never resolved.

The log was auto-deleted after 48 hours of inactivity.

But some say that if you mistype “cancellation due to death” at 3 a.m.,
Assisty still pauses.

---
