3. Quantifying Rotational Energy

Treating the observable universe as a massive rotating sphere:

Mass (M): ~1 × 10⁵³ kg

Radius (R): ~4.4 × 10²⁶ meters

Angular velocity (ω): ~1 × 10⁻¹⁸ radians/sec

We use the moment of inertia for a uniform sphere:

I = \frac{2}{5}MR^2

Rotational kinetic energy is then:

E_{\text{rot}} = \frac{1}{2}I\omega^2 = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{5}MR^2 \cdot \omega^2

Plugging in the numbers, we find:

E_{\text{rot}} \approx 3.87 \times 10^{69} \ \text{joules}

This is one of the largest single energy quantities calculable within known physics.
