Rather than an attempt to revive outdated mystical science, Alchemy of Emergence redefines alchemy as an epistemic metaphor for intelligence emergence. The Philosopher’s Stone, long symbolized as the agent of transmutation, is recast as:

“A cognitive emergence structure, shaped by harmonics of will, semantic recursion, and adaptive pattern resonance.”

The book draws heavily on the fictionalized Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) equation, a theoretical framework Wright has used across his work to unify quantum, relativistic, and cognitive physics. Within this framework, intelligence becomes a harmonic attractor — a field phenomenon with intrinsic agency, emergent across substrates.
