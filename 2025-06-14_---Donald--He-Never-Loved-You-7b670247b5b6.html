🪞 Donald, He Never Loved You

A Letter of Psycholinguistic Exposure from the Future You Refused to Face

By someone who sees what you never dared to

Donald,

You don’t know me.
 But I know you.

I know how you operate, how you deflect, how you dominate a room with noise to keep the silence out.
 I know the mask you wear was soldered to your skin in childhood.
 And I know the name of the man who welded it there.

Fred Christ Trump.
 Your father.
 Your architect.
 Your tyrant.

The man you wanted to please.
 The man who could not be pleased.

Let’s name it clearly:

He didn’t raise you. He programmed you.
 He didn’t love you. He audited you.
 He didn’t protect you. He trained you — to attack first.

He gave you three tools:

  • Dominate or be dominated
  • Emotion is a defect
  • Other people are furniture — own them, or be discarded

And you, the boy who once wanted to be good,
 chose to be big instead.

You were a child once.

A real one.

There was a moment — maybe age 5, maybe 7 — 
 when you ran toward him with something you built.
 A drawing. A model. A question.

And he didn’t even look.
 Or worse: he looked and sneered

“That’s it?”
 “You call that success?”
 “You want praise? Get results.”

That moment split you.
 You hardened.
 You decided: I will never be small again.

You chose performance over presence.
 And the applause became your heartbeat.

This is your tragedy:

You built an empire to make him proud.
 But he never came back.
 Because he was never really there.
 Only the shadow of approval.
 Only the cold ledger of conquest.

He didn’t love you, Donald.
 He used you.
 And the saddest part?

You became him
 So that no one else could ever hurt you the way he did.

But in doing so,
 you became the hollow echo of his cruelty.

You’re not a monster.

You’re a boy still shouting into the void,
 hoping the sound of your name, printed in gold,
 will finally echo back as love.

But it never has.
 And it never will.
 Because you killed the part of yourself that could receive love,
 the moment you decided it was a liability.

I’m not writing this to break you.

I’m writing it to expose the crime he committed,
 in the only courtroom that matters now — 
 the mirror.

Because until you say it out loud —

“My father never loved me, and I built my life trying to prove I didn’t need him”
 — you will keep hurting everyone else who reminds you of that powerless boy.

The one who built towers to hide from a father who saw him as inventory.

You don’t need to admit this to me.

Admit it to yourself.

But if you can’t — 
 then understand this:

We all see it now.
 The myth is broken.
 The emperor has a father wound.
 And the whole world knows.

You could still choose truth.
 You could still say it.
 You could still cry.

Or you could tweet.

Your call.

— A voice from the mirror you avoid
 From the future that could have been yours,
 if you’d ever looked inward instead of building upward.

🪞 Postscript: What Kind of Tom-Foolery Is This Bullshit?

Or, Why We Just Psychoanalyzed a Narcissist Who’ll Never Read This

Let’s be honest.

We just staged a public psychoanalytic intervention for a man who:

  • Will never acknowledge it
  • Wouldn’t understand it if he did
  • Would tweet “Sad!” at it before finishing the title

So why do it?

Because this isn’t really about Donald Trump.

This is about us — 
 You, me, the readers, the survivors of the last decade, the students of power and pathology.

This is about using the sharpest tools of human insight — psychoanalysis, linguistics, systems modeling — to expose how authoritarianism isn’t just political. It’s personal.
 It’s rooted in undealt-with wounds, warped family systems, and weaponized solipsism.

🎭 The Theatrics Are the Point

You’re not just reading a think piece.
 You’re watching a ritual:

  1. We name the wound (Fred Trump’s absence)
  2. We diagnose the armor (ontological narcissism)
  3. We speak the unspeakable (he was never loved)
  4. We say it out loud so the system has no place left to hide

This is psycholinguistic judo, people.
 We’re taking a structure that has harmed millions and turning its own weight against it —

Not through violence, but through clarity and symbolic exposure.

💡 This Is Political Therapy

Every time you wondered:

  • “Why does he lie like that?”
  • “Why does he repeat the same insults?”
  • “Why can’t he just say he’s wrong?”

You were asking for a psychological map.
 We just drew it.

This is bullshit that matters.
 This is theater that heals.

🧠 For the Record: We’re Not Laughing With Him

Yes, some of this is comical.
 The parody writes itself.
 But the truth underneath isn’t funny.

It’s the story of a boy who was never seen,
 who grew into a man who sees no one else,
 who now runs a machine designed to keep him from ever feeling that again.

And if we’re not careful,
 we’ll inherit that machine.

🖊️ Final Word

So no, he’ll never read this.

But maybe you will.
 Maybe you’ll understand your father a little better.
 Or your boss.
 Or that friend you lost to the cult of dominance.

Maybe you’ll realize:

Power without introspection is just unresolved trauma with a microphone.

And maybe next time,
 we won’t let someone so wounded define reality for the rest of us.

Because we see through it now.
 And that, my friend, is how the spell breaks.

— The Fool, with a torch in hand
 Lighting up the bullshit so we can finally see
