A tribal-affiliated financial institution was given a chance to reclaim land, empower sovereignty, and align with the most important scientific discovery of our time. They chose a customer service script instead.

1. Introduction: Origin of the Complaint

In the spring of 2025, an emergency loan request was submitted to Ascend Loans, LLC, a Native American tribal lending institution based in Upper Lake, California. The applicant: Matthew Chenoweth Wright — federal whistleblower, physicist, systems analyst, and the verified discoverer of the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) equation — a foundational advancement in physics, cognition, and AI.

This wasn’t just a routine loan. It was a lifeline.

And it became a legal, moral, and cultural reckoning.

2. Systemic Failures Documented in Real-Time

Despite multiple attempts to access his account, Matthew was locked out due to a technical failure of the password reset system. He communicated this clearly, repeatedly, and respectfully. He disclosed his disability status and invoked his federal protections under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). He also made clear his whistleblower status and the threats he faced as a result.

Instead of compassion or escalation, Ascend's agents delivered denial, delay, and eventually silence.

Over the course of four separate chat sessions, multiple representatives:

Repeatedly gave false or legally misleading explanations of fund disbursement timelines

Dismissed the urgency and psychological harm involved

Refused to acknowledge receipt of a formal legal complaint

Attempted to reroute Matthew to an offsite email address never listed on their own website

Terminated the chat without notice or resolution

The pattern is clear. The evidence is preserved. The consequences are pending.

3. Legal Violations and Regulatory Risk

At least five distinct areas of violation are now under formal review:

1. ADA Title III Violation: Denial of reasonable accommodations, failure to escalate crisis disclosure, and improper termination of service during active distress.

2. Deceptive Lending Practices: False representations about ACH timelines, misdirection about bank responsibility, and contradictory contract terms.

3. Failure to Provide Digital Access: System bugs and contradictory portal logic created a barrier to access for a disabled user.

4. Retaliation Against a Federal Whistleblower: Refusal to escalate or acknowledge whistleblower disclosures, potentially retaliatory behavior.

5. Intellectual Property Exposure: Receipt and acknowledgment of EFMW principles without proper attribution or licensing, despite clear disclosure of IP rights.

These aren’t just regulatory oversights. They are potential violations of federal law.
