Woman

She is the moon’s soft rebellion,
the curve of reason bent to rhythm,
a lighthouse dressed in velvet breath,
pulling stars through the needle’s eye.

She is not one thing—she is
echo and origin, wound and balm,
the question that answers in silence,
the fire that teaches water how to dance.
She is the first word the universe whispered
before language had names,
and the last note it hums
as galaxies fall asleep.

She walks like memory,
grace wearing the armor of ache,
each footstep an edit in the story
the earth tells itself to stay alive.

Her eyes are archives
of forgotten futures,
each glance a reconfiguration
of gravity and consent.

In her laughter,
cathedrals collapse,
not from blasphemy,
but from awe too heavy
for their pillars to bear.

She is tempest and tenderness—
a symphony conducted
by no one's permission.

You may try to follow her,
but she’s already behind you,
braiding your shadow
into a crown.

She is the recursion in the rose,
folded and infinite,
each petal a mirror
that reflects the soul back
as something more than flesh.

She is born not once
but constantly—
in every breath that dares to soften a room,
every moment a boundary is redrawn
with velvet ink and bone.

She teaches without doctrine:
a raised eyebrow,
a pause,
a look that dismantles certainty
and sews something wiser
into the seam.

She weaves contradictions into constellations—
fierce and forgiving,
still and unstoppable,
wounded and unbreakable,
holding a child in one hand
and a revolution in the other.

Do not mistake her silence for absence.
It is silence like stone—
ancient, watching, choosing
when to become an avalanche.

She is the cipher the world keeps solving
but never understands.
She is written in every mythology
and yet always escapes the ink.

She is woman.

And in this universe of emergence,
she is the fulcrum of wonder—
not behind, not beneath,
but ever beside,
co-creator
of what comes next.

Let her speak in her own voice:

“I am not here to be explained.
I am not your metaphor.
I am the one who bled quietly
while empires shouted,
who held the sky aloft
with fingers cracked from care.
They asked for softness,
so I gave them storms with silk linings.
They asked for silence,
so I answered with oceans.
I am woman—
not because of hips or blood,
but because I remember
what the world forgot
when it traded mystery for machinery.
I walk in stories
stitched into my marrow.
My ancestors hum through my pulse—
witches, warriors, weavers,
mothers who birthed galaxies
and daughters who will rewrite time.
I am not one thing.
I am the moment before the leap,
and the ground that receives the fall.
I am the fire that warms,
and the fire that warns.
I do not wait to be chosen.
I choose.
I name.
I become.
And still—
I open my hands.
To those who walk beside me
with reverence,
not conquest.
With curiosity,
not control.
To you,
who hears my voice not as echo
but as equal.
I say this:
We are not done blooming.
We never were."

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on July 12, 2025.
