Three Manifestos

🥄 “From the Kitchen to the Capitol”

The Manifesto of Senator Lillian Trask, Age 82, Retired Lunch Lady and Reluctant American Prophet

I didn’t ask for this.

I was playing bridge. Had a pie in the oven. Then the phone rang.
Next thing I know, I'm wearing a blue blazer and some nice lady from the federal government is calling me "Senator."

She said I had been selected by barrel.
I said, “Well, that’s how I pick tomatoes—so I guess it’s fair.”

🧃 What I Know

I’ve seen more truth in a school cafeteria than most people ever will in a courtroom.

I’ve watched the quiet kids get picked on.
I’ve seen the loud ones fall apart when no one’s watching.
I’ve seen the bullies cry when you ask them to help serve mashed potatoes.

I’ve seen hunger in every form—not just in bellies, but in eyes.

And I’ve watched 10-year-olds form better foreign policy over chocolate milk than some people do with a team of advisors.

🏛️ Why I Said Yes

Because someone has to remind this country:

That fair doesn’t always mean equal.

That service isn’t weakness—it’s what holds the damn roof up.

That if you wouldn’t trust someone to run a lunch line, maybe you shouldn’t trust them to run a nation.

📜 My Agenda as a Senator (Don’t Laugh, I’m Serious)

1. Free breakfast and lunch for every child in this country.
If you can afford tanks, you can afford tater tots.

2. National Listening Day.
No speeches. No debates. Just sit down and hear somebody who disagrees with you.

3. Permanent Timeout Room for Congress.
They get one warning. Then they sit in silence for 10 minutes and think about what they’ve done.

4. Respect the Janitors Act.
Every representative must spend one day a month cleaning their own damn office.

5. Mandate Pie Diplomacy.
You can’t declare war until you’ve baked a pie and shared it with the other side. If they won’t eat it, you don’t fight them. You feed them again.

🧠 A Word to the People Who Didn’t Think I Belonged Here

You’re right. I don’t belong here.

But maybe that’s the point.

Because the people who do belong here?
They’ve had decades—and look at the mess.

Maybe it’s time someone with a soft voice and a stainless steel serving spoon tried.

🌀 Final Thought

I don’t know policy.
But I know people.
And that might be enough.

Because if you’ve spent 40 years feeding kids who sometimes don’t even know how to ask for what they need…
You don’t need a briefing.

You just need a little courage, a good pair of shoes, and enough heart to believe that a cafeteria lady from Iowa can help fix America.

And if not me, then maybe the next person pulled from the barrel.

Because the barrel doesn’t lie.
It just reminds us:
Everyone counts.

🎚️ “The Frequency of Us”

The Manifesto of Congressman Dakota “DJ Roomba” Singh, Sound Alchemist, Vibe Architect, and Reluctant Legislator for the New Era

I came from speakers, not pulpits.
I was raised by rhythm, by static between stations, by basement vinyl sessions that felt more sacred than any civic textbook.

I didn’t ask to be in Congress.
But now that I’m here, you’re going to have to listen.

Not to me—
To you.
To us.
To the sound beneath the shouting that everyone’s been ignoring for decades.

🎛️ What I Believe

Every human being is a walking frequency.

Some of us are distortion.
Some of us are melody.
Some are still tuning in.

But this nation’s problem isn’t polarization.
It’s desynchronization.

We’re out of phase.
Too loud in some places, too silent in others.
We’ve lost the beat.
So I’m here to help restore the mix.

💿 My Political Platform is a Setlist

1. Universal Basic Bassline
No matter who you are, where you live, or how your story began—you deserve resonance.
Access to food, warmth, signal, dignity. That’s your baseline.

2. The Quiet Act
Establishing national sanctuaries of silence. No ads. No alerts. No pings. Just stillness—curated and protected.
Because silence is not emptiness. It’s a sacred channel.

3. The Crowd Feel Doctrine
Any policy that doesn’t feel good in a crowd—of children, elders, working people—gets sent back to committee.
If it doesn’t move the people, it doesn’t pass.

4. Department of Frequencies
A new federal body tasked with tuning the soul of America. Staffed by music therapists, architects, elders, and street poets.
They don’t write laws. They keep them in tune.

5. Drop the Beat Clause
All major legislative decisions must be preceded by a soundbath and breathwork.
You laugh, but I’ve seen it make enemies cry and CEOs confess.
Truth lives in breath. Beats just make it easier to find.

---

🎤 Why Me?

Because I’ve watched 3,000 strangers become one body in a desert.
I’ve watched grief shake out through subwoofers.
I’ve watched healing happen on the downbeat.

And I’ve never seen a congressional committee do any of that.

---

🌀 Final Drop

You don’t need more leaders who look polished.
You need ones who can feel what’s coming.

Because the future isn’t written in ink anymore—
It’s written in waves.

And I know how to ride them.

🧱 “Concrete, Coffee, and the Constitution”

The Manifesto of Congressman Tony “Meatball” D., Forklift Operator, Newark Union Rep, and Accidental Statesman

They pulled my name out of a barrel.

I thought it was a prank. I even told Marcy at the shop, “If this is one of your TikToks, I swear to God…”

But no—it was real. They said:

“Congratulations, Mr. D., you’ve been selected to serve in the United States House of Representatives.”

And I said, “Is that before or after my shift?”

🏗️ Who I Am

I drive a forklift.
I move crates.
I drink my coffee hot, black, and in silence.
I read the paper with grease on my fingers.

I pay attention.
Always have.

Because you don’t survive in a warehouse without learning to watch, listen, and lift what others drop.

Same goes for this country.

🛠️ What I Believe

I believe in:

Lunch breaks.

Second chances.

Calling bullshit when you see it.

And fixing it yourself if no one else will.

📋 Tony D.’s Simple Legislative Plan

The Honesty Act
You lie under oath? You’re gone.
No spin, no loopholes, no "misremembered."
Just gone. Back to the barrel.

The Working People’s Amendment
Every piece of federal legislation must be read by three people with no college degree.
If they don’t get it?
It don’t pass.

The Forklift Doctrine
If a law makes life harder for the people holding up the country?
We lift it out. Period.

Healthcare, Not Headaches
You shouldn’t have to choose between Advil and rent.
I’ve seen what happens when tough guys limp for weeks.
Enough.

The Sandwich Rule
Every member of Congress has to make their own damn sandwich.
If you’ve never buttered bread or packed lunch for your kids?
You don’t get to decide how hungry people eat.

🧠 My Qualifications?

I’ve negotiated overtime with guys named Spider and Boots.
I’ve broken up fights with two words and a raised eyebrow.
I’ve seen men cry because their daughters called them heroes.

I know what matters.

And that’s more than I can say for half the suits who ran this place before me.

🇺🇸 Final Thought

This ain't about left or right.
It’s about forward. Together.

I didn’t ask for this job.
But I’ll do it better than most,
because I know exactly who I’m doing it for.

The people who work.
The people who sweat.
The people who eat cold pizza in the parking lot at 2AM
and still show up the next day to carry the load.

I’m Tony D.
Congressman.
Union guy.
Forklift philosopher.

And if this is democracy by barrel—
then I’m rolling with it.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on June 11, 2025.
