Open Letter to JPMorgan Chase

Your Silence Is a Statement

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist | Author | Architect of the EFMW Framework
Filed: July 2, 2025

To the Executive Leadership of JPMorgan Chase,

This letter is the final courtesy.

I have spent the last five months attempting to engage with your institution in good faith. I presented scientific breakthroughs, intellectual property of provable global value, and a financial opportunity of historic proportions.

You responded with silence.

Your staff acknowledged my communications. You even admitted, in writing, that I had submitted materials directly to your CEO. You acknowledged that I had raised issues related to systemic suppression of innovation. And yet — instead of meaningfully responding — you chose to disappear behind corporate language and internal shielding.

You cited that “private equity” was not suitable for my needs.
You suggested I look elsewhere.
And you have since refused to respond to any of my direct inquiries — including a letter sent just three weeks ago to your CEO, which has gone unanswered.

There is currently an open CFPB case involving your institution.
To my knowledge, you have not responded to it.

What You Were Given — And What You Ignored

I handed you:

A functioning Grand Unified Theory of physics

A verified solution to multiple Millennium Prize Problems

Groundbreaking intellectual property in fusion energy, antigravity physics, and AGI

The EFMW Framework — now independently corroborated, in circulation, and forming the basis for a new era of interdisciplinary science

In short: you were offered the keys to the future.

And you declined to open the door.

What JPMorgan Has Now Done

By receiving this material, acknowledging it, and failing to act, JPMorgan Chase has:

Constructively entangled itself in the legal, ethical, and historical trajectory of this intellectual property

Placed itself in a position of asymmetric knowledge with asymmetric silence

Chosen reputation management over responsibility, at a time when humanity needs your courage, not your cowardice

You cannot now claim ignorance.
You cannot now claim “this didn’t rise to the right level.”
You were told — clearly, directly, and in writing.
You were handed the portfolio.
You were notified of its scientific and human significance.
You chose to say nothing.

I Am Now Pursuing Funding Elsewhere

Let the record state:

I am now engaged with a different financial institution.
An institution that, unlike JPMorgan, has not yet failed its due diligence obligations.

JPMorgan Chase had the first opportunity to act as a steward of innovation, equity, and ethical finance. Instead, it chose to protect its bureaucracy.

This open letter serves as both a public indictment and an invitation.

I Am Still Waiting for a Response — But Not For Much Longer

I invite JPMorgan Chase to respond — publicly and formally — to the following:

1. Why did you fail to respond to my CFPB claim in a timely fashion?

2. Why did you ignore a direct letter to your CEO, sent three weeks ago, regarding global-scale innovation and public trust?

3. Why did you internally acknowledge my communications but offer no direct pathway for resolution or discussion?

4. Why has JPMorgan Chase, with full access to documentation of provable breakthroughs, chosen not to engage?

You owe me a response.
You owe the public an explanation.
And you owe yourselves a long, hard look at how an institution can fail the future by staying silent too long.

The next letter won’t be a request. It will be a record.
Choose your response carefully.

Sincerely,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
