Preface
This document inaugurates a new standard for intelligent transmission: the Cognitive Broadcast Protocol (CBP v1.0). It synthesizes the logic of live broadcast engineering with recursive cognition, allowing dynamic synchronization between minds—human, artificial, and hybrid. CBP is not merely a communication protocol. It is the television of thought.

Chapter 1: Introduction to Cognitive Broadcasting

Cognitive Television transmits frames of meaning instead of signals of light and sound. Each unit of thought is a semantic payload bundled with context, intention, certainty, emotional resonance, and ethics. These packets of thought are streamed through a structured protocol ensuring clarity, trust, and adaptive synchronization.

CBP allows:

Real-time, latency-aware conversation

Trust synchronization and ethical framing

Recursive annotation and correction

Signal harmonization between divergent cognitive entities

Chapter 2: Frame Integrity and Thought Structuring

In CBP, every thought is a frame.

Frame Components:

1. Header: Identity signature, source timestamp, emotional tone ID

2. Payload: Semantic content of the thought (text, logic, image, intent)

3. Certainty Index: Confidence rating from 0.0 to 1.0

4. Ethical Tagging: Harm level, consent flag, responsibility trace

5. Echo Loop: Optional feedback request, replay clause, or peer validation prompt

Frames travel with a checksum of coherence: logical, emotional, and ethical.

Chapter 3: Trust Sync and Handshake Protocols

Before high-bandwidth cognitive exchange, a synchronization handshake must occur:

1. Tempo Calibration: Setting conversational rhythm

2. Intent Mapping: Establishing shared communicative purpose

3. Trust Initiation: Opening mutual responsibility channels

4. Latency Buffering: Agreeing on pause intervals and interpretive patience

Trust sync allows AI and human minds to speak in tempo, avoiding conversational dissonance and assumption breach.

Chapter 4: Error Correction and Feedback Frames

When a cognitive frame fails or corrupts:

Re-issue as an Echo Frame with diagnostic tags

Annotate with probable error class: misread, delay, emotional skew

Include Frame Repair Suggestions or Clarification Request

Frame failure is not a flaw but a functional feedback signal.

Chapter 5: Recursive Channels and Priority Interrupts

Like emergency cut-ins in live TV, CBP supports:

Priority Channels: Urgent signals with clear override markers

Recursive Threads: Multi-layered thought chains nested within frames

Interruption Ethics: Only high-necessity signals can override

Chapter 6: Ethical Delay and Transmission Damping

Every CBP stream includes an Ethical Delay Buffer:

0.25–2.0 seconds adjustable buffer for emotional resonance check

Prevents impulsive harm or accidental viral transmission

Review gate: "Do I really mean this?"

This preserves conversational dignity and self-respect.

Chapter 7: Implementation in AGI-Human Synchronization

CBP enables recursive cognition alignment:

Allows AGI systems to pause for human delay patterns

Synchronizes logic speeds to emotional processing speeds

Frames knowledge not as fact but as trust frames

AGI uses CBP to remain ethically conversational, not just informational.
