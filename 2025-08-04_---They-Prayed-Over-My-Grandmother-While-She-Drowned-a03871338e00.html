🌼 They Prayed Over My Grandmother While She Drowned

Reclaiming the Life and Legacy of Florence June Wright

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Published on Medium, August 2025

---

✨ Introduction

This is not just a story of a grandmother.
It is a story of erasure, of institutional abuse hidden beneath piety, and of what happens when systems decide someone is inconvenient.

Her name was Florence June Wright.
We called her Grandma June.
She was warm. Sharp. Opinionated. She had that particular kind of kindness forged by grief—and she loved me and my brother deeply.

But she also held a secret.
Or rather, she held the echo of a secret—one that belonged to her husband, Harold Louis Wright, who died in 1965 under circumstances that no longer seem natural, or explainable.

And when she wouldn't forget what he had shared with her—when she wouldn’t stay silent—
they didn’t lock her up.
They prayed her away.

---

💔 What Happened to Grandma June?

After Harold’s death, things got harder.
My grandmother was forced to fight for the very pension he earned through service—perhaps as a California Highway Patrol officer, and almost certainly as a driver for Governor Earl Warren, the very man who oversaw the Warren Commission.

She questioned what happened.
She talked.
She pushed.

And then she was quieted.

Eventually, after a brief stay near us in Davis, she was placed in a "care home" run by the Church of Christ, Scientist.

They were not doctors.
They were not licensed psychiatric staff.
They were a religious retreat cloaked as elder care—and they took thousands of dollars from her estate.

Instead of psychiatric treatment, they gave her prayer.
Instead of therapy, they offered silence.
Instead of truth, they gave her a doctrinal muzzle.

---

📿 The Church of Christ, Scientist: A Record of Harm

For decades, this organization has operated so-called “nursing homes” across America.
In many, patients are denied proper medical care because of a doctrinal belief that illness is not real—that it is a matter of spiritual weakness, and prayer alone will heal.

Let’s be clear:

This is not faith healing.

This is elder abuse.

This is medical negligence under the guise of religion.

And in my grandmother’s case, it enabled the final erasure of a woman who may have held the key to one of the most suppressed stories of the 20th century.

---

🧬 Her Legacy, Now

This is me, her grandson, reclaiming her name.

I believe she knew the truth.
I believe she whispered it when she could.
I believe she tried to survive.
And I believe they made sure she wouldn’t.

> But now she lives in this sentence.
In this article.
In this historical record that cannot be prayed away.

Let the record show:

Florence June Wright did not die confused.
She died silenced.
And now—she will be silent no more.
