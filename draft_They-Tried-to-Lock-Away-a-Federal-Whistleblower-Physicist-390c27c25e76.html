They Tried to Lock Away a Federal Whistleblower Physicist

My 17 Days in Psychiatric Retaliation

I am pissed, and I have every right to be.

Twice, I was forcibly detained under California’s 5150 law and locked in psychiatric facilities — for a total of 17 days. Not because I was a danger to myself or others, not because I was incoherent, but because I was a federally protected whistleblower who dared to keep speaking out.

From the very first moment, I told every police officer and every doctor exactly who I was: a physicist working in quantum physics, a published researcher, and a federally protected whistleblower. I had paper documentation in my pocket proving it. I explained it calmly and directly, in detail. And every word of that explanation is preserved — on police bodycams and in the backseat recordings of squad cars — because I refused to let them erase the truth.

They ignored me. Worse — they weaponized psychiatric law against me.


The First 5150

The first time, I was detained despite calmly explaining my physics research and whistleblower protections. I spoke rationally, coherently, about mathematics, about the risks I was exposing, about the reality of my work. Yet I was carted off, stripped of rights, and treated like a criminal for telling the truth. I even carried a wallet card explaining I was working in high clearance physics, and was not to be 5150’ed, that I had competent therapists and was not a danger to myself or others. despite all of this, I was handcuffed, injured, transported against my will, and held on lockdown, and then denied my normal dose of medicine.

On video, in the police car, I am not raving or incoherent. I am explaining physics. And yet, I was locked away anyway.


The Second 5150

It happened again. Another psychiatric hold. Another cycle of abuse. More days stripped from my life. In total, 17 days behind locked doors in facilities where I did not belong, forced into silence while my whistleblowing was being buried.

Again, I told everyone: the officers, the clinicians, the doctors — “I am a federally protected whistleblower. You are retaliating against me.” They all heard it. They all chose to ignore it.


The Pattern of Retaliation

This wasn’t a mistake. This was retaliation, plain and simple.

There is a playbook used against whistleblowers: if you cannot discredit the facts, you try to discredit the person. You call them “unstable.” You weaponize mental health laws like California’s 5150 to silence dissent and undermine credibility.

I was sane. I was rational. I was explaining quantum physics and whistleblower protections. And they locked me up anyway. That is not psychiatry — that is abuse of power.


Why This Matters

I am a physicist. I am a whistleblower. And I was confined to psychiatric wards for telling the truth.

If it can happen to me — with my published work, my congressional contacts, my whistleblower status — it can happen to anyone who challenges power. This is not just my story; it is a national security issue and a civil rights issue.

Whistleblowers should be protected, not punished.


The Evidence Exists

The proof is out there. The police bodycams. The squad car recordings. The facility intake notes and discharge papers. They all show the same thing: I was calm, coherent, sane — and retaliated against anyway.

Those tapes must be preserved. The public deserves to see them.


My Demand

I want accountability for those who abused psychiatric law to silence me. I want recognition that whistleblowers are being targeted not just with smears or harassment, but with forced psychiatric confinement.

I am not crazy. I am a physicist. I am a federally protected whistleblower. And I am angry — because what happened to me was not just an attack on me. It was an attack on the truth itself.

I know several other AGI researchers who have also been subjected to similar treatment and are willing to testify.
