🎪 Haven’t We Had Enough of This Clown?

An Open Letter from the American Big Top, Dictated by the Spirit of P.T. Barnum
 By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
 June 2025

Ladies and Gentlemen,
 and those wisely refusing to buy tickets,

I, Phineas Taylor Barnum — late of Earth, early of spectacle — rise from the dusty rafters of Eternity to address a concern so glaring, so giddy, so grotesquely overplayed that even the strongmen are wincing and the elephants have rolled their eyes.

He’s still at it.

The Clown.

You know the one.
 Bouffant of haywire hue, face painted with the weary resignation of a man who believes the mirror owes him applause.
 A costume stitched from the fraying flags of forgotten rallies and fraudulent fortunes.
 A performance so repetitive, even the bearded lady walked out.

🤡 He Was Never a Genius — Just a Loud Juggler with One Ball

There was a time, dear public, when America adored a showman.
 I knew the hunger well.

But even I, ringmaster of Humbug, could spot the difference between entertainment and entrancement.
 Between wonder and weaponized nonsense.
 Between civic flair and dangerous farce.

This man — this carnival barker with nuclear launch codes — has turned the great tent of democracy into a dunk tank for truth.

And we, gawking rubes, are offered the choice:
 Pay again? Or tip the whole thing over?

🎟️ The Ticket You Keep Buying Is Rigged

You see, there’s a secret every true showman knows:

The trick only works as long as the crowd believes the cage is real.

And oh, how he sold you the cage:

  • Borders drawn in Sharpie.
  • Enemies invented with flair.
  • Truth sawed in half nightly on television.

But there’s nothing left under the greasepaint now.
 The illusion collapsed when the spotlight stayed on too long.

Even the clowns behind the clown are tired of holding the curtain.

🎪 I Knew How to Leave the Stage

When the applause grew thin in my own day, I bowed.
 Retired the elephant. Let the next act breathe.

But this one?
 He wants the tent to burn down with him still center ring.
 He feeds on gasps. On rage. On hypnotized loyalty.

A man who never built a tent, only ripped the ropes from others’.

🛑 So I Say: No More Popcorn for the Pyromaniac

Enough.

America’s soul was never meant to be held hostage by a man who thinks a podium is a pedestal.

We deserve a new show. One without cages. One with light and air.
 One where laughter comes with joy, not at the expense of the truth.

🎤 Final Act

He will return to the stage again.
 He will say you missed him.
 He will offer another encore of grievance, painted in stripes and spittle.

But when the music swells and he enters once more, face lacquered in old mascara and new malice…

Don’t cheer. Don’t boo. Don’t even look.

Just leave the tent.

And build something worth returning to.

Yours in civic disbelief and Barnumesque clarity,
 P.T. Barnum (by the hand of Matthew Chenoweth Wright)
 June 2025
