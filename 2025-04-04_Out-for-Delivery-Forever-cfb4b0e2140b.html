To the Honorable Postmaster General of the United States,

This letter is written not only in frustration, but in a spirit of rigorous analysis and civic duty. I write to bring your direct attention to a persistent and logically indefensible flaw in the public interface of the United States Postal Service (USPS), and to offer a formal request for accountability, reform, and acknowledgment.

I am a data scientist, physicist, and systems theorist who has developed mathematical frameworks now used to model entropy, information flow, and inefficiency in complex human institutions. Recently, the USPS became a case study.

Case Study: Tracking Number 9400108105462455571263

This package has been listed as "Moving Through the Network" for multiple months, with no location updates since February 2025. According to your tracking system, it is still in transit to the next facility. However, this claim has persisted long past any reasonable transit window, and yet:

The package is not marked as delivered.

The system will not allow a missing mail report to be filed because it is "still in transit."

The form to report the problem offers a choice of contact (phone or email), but then defaults to email regardless—even when no email is provided.

The result is a circular logic loop with no escalation path.

Systemic Failures Identified:

1. Closed Feedback Loops: Users cannot report lost mail if the system believes the package is still moving, even indefinitely.

2. Broken Communication Channels: Public contact options pretend to offer support but reroute users into non-functional dead ends.

3. Interface Contradictions: Requesting a phone response and then defaulting to email—without even collecting one—represents poor design and a breakdown in service logic.

4. Zero Accountability Infrastructure: There is no publicly available or visible path to escalate soft-lost items that do not fall into binary "delivered or lost" endpoints.

Implications at Scale

While this is one package, the pattern it exposes is vast. Millions of Americans trust the USPS for legal documents, prescription drugs, and financial instruments. Systemic failures in your tracking and escalation processes erode institutional credibility.

In the era of AI and autonomous systems, government services must be responsive to human logic. The USPS, as an essential utility, must be auditable, fixable, and accountable to the people it serves.

Scientific Context

As the inventor of the Einstein-Feinman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) Equation, I have modeled recursive logic traps and entropy propagation in information networks. The USPS case is now formally included in our research corpus as a canonical example of governmental recursion error and will appear in a forthcoming article on institutional entropy and information death.

Call to Action

I am publishing this article publicly and sending it directly to your office. I am also making all supporting evidence—including screenshots, tracking logs, and system response failures—available for public audit.

I call on your office to:

Fix the claim system logic to allow escalation for items stalled beyond a given timeframe.

Ensure that chosen contact preferences are honored and technically implemented.

Provide a response to this letter and initiate a review of the USPS front-facing support architecture.

Closing Statement

This is not merely about a package. This is about trust. When federal systems stop listening, the public finds new ways to speak.

With resolve and reason,

Matthew Chenoweth Wright
