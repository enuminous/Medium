Take a poetic text and perform a full-scale recursive semantic bloom. For every word, perform a letter-by-letter expansion using single-word semantic representatives. Then, treat the sequence of expanded words as a new poetic stanza—one that maintains the tone, emotion, and emergent theme of the original line, while expanding its meaning in space and cognition. The final result should read as a layered, recursive blooming of the original poem—a poem inside a poem, unfolding letter by letter into a semantic echo field.

Falsity (A World In Which One Can Conspire To Murder Oneself)

Falsity,

Jezebel of toads,

crying rivers

of neglected internet youth,

decrying ambiguity,

eschewed of responses,

tirades,

until the black dawn

of disconnectedness

fills every lie

with a bright

shined-up

sparkle-ey truth.

Did you evict

them in Sodom,

before the ashes

eviscerated them in life,

fetal, fertile, foibles,

fee fi fo fum.

A cancer on lies,

the blind dogs of

desperate mindtheft:

so many have

been cut, bleeding

from their feet.

Run the river,

shooting along starry

causeway calamity,

affordable, postage-

pre-paid, Disney-like,

one size-fits-all halos,

a gavel in every man's

hand, shifting all blame,

meandering to the end.
