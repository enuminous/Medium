1. Introduction

The Butter-Toast Cat Paradox suggests that if a piece of buttered toast (which “always lands butter-side down”) is strapped to a cat’s back (which “always lands on its feet”), the system will hover and begin spinning rather than resolving to a single outcome.

While often treated as a humorous paradox, we propose that this system can be analyzed rigorously under the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) framework, which governs emergent motion, recursive instability, and rotational phase equilibria.
