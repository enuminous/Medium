When a Nation Seals Itself In and Turns Its Citizens into Human Shields

*By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Physicist, Federal Whistleblower, and Creator of EFMW

In a move that should shake the conscience of the world, the Israeli government has effectively sealed its borders, preventing its own citizens from leaving the country amid escalating warfare with Iran.

This is not speculation. This is confirmed by multiple credible news outlets: airspace is shut, airports are grounded, and stay-of-exit orders — once reserved for debtors or military evaders — are now being applied to the entire civilian population. The state is no longer just conscripting soldiers. It is drafting the population’s physical presence as a tool of war.

Let us be clear about what this means:

The Israeli government is actively restricting the freedom of its people to flee a warzone.

Whether through cynical calculation or desperate reflex, this act transforms civilians into strategic assets — not by giving them weapons, but by denying them movement. They are made immobile, vulnerable, and symbolic — a population no longer served by its government, but held by it.

Human Shields by Policy

It may not be stated explicitly. It never is.

But what is a population trapped between incoming missiles and outgoing strikes?
 What are civilians, confined by courts and blocked at borders, if not unarmed collateral held in place?

These are not hypothetical concerns. Families, children, dissidents, and ordinary citizens who do not support Benjamin Netanyahu’s government — including those who oppose this war entirely — now find themselves unable to escape it.

This is not defense.
 This is containment of the innocent.

A Global Call to Conscience

This is not just a military or diplomatic issue. This is a human rights emergency.

Israel has every right to defend itself. But no state has the right to confiscate the agency of its own people, especially in a moment of such grave peril.

We must speak out for the Israeli civilians who oppose this war, who never voted for it, who want no part in this spiral of mutual destruction.

The right to flee a warzone is not conditional on loyalty. It is not determined by geography. It is not up to the whims of national security councils.

It is a fundamental human right.
