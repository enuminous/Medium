A Broken Clock and a Clear Truth

Why Newt Gingrich Is Right About Putin, Ukraine, and America’s Role in the Fight for Democracy

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist · Author · Whistleblower

We live in an era so upside-down that when Newt Gingrich—yes, Newt Gingrich—makes a morally accurate and strategically sound point about Ukraine, it’s easy to feel whiplash. But that doesn’t make him wrong.

Sometimes, the broken clock chimes at the right hour.

And today, the time is urgent.

What Newt Said

“Putin is making it very clear that any truce in Ukraine will come only when he is defeated. We talk, he kills Ukrainians. He has openly said he wants to destroy Ukraine and that is his war aim. A combined Ukrainian-US-European effort will be needed to defeat him badly enough that he wants a ceasefire.”

He goes on to call for:

No American boots on the ground

Massive support in advanced weapons

Permission for Ukraine to strike military targets inside Russia

Vastly expanded sanctions

This is not some neocon fever dream. This is reality. And it is a reality that many Americans—particularly those poisoned by the disinformation echo chamber created by Russian psyops and MAGA media—have failed to understand.

Ukrainians have shown the world what courage looks like. They’ve fought with ingenuity, ferocity, and a moral clarity that’s been sorely lacking in the West. They’ve built battlefield drones out of duct tape and ambition. They’ve reclaimed land through sheer determination. They’ve held the line against the second-largest army on Earth.

And they’ve done it not with American boots on the ground—but with American resolve.

What they need now is more of that resolve:

Logistics

Munitions

Training

Intelligence cooperation

Clear rules of engagement that allow them to hit the war machine where it lives: inside Russia

This isn’t about escalation. It’s about survival—for them and for democracy itself.

America doesn’t need soldiers in Kyiv.
America needs citizens in Kansas, California, and Kentucky who understand what’s at stake.
We need voters, writers, coders, teachers, and journalists who will:

Call out Russian propaganda, even when it’s repackaged by American outlets

Resist the siren song of isolationism

Understand that appeasement never leads to peace

Recognize that Trump’s admiration for Putin is not just disturbing, but dangerous

This isn’t just a foreign war.
This is a war of narratives, a war of memory, a war of moral clarity—and it’s happening in our living rooms, our social media feeds, and our voting booths.

Let’s say the quiet part loud:
Donald Trump has repeatedly done Vladimir Putin’s bidding.

He tried to extort Zelenskyy.
He undermined NATO.
He has praised Putin’s strength while attacking American intelligence agencies.
He has flooded the GOP with operatives who parrot Kremlin lines about “endless wars” and “protecting Russian sovereignty.”

Trumpism and Putinism are ideological siblings. One hides behind nationalism, the other behind imperialism, but both are built on authoritarianism, cult personality, and contempt for democratic process.

Ukraine is not losing.
In fact, if the West holds strong, Ukraine can decisively defeat Putin’s aggression.
Not by occupying Moscow.
Not by regime change.
But by doing what America does best when it remembers who it is:

Out-manufacturing tyranny

Out-thinking militarism

Out-supplying corruption

Outlasting fascism with endurance, vision, and a free press

Let’s not waste time arguing over who said the truth.
Let’s act on it—because even a broken clock can ring the bell of justice.

Newt Gingrich is right.
Putin only stops when he is stopped.

And we know exactly how to do it.
