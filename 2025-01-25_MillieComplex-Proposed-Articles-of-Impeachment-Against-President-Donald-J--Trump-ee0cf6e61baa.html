Resolution

Impeaching Donald J. Trump, President of the United States, for high crimes and misdemeanors.

Article I: Abuse of Power in the Dismissal of Inspectors General

Action: President Trump dismissed 17 independent inspectors general from various federal agencies without providing the detailed reasons required by law.

Specific Law Violated:

  • Inspector General Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. App. § 3(b))
  • U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 3 (“Take Care Clause”)

Evidence:

  • On January 24, 2025, President Trump dismissed inspectors general without prior consultation or explanation to Congress, as required by statute. This included the Department of Defense Inspector General, whose investigations into military procurement irregularities were abruptly halted.
  • Public statements by lawmakers, including Senator Elizabeth Warren, called these actions “a purge designed to eliminate independent oversight.”

Charge: President Trump’s actions undermined the independence of federal oversight, eroded checks and balances, and violated statutory requirements, constituting an abuse of power.

Article II: Violation of Reproductive Rights Protections

Action: President Trump signed executive orders reinstating the Mexico City policy and limiting federal funds for abortion services, in violation of established protections.

Specific Law Violated:

  • Foreign Assistance Act of 1961 (22 U.S.C. § 2151b(f))
  • U.S. Constitution, Amendments IX and XIV (Due Process Clause)

Evidence:

  • The Mexico City policy, reinstated on January 22, 2025, restricts global health organizations from using U.S. funds if they provide abortion services or counseling, even with non-U.S. funds.
  • Advocacy groups such as Planned Parenthood and Amnesty International issued statements condemning these actions as violations of international human rights norms.

Charge: These actions disregarded constitutional protections and international human rights obligations, demonstrating reckless governance.

Article III: Discrimination Against Transgender Individuals

Action: Issuance of an executive order defining “male and female” as binary categories for federal policy purposes.

Specific Law Violated:

  • Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. § 2000e)
  • Equal Protection Clause of the 14th Amendment

Evidence:

  • The executive order, issued on January 23, 2025, reversed prior federal guidance allowing transgender individuals access to appropriate facilities and identification documents.
  • The American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) labeled this order as “blatantly unconstitutional and harmful to millions.”

Charge: This discriminatory policy violates federal anti-discrimination laws and the constitutional guarantee of equal protection under the law.

Article IV: Dissemination of False and Misleading Claims

Action: Repeated public dissemination of false claims regarding election results, immigration policies, and the January 6 insurrection.

Specific Law Violated:

  • 18 U.S.C. § 1001 (False Statements)
  • U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 3 (“Take Care Clause”)

Evidence:

  • In his January 21, 2025, address, President Trump falsely claimed victory in the 2024 election by “the largest margin in history,” a claim debunked by the Federal Election Commission.
  • He repeated misinformation about immigration policies, inaccurately stating that “over 5 million illegal immigrants crossed the border in January alone.”

Charge: These actions constitute a dereliction of duty and a breach of the public trust by spreading misinformation.

Article V: Withdrawal from the Paris Climate Agreement

Action: President Trump withdrew the United States from the Paris Climate Agreement, reversing prior commitments to combat climate change.

Specific Law Violated:

  • U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 3 (“Take Care Clause”)
  • Global Climate Protection Act of 1987 (Title X of the Foreign Relations Authorization Act)

Evidence:

  • Official announcements on January 20, 2025, declared the withdrawal, citing economic reasons without acknowledging scientific consensus on climate change.
  • The European Union and United Nations issued condemnatory statements highlighting the agreement’s importance for global cooperation.

Charge: This withdrawal undermined global cooperative efforts to address climate change, damaging U.S. credibility and interests.

Article VI: Pardoning of January 6 Rioters

Action: Issued pardons for individuals involved in the January 6 Capitol attack, including members of extremist groups.

Specific Law Violated:

  • 18 U.S.C. § 2383 (Rebellion or Insurrection)
  • U.S. Constitution, Article II, Section 3 (“Take Care Clause”)

Evidence:

  • On January 22, 2025, President Trump pardoned 1,500 individuals, including leaders of the Proud Boys and Oath Keepers convicted of seditious conspiracy.
  • Bipartisan condemnation arose, with Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell stating, “These pardons embolden further insurrection.”

Charge: These pardons undermine accountability for acts of insurrection and constitute an abuse of the pardon power.

Article VII: Politicization of the Civil Service

Action: Dismissed over 1,000 federal employees perceived as disloyal to the administration.

Specific Law Violated:

  • Civil Service Reform Act of 1978 (5 U.S.C. § 2302)

Evidence:

  • Reports from federal agencies indicate mass dismissals on January 25, 2025, targeting individuals involved in oversight and whistleblowing activities.
  • The Office of Special Counsel described these actions as “the most egregious attack on civil service protections in decades.”

Charge: These actions undermine the integrity of the civil service and erode public trust in federal institutions.

Article VIII: Conflicts of Interest and Ethical Violations

Action: Engaged in personal business ventures and financial dealings that intersected with official duties.

Specific Law Violated:

  • 5 U.S.C. § 7353 (Gifts to Federal Employees)
  • U.S. Constitution, Article I, Section 9 (Emoluments Clause)

Evidence:

  • Investigative reports revealed ongoing ties to Trump Organization branding deals, including contracts with foreign entities during his presidency.
  • Ethics watchdog Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington (CREW) documented 56 instances of potential emoluments violations in January 2025 alone.

Charge: These conflicts of interest compromised the impartiality of the presidency and violated constitutional and ethical standards.

Article IX: Retaliation Against Political Opponents and Media

Action: Revoked security clearances and initiated investigations targeting political adversaries and members of the media.

Specific Law Violated:

  • 18 U.S.C. § 242 (Deprivation of Rights Under Color of Law)
  • U.S. Constitution, First Amendment (Freedom of Speech)

Evidence:

  • On January 24, 2025, security clearances were revoked for 12 former officials, including John Brennan and James Clapper, without credible justification.
  • Journalists from major outlets faced IRS audits days after publishing investigative reports critical of the administration.

Charge: These retaliatory actions violate constitutional protections and suppress dissent, endangering democratic norms.

Article X: Revocation of Security Protections

Action: Revoked security protections for individuals facing credible threats, including former officials.

Specific Law Violated:

  • 18 U.S.C. § 1114 (Protection of Officers and Employees)

Evidence:

  • Protections for former officials such as Dr. Anthony Fauci were revoked on January 20, 2025, despite documented threats to their safety.
  • The Secret Service union expressed concern, citing violations of duty to protect individuals under threat.

Charge: This decision jeopardized the safety of individuals, constituting a failure to ensure their protection.

Resolution

Resolved, That Donald J. Trump, President of the United States, is impeached for high crimes and misdemeanors, and that the following Articles of Impeachment be exhibited to the Senate:

The House of Representatives charges that President Trump has committed acts that warrant removal from office and disqualification to hold and enjoy any office of honor, trust, or profit under the United States.
