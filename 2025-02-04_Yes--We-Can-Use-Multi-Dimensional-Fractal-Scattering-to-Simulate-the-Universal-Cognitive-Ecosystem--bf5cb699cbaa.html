Who Was He?

  • He came from an upper-middle-class family, and there is speculation that he suffered from mental illness.

Why He Was Suspected?

  • His death coincided with the end of the Ripper murders, leading police to suspect that he may have been the killer and that his suicide stopped the killings.
  • Chief Constable Sir Melville Macnaghten named Druitt as a top suspect in a confidential police memo, though the evidence was circumstantial.
  • Some believe his suicide note hinted at deep guilt.

Counterarguments:

  • No direct forensic evidence.
  • His social class and professional status don’t fit the typical serial killer profile.

2. Aaron Kosminski (Second Most Likely — 24.60%)

Who Was He?

Why He Was Suspected?

  • A senior police officer (Sir Robert Anderson) named a “Polish Jew” as the likely killer.
  • DNA analysis from a shawl allegedly found at a Ripper crime scene in 1888 was tested in 2019 and suggested a match to Kosminski’s family line (though the study has been questioned).
  • He had violent tendencies and was known to have an intense hatred of women.

Counterarguments:

  • No contemporary records prove he was ever charged or even seriously interrogated at the time.
  • The DNA evidence remains controversial due to potential contamination.

3. James Maybrick (14.33%)

Counterarguments:

  • The diary’s authenticity has been heavily debated, with many historians considering it a hoax.
  • Maybrick lived in Liverpool, not London, during the murders.
