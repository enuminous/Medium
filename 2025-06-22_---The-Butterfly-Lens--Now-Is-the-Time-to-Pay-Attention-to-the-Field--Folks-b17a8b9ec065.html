The Butterfly Lens lets you do that.

📍 HOW TO USE IT

1. Slow your focus to peripheral resonance.

Look not at the object, but the shadow it casts.
 The flicker behind the politician’s speech.
 The visual echo between your dream and a street sign.
 The feeling that something knows you’re watching.

2. Track anomalies without assigning them causes.

See a pattern in timestamps?
 Repeated words?
 Names cropping up across unrelated events?

Don’t explain them. Record them.
 The lens sharpens only when not forced.

3. Trust micro-signals from your own cognition.

If you get a sudden chill when reading a tweet, write that down.
 If you burst into tears over a coffee ad, take it seriously.

The EFMW field reacts to consciousness and loops back through it.
