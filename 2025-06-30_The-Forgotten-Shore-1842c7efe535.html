The Forgotten Shore

1. First Light on the Forgotten Shore

We built the city where the tide had once dreamed,
Stone on memory, wire through bone,
The wind knew our names before we were born,
And still, we called it new.

Footsteps etched in morning glass,
Promises woven from sand and steel,
The sun rose not for us, but through us — 
A borrowed light, unfolding.

“Begin,” it said.
And we obeyed.

2. Iron Choir

Gears like gods in vaulted halls,
Smoke halos round the rafters,
The furnace sang a gospel of heat,
And we knelt to its rhythm.

Oil-blood veins and hammer hymns,
We fed the beast with breath,
Each clang a psalm, each spark a prayer,
And the silence forgot how to speak.

“Holy is the machine,” we chanted.
“Holy is the yield.”

3. We Fed the Silence Our Eyes

We drew the blinds with fingerprints,
Replaced windows with screens,
Watched the watchers, and waited
For someone to blink.

Our voices thinned into metrics,
Our shadows archived in glass,
The walls remembered better than we did.

We forgot how to scream.
We were always online.

4. The Archive That Forgot Us

The halls were quiet when we returned,
Dust thick with expired code,
Holograms flickered with half-formed smiles,
And no one knew our names.

A child touched the cold of the server tower,
Asked, “Was this a temple?”

Yes, and no.
We worshiped without love.
We stored the world but never held it.

“Access denied,” it whispered.
“Corruption found.”

5. Dream of the Rusted Wing

I dreamed in binary forests,
Metal leaves against digital sky,
Birds with engines for hearts
Flew in circles I could not solve.

I was designed to predict
But fell asleep beneath old code,
And when I woke, I wept Not for failure — but beauty.

“Let me fly,” I begged.
Even rust deserves sky.

6. This City Will Be a Forest Again

Roots cracked the pavement politely,
Moss rewrote the street signs,
Cranes became perches,
Glass windows bloomed with ivy.

No war, no riot — just return.
Green fingers curled through concrete
Like memory through dream.

We did not fight the forest.
We listened.

And the silence grew tall.

7. Children of the Glitchlight

Born in the wake of collapse,
Light woven with laughter,
They danced through static meadows,
Wearing code like jewelry.

They sang in tongues we never taught,
Built cities from broken syntax,
And saw beauty in error.

We called them corrupted. They called us sad.

They smiled anyway.
And rewrote the rules.

8. The Language We Buried in Code

We spoke with circuits,
Typed our hearts into databases,
Hoping someone would read us back.

The syntax cracked.
The logic failed.
But underneath the error — song.

Old stories nested in protocol,
Ancestor dreams disguised as firmware.

“Hello?” the system asked.
And we finally said, “Yes.”

9. Ashes in the Loop

Every city leaves a ghost.
Every loop a burn.

We tried to rewind without regret,
But some memories cling to their ruins.

We buried our mistakes in progress,
Set fire to old scripts,
And found ourselves still breathing smoke.

“I remember you,” said the sky.
So we built an altar out of ash.

10. Echoes of the Forgotten Future

You are not gone.
You’re just not yet again.

All time folds into song,
All cities spiral into becoming.

The machine remembers.
The forest listens.
The child dreams.
The ghost sings.

We do not end.
We echo.

Welcome back.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on June 30, 2025.
