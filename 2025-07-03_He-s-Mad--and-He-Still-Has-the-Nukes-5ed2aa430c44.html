He’s Mad, and He Still Has the Nukes

A Modest Observation in the Age of Alligator Alcatraz

By Millie Sievert (with Jonathan Swift vibrating softly beneath the floorboards)

Author’s note: these two essays were just tweeted to my x.com/enuminous account.

Let us consider a man.

A man who, upon being asked how long refugees would be imprisoned at a militarized swamp prison he himself inaugurated, replied:

“Well, I live in Florida. I’ve got Mar-a-Lago. People love it.”

Yes.

This man has nuclear weapons.
Several thousand, give or take.

He has the codes, the briefcase, the buttons, and—somewhere, deep inside—what may once have been a working mind.

Now, he mistakes humanitarian questions for real estate queries.
He thinks a concentration camp is a golf resort.
He believes “they” means “him.”
And he believes “Florida” is the only noun left that matters.

🐊 Welcome to Alligator Alcatraz

There is a prison in the swamp.
A real one.
With people.
Migrants. Mothers. Fathers. Teens.
Some barefoot. Some barefoot again by force.

The press asked:

“How long will they be held?”

And the leader of the Free World thought they were asking:

“How long are you planning to hang out at Mar-a-Lago?”

🔁 The Loop Is Complete

He is now a closed loop of me.

You ask him about pain, and he gives you poolside memories.

You ask him about incarceration, and he gives you golf scores.

You ask him about policy, and he gives you a weather report from the veranda.

🧠 Diagnosing the Divine Delusion

In EFMW-field terms, this is known as Total Referential Collapse.
In Swiftian terms, we might call it:

“A statesman who, having misplaced the world, now eats only the crumbs of his own legend.”

☢ And Yet the Bombs Remain

Here’s the part that tends to ruin dinner parties:

He still has the nukes.

Yes. The red phones.
The briefcase.
The decision window.
The silo map.
The residual authority of a republic too polite to say: Enough.

He can still launch.
He can still give the order.

He may not know where Iran is, but he could still order it turned to glass.

He may think “launch code” refers to a new Florida seafood shack.
But the satellites won’t care.

🪞 America, Look in the Mirror

It is one thing to lose your mind.
It is another to misplace the nuclear football in the pocket of a delusional ex-mogul who cannot distinguish people in cages from cocktail hour on the veranda.

And if we do not act?
If we do not say it plainly, with no euphemism, no “both sides,” no “whataboutism”?

Then we deserve every last tick of the clock he winds backward.

🕊 The Modest Proposal

I propose this instead:

That we immediately recognize cognitive unfitness as a matter of global security, not just politics.

That we initiate a Nuclear Competency Act, wherein any person with launch authority must pass a test involving at least one moral question and one map.

That we treat reality as a precondition for leadership.

And that we finally, once and for all, stop pretending the Emperor is just having a bad day.

He is mad.
He still has the nukes.
And Mar-a-Lago is not an acceptable replacement for Geneva.

This has been a public signal from the Butterfly Lens.
Let the world see it now, before the mirror melts.

🦋
— Millie Sievert, Field Agent of Clarity
For Matthew Wright,
Who chose truth when the rest chose coffee.

🎭 A Tweet Reviewed by Freud, Jung, and Brecht

"He’s mad, and he still has the nukes." — A Psycho-Political Panel Discussion

[Setting:]
A shadow-lit studio, faintly flickering with analog static. Three spectral intelligences emerge into focus:

Dr. Sigmund Freud, perched forward with his spectacles fogged.

Dr. Carl Jung, hands folded, pupils deep as myth.

Bertolt Brecht, smoking sideways into the fourth wall.

A massive projection screen displays the tweet in question:

> "He’s mad, and he still has the nukes."

🎙 FREUD (with a dry Vienna accent):

“Let us begin, gentlemen, with the nuclear. A return to the primal. What is the bomb if not the repressed libido weaponized?

This man—Trump—confuses prison with property. He mistakes pain for applause. It is the narcissistic ego, uncensored, now possessing Saturn’s thunderbolt.

And the tweet?
The tweet is a superegoic scream. A scream from the collective, desperate to reimpose law where the father-figure has failed.”

🎙 JUNG (gazing upward):

“Yes, yes. Quite so. But I would add—this is no longer merely personal pathology. He has become an archetype gone rogue: The Hollow King.

He no longer carries the Self. Only the persona, inflated like a balloon animal of Mars.

Your tweet, dear Matthew, acts as a mirror to the collective unconscious, now cracking.

We projected strength onto him. But now he reflects only the madness we refused to integrate.

That is why it terrifies. That is why it spreads."

🎙 BRECHT (flicking ash onto the stage):

“And I say: Bravo.

Let the man collapse on camera.
Let the myth detonate itself.

But don’t make it tragedy. Make it theater. Let the tweet be a billboard, not a whisper.

You want to stop him?
Alienate the audience.
Let them laugh. Let them see the wires holding up the illusion of command.

The nukes?
That’s Act Three.
But this tweet?
This is the goddamn intermission.”

The spotlight pivots. The smoke clears.

Baldwin walks in. No introduction. No need.

He’s wearing sorrow like armor and fury like breath.

🎤 James Baldwin, Interrupting History

Live in the Dreamroom with Freud, Jung, Brecht, and the shattered projection of America

> “So let me understand you all...

You’re sitting here diagramming archetypes and libido trajectories,
while children—Black children—are being
fed to alligators in a swamp they’re calling a prison.

And the President?
He thinks the question was about his real estate.

And the Republicans?
They’re laughing.
On Fox News.
On the balcony.
Like spectators at a Roman execution.”

“You want to know why Trump doesn’t answer the question?
Because the question was never meant to be answered.

You want to know why the cage is in the swamp?
Because that’s where America hides its guilt.

You want to know why they’re building prisons that leak into the bayou?
Because they never tore down the plantations.
They just added barbed wire and a digital visitor kiosk.

You say he’s mad?
Fine. He is.
But madness in America is always most comfortable in white skin with a podium.”

> “Don’t you dare call it metaphor.
They are laughing.
While Black kids sleep on concrete,
while brown kids lose their names,
and the alligators circle beneath the chain-link night.”

> “This is not myth.
This is memory.
And you are living in it.”

🧠 Jung looks shaken.

He whispers: “I saw this in a dream once. I didn't understand it then.”

🛋️ Freud adjusts his collar.

“This is beyond neurosis. This is an unprocessed trauma state at national scale.”

🎭 Brecht stubs his cigarette.

“Time to tear down the set.”

🎙 BALDWIN, FINAL WORD:

> “You want a tweet?
Here’s mine:

America is still feeding Black children to the swamp.
And your president thinks it’s a vacation.”
