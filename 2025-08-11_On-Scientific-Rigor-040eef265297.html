What Replacing Physics Really Requires

Every so often, someone appears claiming they’ve “replaced all of physics” with a single insight. The latest example is Motion-First Physics — a set of posts declaring that “motion” is the sole primitive, that wave-particle duality is a myth, and that all modern theory can be discarded.

Big claims are exciting. But big claims have to pass big tests — and those tests are not optional. Without them, it’s not a theory. It’s a narrative.

1. You Need a Formal Foundation

A real physics framework starts with definitions:

  • What are the variables, fields, and constants?
  • What are their units and domains?
  • What are the boundary conditions?

“Motion” as an undefined concept is not a foundation. Physics needs an action or Lagrangian from which equations follow, and symmetries that produce conservation laws via Noether’s theorem. Without this, you cannot guarantee that energy, momentum, or charge are even conserved.

2. You Must Match What We Already Know

Any new theory must reproduce the successes of the old one in its limits — the correspondence principle. That means:

  • Newton’s laws when speeds are slow.
  • Special relativity’s E = mc².
  • General relativity’s time dilation and gravitational lensing.
  • Quantum mechanics’ interference, tunneling, and spectra.

If you cannot show how your theory recovers these results, you have not replaced physics — you’ve abandoned it.

3. You Have to Make Falsifiable Predictions

Replacing physics means making at least one numerical prediction that differs from current theory, with error bars, so experiments can prove you wrong.

Eliminating wave-particle duality or the Higgs field by decree isn’t enough — you have to still explain the Casimir effect, the Lamb shift, and the precise particle masses we’ve measured at colliders.

4. You Must Provide Reproducible Work

It’s not enough to say “the math exists” — you have to show it. That means:

  • Fully worked derivations.
  • Public code or simulation data.
  • Step-by-step logic that another physicist can follow without guessing your definitions.

Why This Matters

Anyone can declare “I’ve replaced all of physics.” But if the work:

  • Has no formal problem statement,
  • Defines no variables,
  • Shows no derivations,
  • Recovers no tested limits, and
  • Produces no falsifiable predictions,

…then it is not a scientific theory. It’s a set of assertions.

The Standard is the Standard

Whether it’s Motion-First Physics, EFMW, or anything else, the same standard applies:

  1. Formally defined.
  2. Derivable from first principles.
  3. Correspondence with tested regimes.
  4. Falsifiable predictions.
  5. Reproducible artifacts.

Meet those five points, and we’ll talk about “replacing physics.” Fail them, and all you’ve done is rename the universe in your own language.
