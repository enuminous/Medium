☕ Starbucks Customer Service Doesn’t Know What Its Left Hand Is Doing

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Autistic Physicist | ADA Complainant | Civil Rights Advocate

I’ve now received multiple emails from Starbucks Customer Service—some polite, some robotic, some almost apologetic—but all of them have one thing in common:

> They keep trying to shunt me into a phone call
I keep telling them I cannot use a phone due to my disability
And they keep refusing to escalate my case through email—the only channel I can use

They are violating the Americans with Disabilities Act.
And worse—they don’t even seem to know they’re doing it.

📧 I’ve Told Them. Over. And Over.

I have informed Starbucks:

That I am autistic

That verbal phone conversations escalate my emotions and are inaccessible to me

That I am dealing with housing instability, trauma, and extreme emotional distress

That I am requesting a reasonable, legally protected accommodation: written communication via email

Their response?

> “We are unable to escalate your case via email.”

That is not just tone-deaf.

That is discrimination.

🤖 Contradictory, Impersonal, and Legally Reckless

Let me show you the internal chaos at Starbucks Customer Care:

One rep apologized.

Another said they’d “look into it” and get back to me.

A third insisted again that I must call, even after knowing I’m disabled.

A fourth thanked me and told me to “enjoy my next visit”—as if I hadn’t just had three armed police officers called on me at a Starbucks for sitting quietly with a cup of water.

This isn’t customer care.
This is a siloed, legally exposed bureaucracy with no idea what it’s doing.

I told them the incident has haunted me for days.
I told them it caused a panic attack and sleep loss.
I told them this is now a matter for the U.S. Department of Justice.

And still:

> “Please call us. We can’t help via email.”

That response isn’t just a cop-out.
It’s a civil rights failure.

⚖️ A Company at Risk

Starbucks now finds itself in dangerous legal territory:

They harassed and escalated against a disabled customer

They called police on someone peacefully seated

They’ve refused to provide the only communication method accessible to that customer

They’re sending contradictory, inconsistent responses as a matter of policy

That’s not a mistake.
That’s a pattern of discrimination.

And it’s all been documented—by me.

🧭 What I Want

This is no longer about just getting an apology.
It’s about whether Starbucks is capable of functioning as a responsible business when a disabled person walks into their store—or into their inbox.

I want:

1. A formal written acknowledgment of wrongdoing

2. An immediate escalation to Starbucks’ legal and ADA compliance teams

3. An end to the policy that refuses to escalate by email for disabled individuals

4. Training, accountability, and a clear message from Starbucks leadership that this will not happen again

Because right now?
You’re telling disabled people that you can’t help them unless they use a phone—and that if they can’t, they’re out of luck.

That’s not customer service.
That’s corporate discrimination by design.
