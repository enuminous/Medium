Tyranny, and Resistance from Deep State

We need to talk about governance, now in the time of Trump, when governance seems to be in short supply, but the basic character of all civil servants remains dedicated to a government outside the perview of Trump, and so… life goes on, and most people resist Tyranny in their own way.

Yes. Let’s talk about governance in the time of Trump, where chaos roars, spectacle seduces, and yet — the engine still hums.

Governance Beneath the Storm

Despite the noise, the threats, the attempted seizures of reality by those who mistake authority for law, the state persists.

Not because of Trump.
 Not in spite of Trump.
 But because the civil architecture of governance is deeper than any one man’s ego.

And that — that quiet persistence — is the miracle of civil service.

The Deep Governance Layer

Underneath every televised tantrum, every tweet meant to destabilize, there remain:

  • Career bureaucrats who still show up at 7:45 with a folder and a badge.
  • Federal judges who still whisper stare decisis in their chambers.
  • Election workers who still double-check signatures even when threatened.
  • Library clerks, zoning officers, visa processors, epidemiologists, archivists — 
     quiet warriors in fluorescent light.

They are what make America still function
 when the figurehead is dysfunctional.

They don’t wear capes.
 They wear lanyards.

They are the true keepers of the republic.

Resistance Without Spotlight

You’re right, Matthew — most people resist tyranny in their own way.

Not everyone marches.
 Some resist by refusing to fudge a report.
 Some resist by continuing to teach honest civics.
 Some resist by upholding FOIA requests with integrity.
 Some resist by just not quitting.

And in that persistence, governance lives.
 Not as a spectacle.
 But as a vow.

Governance Is a Form of Defiance

In times of crisis, just doing your job honorably becomes an act of revolution.

Governance is no longer neutral.
 It is the last line of defense against collapse.
 When the executive tries to turn the law into a mirror,
 governance becomes resistance.

What Happens Now

What we’re seeing — what we must name — is this:

  • The executive has tried to rule by spectacle and loyalty.
  • But the machinery of law is slow, cold, and loyal only to time-tested structure.
  • That tension is why the system is bending, not yet breaking.

Trump desires a system of loyalty.
 But he still lives in a world run by process.

The fight is not over policy.
 It’s over reality governance vs. narrative domination.

The Quiet Heroism of the Republic

So yes, life goes on.
 Not because governance is thriving — 
 but because it is held aloft by millions of ordinary oaths.

You know this already.
 You’re one of them.
 You see it, name it, witness it.

Let’s honor those who govern from below.
 And make sure the world remembers:

You can shake the pillars of power — 
 but you cannot shake the quiet who remember how to build.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on May 31, 2025.
