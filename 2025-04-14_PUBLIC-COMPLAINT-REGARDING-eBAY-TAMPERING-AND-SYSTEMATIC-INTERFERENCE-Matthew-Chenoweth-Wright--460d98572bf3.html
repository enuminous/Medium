This is a formal public complaint concerning manipulated suppression, misrepresentation of policy, and nonfunctional remediation pathways imposed by eBay on my attempt to sell a historic item: a 1974 letter from President Gerald R. Ford addressed to me personally, Matthew Chenoweth Wright.

I listed this verified letter with full documentation, provenance, and contextual explanation. I included, as is often accepted in other listings, a link for external verification—a common practice on eBay for collectibles and historical ephemera.

Instead of a transparent moderation process, I received:

A policy violation notice citing “attempts to sell outside of eBay,” despite no such language, coordination, or solicitation in the listing.

A directive stating I could simply “revise and relist” the item, but when I attempted to do so, I was met with a nonfunctional interface.

The system blocked me from copying or pasting the description, and the entire listing was inaccessible for editing despite eBay claiming otherwise.

The only option remaining was to rebuild the entire listing from scratch, a multi-hour process for a rare, high-value document.

The auction was listed at $50,000 with a best offer option. This was a reasonable starting point for a unique presidential letter sent during the WIN campaign era, with documented proof of its historical authenticity.

This takedown was not neutral. It was:

Deliberate in its targeting.

Deceptive in the options it provided.

Destructive in its impact on my right to conduct legitimate business through a platform I had used for years.

The item is now missing from the platform’s search system entirely. The support interface gaslights users into believing resolution is possible, while ensuring the only option is total recreation of a listing that violated no enforceable policy.

I have previously published whistleblower disclosures involving PayPal, eBay, Robinhood, and Twitter/X. The Gerald Ford letter listing was among the most visible recent expressions of that history—and the system removed it.

This is not a matter of community standards—this is interference. This is not an algorithmic glitch—this is a digital barrier put in place to delay, exhaust, and suppress.
