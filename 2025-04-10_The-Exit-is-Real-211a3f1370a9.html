The Exit is Real

To Those Living in Silence, You Can Leave

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

“Don’t like what I write, don’t read them.”

That’s what Patrick Prescott—a man with a public author badge on Medium—responded with when confronted over the nature of his writing. A dismissive shrug from someone who either doesn’t care, or doesn’t believe, that his words can harm others.

But this isn’t about hurt feelings. This is about power.
This is about patterns of abuse, emotional manipulation, and the way abusers hide in plain sight—often cloaked in language, wit, and plausible deniability.

I called him out.
Not just because I disagreed with him—but because I recognized the signature.
The dodge. The deflection. The attempt to silence critique with shame and distance.

And then I asked the most important question of all:

> “Is your partner okay?”

Because sometimes, that’s the only way to get through to the people who are watching silently. Not him. Her.
If she exists—and I suspect she does—then she may now know:

Someone sees her.

---

To Every Woman (or Man) Who Saw Themselves in That Exchange:

You are not crazy.
You are not too sensitive.
You are not imagining it.

If your partner belittles your feelings, isolates you, controls your movements, or makes you feel small so they can feel large—that is not love.

If you’re scared to speak, scared to leave, scared to even think about what life might be like outside of their control—
I am telling you right now: The exit is real. You can leave.

You can walk to a friend’s house and never go back.
You can pack a bag while they’re asleep.
You can message a stranger who posted something that made your heart skip.

The world is filled with people who will believe you, protect you, and help you rebuild.

---

Why Call Out Patrick by Name?

Because this is no longer the time for polite silence.
Writers with public platforms who use that platform to poison minds, dismiss accountability, or subtly reinforce patriarchal control deserve to be called out.

I’m not doxxing him.
I’m not threatening him.
I’m naming a pattern, and showing others how to break it.

Because the next woman who reads what he writes might feel the same chill run down her spine—and think she’s the only one. She’s not.

---

The Signal is Sent.

If you’re reading this and feel seen, feel shaken, or feel unsure—

That feeling?
That’s your truth trying to speak.

Don’t silence it.

---

You are allowed to cry.
You are allowed to collapse.
You are allowed to leave.
And most of all—you are allowed to be free.

To Patrick:
Your words didn’t go unnoticed. Neither did your evasions. You’ve been named not for shame—but for truth. What you do with that truth is now up to you.

To everyone else:

We are listening.
We believe you.
We’re right here—when you’re ready.

—

STATE AND LOCAL RESOURCES

The National Domestic Violence Hotline provides a full state-by-state directory of local support organizations, shelters, and legal services.

ADDITIONAL RESOURCES

Housing Assistance

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) provides information on emergency and long-term housing options for survivors, including tenant protections under the Violence Against Women Act (VAWA).
