✡️ When Annihilation Wears a Tie

A Letter to John Wight and the Architects of Erasure

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

“Every war begins with the idea that someone else has no right to exist.”
— Anonymous Holocaust survivor

On July 31st, 2025, a Medium article was published by John Wight, titled:
“Israel’s Non-Existence Is an Idea Whose Time Has Come.”

It is not simply controversial. It is morally reprehensible.

I am writing this in open reply—not as a Zionist, not as a political operative, not as a paid voice—but as a citizen of the human condition. A witness. A thinker. A man who knows how these things start.

And I am here to say plainly:

John Wight, you are a bigot.
And this article is an incitement, veiled in intellectual camouflage.

🕯️ The Eliminationist Framework

Let’s define this clearly, because history has before:

An eliminationist narrative is any ideological structure that seeks to frame an entire people, ethnicity, nation, or belief system as:

Inherently illegitimate

Intrinsically dangerous

Better off erased

These arguments always sound “reasonable” at first. They wear the clothes of justice. They come bearing maps, charts, theories, and a certain cold calmness.

What they lack is humanity.

📜 Historical Precedents: Words That Led to Ashes

🇷🇼 Rwanda, 1994:

Radio Télévision Libre des Mille Collines began referring to Tutsis as "cockroaches."

The genocide was not sudden. It was narrated.

The idea? “This group should not exist.”

🇩🇪 Nazi Germany, 1930s:

Jews were framed not just as outsiders, but as a contagion—a people without a right to citizenship, to land, to breath.

Intellectuals signed petitions. Professors gave lectures. Editors wrote calmly about "solving the Jewish problem."

🇧🇦 Bosnia, 1990s:

In Serbian media, Bosnian Muslims were painted as “invaders,” a group whose presence itself was a threat to identity.

Again, not framed as war—but as cleansing.

What ties them all together?

The calm assertion that a people’s nonexistence would be a solution.

That’s what you’ve written, John.
You’ve offered genocide as a policy position.

🧠 But I’m Just Criticizing Zionism!

No, you’re not.

Criticizing Zionist policy is legitimate. I do it myself.
Opposing Netanyahu’s government? Absolutely.
Condemning war crimes, civilian death, apartheid policies, surveillance states, and systemic oppression? Without hesitation.

But calling for the non-existence of Israel is something else entirely.

You are not talking about government.
You are talking about erasing the collective existence of a people who have survived pogroms, exiles, inquisitions, ghettos, genocide, and diaspora.
You are calling for the removal of the only Jewish-majority state on the planet.

🎭 Anti-Zionism Is Not an Alibi

Let me be clear:

Not all anti-Zionism is antisemitism.
But eliminationist anti-Zionism is.

When you call for the erasure of the only home that ever took them all back—you are not engaging in critique.
You are continuing a very old story.

🧨 A Future of Fire or Dialogue?

The only viable future in the Middle East—or anywhere fractured—is one of dialogue, survival, justice, and compromise.

Not erasure.
Not “non-existence.”
Not fantasies of righteous vengeance written in the blood of millions.

You don’t have to agree with a nation’s policies to acknowledge its right to exist.

You don’t have to support a regime to believe in its people’s right not to die.

🗣️ My Message to John Wight

You want to write something radical?

Write a roadmap for coexistence.
Write a call for truth commissions, peace accords, and demilitarized imagination.
Write an article titled “How to Save Every Child in Gaza and Every Child in Tel Aviv.”

But when you write this?

“Israel’s Non-Existence Is an Idea Whose Time Has Come”

…you write a call to wipe a people off the map.
And I will not let that pass without saying:

That is bigotry.
That is dehumanization.
That is moral rot, and I rebuke it fully.

✡️ Let This Be Known

You are not brave.
You are not radical.
You are not justice-minded.

You are speaking the same cold language that lit every crematorium, toppled every minaret, and drowned every refugee boat for the last thousand years.

We’ve heard it before.

We are done pretending it’s clever.

Signed,
Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist. Writer. Witness.
