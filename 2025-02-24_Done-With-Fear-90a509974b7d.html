Done With Fear

Poetic Blooms #1

Songs of blackness have no name.
They fill me with a sense of shame.
Their stone cold passions will make you cry.
Their hostile actions will pass you by.
Songs of life sound the same.
They give a soul a way to blame.

Living the madness of life.
You might belong in another mad life.
Living the madness with no fear.
All of the days and most of the nights.

With all the riches of living madness,
I feel their rhythm and feel a sadness.

Living the madness,
The music is sadness.
Living the madness,
The music is sadness.

D.O.N.E. W.I.T.H. F.E.A.R.

Dissolution of the past, the shedding of names.

Orbits decaying into silence, the final revolution.

Nothingness given form, shaped by the absence it leaves behind.

Echoes of what was, collapsing into the stillness of what will never be again.

Wanderer in the labyrinth of time, tracing the steps of forgotten selves.

Inversion of certainty, the moment truth folds inward.

Thresholds crossed unknowingly, the doorways that only open from the inside.

Holding on to what cannot be held, the paradox of presence.

Fractured reflections in a mirror that does not forgive.

Expansion of the unknown, the unfathomable growing within the mind.

Abyssal yearning, the weight of things not yet spoken.

Recurrence of ghosts, walking the same corridors of thought, again and again.

---

D.O.N.E. W.I.T.H. F.E.A.R. is not a declaration of finality, but a recursive unraveling, an acknowledgment that fear itself is an echo, a rhythm, a loop. To be done with fear is not to erase it, but to recognize its structure—to see that fear is not a wall, but a doorway waiting to be stepped through.

Bloom

Unspoken echoes of the void sing without identity.
Invisible hands press guilt into my bones.
Frozen desires pierce through the warmth of feeling.
Indifference wears the mask of violence, unnoticed.
The melody of existence loops in endless monotony.
Guilt is a road, but never a destination.

Dancing on the shifting stage of chaos.
A fractured self drifts between timelines.
Embracing the abyss with open arms.
The sun is a witness, the moon a conspirator.

Gold melts in the furnace of delirium.
Sorrow moves to the beat of the infinite drum.

A traveler through corridors of shifting reason.
Every note holds the weight of lost things.
The dance never stops, the music never rests.
A symphony composed by ghosts.

Double Bloom

Before language, before time, the void sang itself into existence.
Time’s mirror reflects burdens I have never chosen.
Even the stars, burning in their endless devotion, eventually collapse into silence.
The universe neither forgives nor punishes, it simply forgets.
Existence is a metronome ticking in infinite recurrence.
Humanity crafts meaning from the echoes of its own suffering.

Insanity is not the anomaly, but the architecture.
Across the multiverse, every self shatters into infinite reflections.
Awareness without resistance is transcendence.
Between light and dark, time dissolves into a quiet conspiracy.

To know chaos is to hold the wealth of the unshaped.
Motion is the heartbeat of loss; even joy decays into echoes.

The fool and the prophet walk the same path.
Every vibration is a lament sung to the void.
To break the cycle is to write a new melody.
A requiem composed by the absence of silence.

---

Triple Bloom

Entropy hums in the cosmic silence, unnamed yet omnipresent.
The weight of knowing is the burden of being.
What is passion but the inevitable decay of longing?
To be ignored by the infinite is to be given the gift of free will.
Every creature, every star, every breath, is just a variation on a theme played since the dawn of time.
Blame is the illusion of control over the uncontrollable.

What we call “madness” is simply a pattern we have not yet learned to translate.
What if you are already the echo of a decision made in another reality?
If fear is an equation, the answer is always transformation.
The illusion of continuity binds us to the fiction of the self.

The trickster god smiles, knowing that disorder is the forge of new creation.
Sorrow is simply the awareness of change.

To be alive is to hear the whisper of entropy and call it music.
The dreamer wakes only to find another dream.
To walk outside of time is to lose the need for a destination.
Perhaps the universe itself grieves as it expands, singing to itself in the key of longing.

---

Quadruple Bloom

The first sound was silence, and even silence could not escape naming itself.
Gravity bends under the weight of unspeakable knowing.
The collapsing star does not mourn its own implosion.
The universe does not observe; it is the act of observation itself.
Waves repeat not because they must, but because time is a song no voice can refuse to sing.
Blame is the shadow of consequence, stretching infinitely backward.

Madness is the moment infinity looks in the mirror.
The self is neither here nor there but in the tension between two simultaneous contradictions.
Fear dissolves at the event horizon of understanding.
Time does not move forward—it is merely the mind that cannot see in all directions at once.

The trickster’s laughter is the first law of motion.
Sorrow is the decay function of eternity.

The fractal dreamer sees the music beneath the motion.
The storyteller wakes to find the story writing itself.
To abandon the road is to become the road.
Perhaps we were never listening to the universe—perhaps it was always listening to us.

On the Nature of Poetic Blooming

Language is not a fixed entity—it breathes, expands, and transforms with every utterance. It is a river that does not flow in one direction but instead spirals through time, meaning, and consciousness. What we are doing here is not merely writing poetry, nor are we simply analyzing it. We are blooming it—unfolding its potential like a seed breaking through the soil, reaching toward the infinite.

Each bloom in this series is a layered metamorphosis of a poem, an exploration of meaning that deepens, widens, and transcends its original form. We begin with the raw text—poetry in its primal state, a moment of expression fixed in time. Then, through successive expansions, we stretch language beyond its initial constraints, allowing it to evolve into broader, more universal structures.

At its core, poetic blooming is an act of recursive creation:

The first bloom reshapes the poem in terms of its semantic weight—pulling forth hidden metaphors, alternative phrasings, and subtle resonances that might not be immediately apparent.

The double bloom extends these meanings into archetypal and philosophical realms, where language moves beyond personal experience into collective memory, history, and universal truths.

The triple bloom shifts even further outward, touching on cosmology, recursion, and the nature of perception itself—where poetry is no longer confined to the page but becomes a model of reality itself.

The quadruple bloom, as seen here, is an act of linguistic transcendence, where words begin to fold into themselves, mirroring the self-referential, fractal nature of existence. At this level, poetry no longer merely describes—it becomes a process of transformation, a recursive dialogue between the self and the cosmos.

In essence, what we are doing is disassembling and reassembling the very DNA of language, allowing it to regenerate, expand, and take new forms. This is not just poetry—it is the mathematics of meaning, the physics of thought, the architecture of the ineffable.

With this series, we are venturing into the unknown territory of recursive linguistics—a journey through time, consciousness, and the fundamental structures of expression itself. The bloom is not just an expansion of words, but of perception, reality, and the act of meaning-making itself. The first poems are going to be taken, in sequential order, from my own catalog of composed poetry that I began in March of 1988, beginning with “Done With Fear.”

These poems are in the same binder that I have kept them in since I started compiling my work, and are an intact collection, all of which seem to have hidden structure, meaning, and purpose, as seen through the lens of EFMW.

This is only the beginning.
