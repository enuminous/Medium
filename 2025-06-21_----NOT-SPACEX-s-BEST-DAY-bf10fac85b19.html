üîç 2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

At T-0 of a planned cryogenic pressurization test, Starship SN36 experienced a catastrophic structural failure, resulting in the loss of the vehicle. Preliminary analysis indicates the failure was initiated by a rupture of a Composite Overwrapped Pressure Vessel (COPV), likely located in the payload bay or upper fuselage.

The rupture caused a localized overpressure event that breached adjacent plumbing and likely ignited a methane-air mixture within the vehicle skin, culminating in a total midbody disassembly. Containment systems and methane tank orientation prevented a secondary cascade into the adjacent tank farm.

3.1 Phase I: Nominal Pre-Test Pressurization

  • Nitrogen was loaded into onboard COPVs for thermal conditioning and structural pressurization.
  • All telemetry and environmental sensors remained within nominal parameters until approximately T-2s.
  • No thermal hotspots or vibrational anomalies were detected during the fill sequence.

3.2 Phase II: Structural Breach of¬†COPV

  • At T-1.9s, a rapid depressurization was registered in COPV channel 3.
  • High-speed footage and audio spectrum data suggest a mechanical delamination or cap shear event.
  • Contributing factors under investigation:
  • Cyclical cryogenic fatigue from prior use.
  • Manufacturing flaw or resin failure in composite overwrap.
  • Insufficient stress relief at trunnion mounting point.

3.3 Phase III: Internal Overpressure and Fireball Initiation

  • The COPV rupture produced a directed shockwave that compromised secondary plumbing‚Ää‚Äî‚Ääpotentially fuel line insulation or routing within the skin cavity.

3.4 Phase IV: Fireball Propagation and Facility Containment

  • Ignition remained localized to the vehicle envelope.
  • Methane/oxygen tank farm vented nominally, preventing detonation.
  • Facility shielding absorbed the remaining pressure wave; no critical hardware was damaged beyond SN36.

6.1 Design and Materials

  • Redesign COPV collars with improved shear margin and delamination arrest features.
  • Introduce cryogenic-specific composite weave analysis for trunnion areas.

  • Incorporate methane vent redirection away from overwrap-critical areas during static prep.
