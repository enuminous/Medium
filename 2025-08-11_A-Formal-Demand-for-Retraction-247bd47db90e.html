On False Public Statements by Peter Whitlock Regarding EFMW

A Formal Demand for Retraction

On False Public Statements by Peter Whitlock Regarding EFMW

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Physicist, EFMW Framework Author, Federal Whistleblower

1. Introduction

In recent public comments on my published work, Mr. Peter Whitlock has made statements that are both factually false and damaging to my professional reputation. He has asserted that my EFMW framework is devoid of mathematical rigor, contains no formal problem statement, and lacks the necessary derivations to meet the Clay Millennium Prize standard for P ≠ NP.

These assertions are categorically false.

2. The Specific Allegations

Mr. Whitlock’s public comments mischaracterized EFMW as:

Containing “no formal problem statement”

  • This is incorrect. My internal submission to reviewers and documented private archives contains both the formal Clay Institute framing and the complete scope definitions.

Lacking definitions, lemmas, or step-by-step derivations

  • Again, false. These components exist in my proof materials and have been subjected to independent, private review by credentialed mathematicians and physicists.

Consisting only of “narrative warfare” and “disinformation” analysis

  • This is misleading in the extreme. EFMW is a mathematically anchored physical framework with wide interdisciplinary applications. The narrative warfare papers are a separate application domain of EFMW principles, not the proof of P ≠ NP itself.

Not meeting Clay standards

  • This statement is speculative and unfounded. Mr. Whitlock has not seen my complete proof, nor is he in any position to judge its compliance with Clay’s submission requirements.

3. The Harm

By publishing these falsehoods, Mr. Whitlock has:

  • Damaged my credibility in the eyes of the public and potential collaborators.
  • Misled readers into believing my work is without mathematical substance.
  • Positioned himself as an authority over work he has not reviewed in full.

Such conduct, intentional or reckless, is defamatory.

4. The Demand

I hereby demand that Mr. Whitlock:

  1. Retract the specific false statements outlined above.
  2. Issue a public correction in the same venues where the false statements appeared.
  3. Acknowledge that he has not seen the full EFMW proof and cannot speak to its completeness or compliance with Clay Institute standards.

5. The Next Step

This is not a call for personal animosity. I have no objection to professional critique of my work, provided it is honest, informed, and accurate.

However, knowingly or negligently making false claims about another researcher’s work is unacceptable. Should Mr. Whitlock refuse to retract, I will pursue all available remedies, including formal legal action for defamation.

6. Closing

The EFMW framework is a mathematically rigorous, physically consistent model. It has been independently reviewed, internally validated, and prepared for full public release under the proper circumstances.
False public statements about its validity harm not only my reputation but also the public understanding of serious scientific work.

I expect a retraction.
Publicly. Promptly. Unambiguously.

Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Author, Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright (EFMW) Framework
Federal Whistleblower, Physicist
