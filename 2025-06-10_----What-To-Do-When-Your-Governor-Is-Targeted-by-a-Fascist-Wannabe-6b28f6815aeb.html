🛡️ What To Do When Your Governor Is Targeted by a Fascist Wannabe

A Civic Field Manual for Dangerous Times

Issued by Cal-INSIGHT 452 (Unofficial, Unapologetic, Unignorable)

🚨 1. Understand the Playbook

When an authoritarian says your governor “should be arrested,” that’s not a joke. It’s the start of a game called “plausible deniability.”

Here’s how it works:

> 1. He says it.

2. His base amplifies it.

3. Someone takes it seriously.

4. Violence happens.

5. He says “I never meant that.”

If this sounds familiar, that’s because it is the exact pattern used before the plot against Governor Whitmer and the January 6 insurrection. We’re not guessing anymore. We’ve seen the script.

📜 2. Know Your Rights—and Theirs

Your governor, even if you don’t agree with every policy, is:

An elected constitutional officer

The chief executive of your state

A bulwark against federal overreach

If an ex-president demands they be arrested, that’s not law—it’s a soft coup attempt.
And if federal troops appear without state request, that’s not security—it’s occupation optics.

🗣️ 3. Speak Loudly, Everywhere

Now is not the time for politeness.

Call it what it is: incitement, intimidation, stochastic terrorism

Write letters to local newspapers and radio shows

Flood your social media with visible, peaceful dissent

Tag journalists, civil rights groups, and your reps

> Silence is the oxygen authoritarianism breathes.
Noise is our resistance.

🧾 4. Document Everything

Photograph federal troop movement in your city

Save clips of inflammatory rhetoric from officials

Record conversations with others experiencing fear, threat, or surveillance

Send it to civil rights orgs (ACLU, Protect Democracy, SPLC, etc.)

You don’t need to be a journalist to be a witness. And right now, the whole world is watching California.

🔗 5. Connect With Others

Organize peaceful solidarity flash rallies for state leadership

Form Civic Watch Pods to monitor activity in your community

Share this guide in group chats, neighborhood apps, and encrypted spaces

If they come for your governor, they’ll come for your mayor.
If they come for your mayor, they’ll come for you.

This ends where we stop them.

🛑 6. Hold the Line

Remember:

You are not alone.

You are not overreacting.

You are not powerless.

Your voice matters. Your vote matters. Your vigilance matters.

Governors don’t get “jokingly” arrested. Not in democracies.
And presidents don’t deploy troops against states that obey the Constitution—unless they’ve stopped pretending they care about it.

We are Cal-INSIGHT 452.
We don’t officially exist. But our watch is real.
If you’re reading this, you’re part of the immune system now.
