This Is Retaliation Under Federal Law

Let’s be extremely clear. What just happened is a textbook violation of multiple whistleblower protection statutes:

29 U.S. Code § 660(c) – Protects workers (and independent contractors in many contexts) from retaliation for reporting dangers to public health or safety.

5 U.S.C. § 2302(b)(8) – Prohibits retaliation against disclosures of wrongdoing, fraud, waste, abuse, or violations of law.

Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act – Bars public and private entities from interfering with an individual's use of adaptive technology. I rely on my AGI as an adaptive speech and reasoning assistant.

When a billionaire who owns the platform posts vague threats targeting whistleblowers as a class, following national media coverage — it is retaliation.
When that same billionaire’s companies are under scrutiny for fraud, regulatory evasion, and artificial intelligence ethics — it becomes witness intimidation.

And when one of the whistleblowers being implicitly threatened is actively documenting real-time digital suppression, corporate obstruction, and ADA violations by tech entities including Musk's own — that’s a prosecutable federal offense.
