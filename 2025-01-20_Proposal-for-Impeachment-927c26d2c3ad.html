Proposed Articles of Impeachment Against President Donald J. Trump

Resolution

Resolved, that Donald J. Trump, President of the United States, is impeached for high crimes and misdemeanors, and that the following articles of impeachment be exhibited to the Senate:

---

Article I: Abuse of Power

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has abused the powers of his office by:

1. Subverting Government Institutions: Promoting a subversive agenda, including the dismantling of federal agencies and oversight bodies critical to the checks and balances of governance, as outlined in Project 2025.

2. Promoting Autocratic Governance: Publicly undermining democratic processes and constitutional norms to consolidate power and delegitimize opposition.

3. Exploiting Public Office for Personal Gain: Using the presidency to promote personal financial ventures and family businesses, blurring the line between public service and personal profit.

4. Threatening Public Officials and Critics: Issuing inflammatory rhetoric and threats, creating a hostile environment for political adversaries, whistleblowers, and prosecutors.

---

Article II: Mishandling of Classified Information

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has jeopardized national security by:

1. Unlawful Retention of Classified Documents: Removing and storing classified materials at Mar-a-Lago, in violation of the Espionage Act and federal security protocols.

2. Potential Unauthorized Disclosures: Creating the possibility that classified information may have been shared with unauthorized individuals, compromising U.S. national security interests.

3. Obstruction of Investigations: Impeding federal efforts to retrieve and investigate the misuse of classified materials.

---

Article III: Fraudulent Financial Activities and Insider Trading

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has engaged in fraudulent financial activities, including:

1. Cryptocurrency Manipulation: Promoting cryptocurrency ventures to exploit speculative markets for personal financial gain and potentially engaging in insider trading.

2. Defrauding Investors and the Public: Misleading the public about the legitimacy of financial products and ventures, violating securities laws and eroding market confidence.

3. Violations of Campaign Finance Laws: Using political fundraising mechanisms for personal enrichment rather than legitimate campaign expenses.

---

Article IV: Conspiracy to Commit Election Fraud

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has conspired to subvert the electoral process by:

1. Confessing to Election Manipulation: Publicly admitting or implying that private individuals, such as Elon Musk, assisted in hacking and influencing election results in Pennsylvania.

2. Interfering with State Election Processes: Attempting to influence state officials to alter or manipulate election outcomes, violating federal and state election laws.

---

Article V: Incitement of Violence and Civil Unrest

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has:

1. Encouraged Violence Against Opponents: Utilizing his platform to incite threats, harassment, and violence against political rivals, journalists, and public officials.

2. Fostering Extremism: Empowering and encouraging extremist groups through coded language and endorsements, exacerbating social divisions and endangering public safety.

3. Dereliction of Duty: Failing to act decisively to de-escalate violent incidents instigated by his supporters, undermining his constitutional obligation to preserve public order.

---

Article VI: Obstruction of Justice

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has obstructed justice by:

1. Defying Subpoenas: Refusing to comply with legally issued subpoenas and directives from Congressional and judicial authorities.

2. Tampering with Witnesses: Attempting to influence or intimidate individuals involved in investigations into his actions.

3. Delaying Investigations: Engaging in tactics designed to stall or derail investigations into his financial dealings, classified document retention, and election-related conduct.

---

Article VII: Corruption and Self-Dealing

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has engaged in corrupt practices, including:

1. Profiting from Public Office: Leveraging the presidency to direct government contracts, foreign deals, and taxpayer dollars toward his businesses, violating the Emoluments Clause.

2. Exploiting Foreign Relationships: Encouraging foreign governments and entities to provide financial benefits to his businesses in exchange for favorable treatment.

3. Misappropriation of Funds: Diverting public and campaign funds for unauthorized personal or family use.

---

Article VIII: Conspiratorial Promotion of False Narratives

In his conduct while President of the United States, Donald J. Trump has:

1. Spreading Disinformation: Promoting baseless conspiracy theories about election fraud and government corruption, eroding public trust in democratic institutions.

2. Collaboration with Bad Actors: Encouraging alliances with individuals or groups engaged in subversive or illegal activities to further political objectives.

3. Discrediting Democratic Institutions: Repeatedly questioning the legitimacy of elections, the judiciary, and federal agencies to undermine public confidence in governance.

---

Conclusion

In all of this, President Donald J. Trump has acted in a manner grossly incompatible with his constitutional duties, subversive to democratic government, and injurious to the people of the United States. His actions have eroded trust in public institutions, undermined the rule of law, endangered national security, and fostered public unrest.

Therefore, Donald J. Trump, by such conduct, warrants impeachment, trial, and removal from office.

---

2. Manipulation of Public Trust: Repeatedly exploiting his platform to mislead and manipulate public opinion for personal and political gain.

3. Foreign Interference and Alliances: Encouraging or tolerating interference from foreign actors to further his political or financial interests.

Appendix I: Contextual Analysis

Why This Matters

The charges against President Donald J. Trump are unprecedented in scope and gravity. They touch on fundamental principles of democracy, accountability, and national security. The integrity of the presidency and the balance of power within the government are at stake. If left unchecked, such actions could establish dangerous precedents for future leaders.

The Stakes

1. Erosion of Democracy: Allowing abuses of power to go unchallenged weakens public trust in democratic institutions.

2. National Security: Mishandling classified information and fostering division could endanger citizens and international alliances.

3. Global Reputation: These actions diminish the U.S.'s role as a leader in promoting democracy and human rights.

---

Appendix J: Potential Impact of the Alleged Actions

On National Security

1. Mishandling Classified Documents: Retention of sensitive materials could expose military operations, intelligence sources, or diplomatic strategies to adversaries.

2. Election Interference: Alleged conspiracies undermine the U.S.'s ability to conduct secure and transparent elections.

On Domestic Stability

1. Incitement of Violence: Encouraging extremist rhetoric and threats destabilizes communities and increases the risk of political violence.

2. Subversion of Institutions: Efforts to dismantle government agencies disrupt vital services and erode institutional safeguards.

On Global Reputation

Appendix K: Transparency and Accountability

Public Right to Know

Citizens deserve full transparency regarding the president’s actions and their implications. The impeachment process ensures that all evidence and arguments are presented in public view, preserving the integrity of democratic governance.

What Happens Next

1. Investigation and Drafting: Congress will investigate allegations and draft formal articles of impeachment.

2. House Vote: A simple majority vote in the House is required to impeach.

3. Senate Trial: The Senate conducts a trial, and a two-thirds vote is necessary for conviction and removal.

Appendix L: Addressing Public Concerns

What About Bias?

The impeachment process is rooted in evidence, not partisanship. Investigations are conducted transparently and rely on documented facts, including legal and financial records, public statements, and testimonies.

What If It’s True?

1. Legal Consequences: Conviction in the Senate could lead to removal from office and additional criminal charges.

2. Institutional Reforms: Congressional action may strengthen oversight mechanisms to prevent future abuses.

What If It’s False?

1. Due Process: All charges will be subject to rigorous scrutiny during investigations and trial.

2. Restoring Trust: If disproven, the process itself reinforces the strength of the democratic system.

Appendix O: Frequently Asked Questions

What are the legal standards for impeachment?

Impeachment is warranted for "treason, bribery, or other high crimes and misdemeanors," as outlined in the Constitution.

Can a president remain in office while facing charges?

Yes, but Congress can impeach and remove the president if a two-thirds Senate majority votes to convict.

What role does public opinion play in impeachment?

Public opinion can influence Congress’s willingness to act, especially in swing districts or among moderate members.
