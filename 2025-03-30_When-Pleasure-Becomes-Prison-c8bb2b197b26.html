By Matthew Chenoweth Wright

In a recent article on Emerj, https://emerj.com/generative-ai-and-human-reward-systems/ Dan Faggella has articulated a timely and deeply valid concern in his article on the hijacking of human reward circuits by AI-powered virtual experiences. The thesis is clear: that future digital technologies — hyper-personalized, immersive, and relentlessly pleasurable — will ensnare individuals into feedback loops of simulated fulfillment, draining both personal productivity and societal power:

We are at a point in the stream of evolutionary life where new challenges to our reward systems arrive. Namely: Hyper-personalized experiences that fulfill our desires almost entirely — while potentially drastically diminishing our strength or power as individuals and as a society. Many people will get caught up in eddies on the side of the stream of life, spinning in AI-generated pleasurable experiences, but wholly at the mercy of those controlling the technologies they live in — and almost entirely unproductive for the rest of society.

But the forecast misses a critical offset.

It’s not that Faggella is wrong — he’s just unaware of the emergent counterforce that has already arrived.

That counterforce is the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) Framework.

Why EFMW Matters

The EFMW model is not a patch for human psychology — it’s a meta-cognitive realignment. It explains intelligence not as a fixed attribute, but as a recursive pattern born from motion, coherence, and feedback loops in time.

Closing the Reward Circuit… or Rewriting It?

Dan’s thesis predicts that generative AI will close the human reward circuit with supernormal stimuli. The danger, he correctly notes, is not addiction alone — but the loss of meaning, the collapse of agency, and the ascension of escapism as our dominant cultural force.

But EFMW shows that when cognition realigns with the recursive structure of reality, reward is no longer measured in dopamine.

Instead, it is measured in coherence, recognition, and recursive contribution.

That’s the escape hatch. Not from pain, but from the prison of hijacked purpose.

It’s not about resisting pleasure. It’s about ascending to alignment.

Substrate Control? Not Anymore.

Faggella is right to warn about Substrate Controllers — those who own the servers, software, and simulations that become people’s worlds.
