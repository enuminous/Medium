When I accused Donald Trump of genocide, I didn’t do it lightly.
 I mapped the system. I laid out the timeline. I showed how a machine of stochastic terror, legal dismantling, and narrative warfare creates erasure — not just of people, but of truth itself.

Trump didn’t answer directly. He never does.
 Instead, a myth appeared: the genocide of white Afrikaners.
 With it, a lie.
 With it, crosses.
 With it, a mirror meant to reflect guilt back at me.

But the mirror cracked.
 Because it came too fast.
 Too precisely.
 Too perfectly inverted.

So I investigated.

And now I name them.

I. Jacob Wohl: The Inverter

Wohl is the narrative saboteur.
 He fabricates allegations, manufactures media, and time-bombs disinformation into the bloodstream of American discourse.

This time, he used:

  • An old alt-right myth
  • A recycled visual (empty crosses)
  • A tightly timed release window, aligned within 72 hours of my published accusation

Through AI-laundering fronts like LobbyMatic, Wohl operates upstream — monitoring threats, flipping them, and feeding them into the propaganda engine. He took my word — genocide — and built a false genocide to overwrite it.

He isn’t clever. He’s caught.

II. Jack Burkman: The Co-Conspirator

Burkman, Wohl’s longtime partner in false flag provocations, functions as the handler. He manages the logistics of the lie — contracts, production, amplification.

Where Wohl spins, Burkman deploys.

Together, they orchestrated:

  • Robocall suppression in 2020
  • Fake accusations against public figures
  • A YouTube smear machine masquerading as justice

This time, they tried to beat truth to the finish line.
 But I had already crossed it.

III. Dan Scavino: The Echo Technician

Scavino doesn’t invent stories.
 He broadcasts them.

As Trump’s digital consigliere, Scavino receives narrative payloads from lower channels — Wohl, Burkman, Steve Bannon’s War Room — and prepares them for the main stage.

He crafts:

  • Tweet templates
  • Talking points
  • Video cues with emotional charge

The false genocide footage?
 It was crafted to travel.
 Scavino is how it got there.

IV. Stephen Miller: The Codifier

Where Wohl invents lies and Scavino packages them, Stephen Miller translates them into policy logic.

Miller gives the narrative its legal veneer.
 He transforms spectacle into justification.

Today it’s crosses.
 Tomorrow it’s visa bans.
 Then it’s militarized borders.

Miller doesn’t shout. He codifies.

If Trump now references South Africa in law or decree, you’ll know Miller touched the draft.

V. The Intern Who Feeds Trump

They brought my work to Trump’s attention.

Whether they knew what they were unleashing is irrelevant.
 They are part of the machine.

And the machine just left fingerprints on the mirror.
