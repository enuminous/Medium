Abstract

Unauthorized book resales on international e-commerce platforms pose a significant challenge for authors and intellectual property rights holders. Applying the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) framework, this study examines the propagation, distortion, and recursive drift of literary works in digital marketplaces. The case of On The Water: Poetry & Prose being listed on Coupang Taiwan under a different title exemplifies how information distortion and marketplace loopholes create new challenges for authors. 2. Background: Unauthorized Listings in Global E-Commerce

Unauthorized book listings occur through various mechanisms, including:

  • Third-Party Resellers leveraging international ISBN databases.
  • Automated Print-on-Demand Aggregators creating listings without authorial consent.
  • Cross-Border Marketplaces lacking stringent copyright verification protocols.

In this case, the book was:

  • Listed under a modified title (水：詩與散文 / Water: Poetry and Prose).
  • Priced at 214 TWD (~$6.90 USD), diverging from the Amazon-listed price of $7.39 USD.
  • Available through an unverified seller with no disclosed rights to distribution.

  4. International IP Violations — Variances in enforcement mechanisms allow sellers to operate across legal gray zones.

5. Countermeasures and Authorial Protections

5.1 Cease and Desist Action

A formal cease and desist letter has been issued to Coupang Taiwan, demanding:

  • Immediate delisting of the unauthorized product.
  • Disclosure of seller identity to trace the origin of unauthorized distribution.
  • Prevention of future listings through ISBN verification.

5.2 ISBN Tracking and Digital Fingerprinting

  • Authors should monitor ISBN activity to detect unauthorized resales.
  • Implementing digital watermarks in future editions can trace unlicensed reproductions.

5.3 Platform Accountability and Policy Reforms

  • Stronger verification requirements should be enforced on open marketplaces.
  • Automated content recognition (ACR) systems can flag potential IP violations.

5.4 Author Self-Distribution in International Markets

To prevent unauthorized resellers from exploiting international demand, authors should:

  • Proactively publish localized editions in foreign markets.
  • Utilize direct-to-consumer platforms that bypass third-party sellers.

6. Conclusion: The Need for Systemic Change

The case of On The Water: Poetry & Prose illustrates the fragility of digital intellectual property in an unregulated e-commerce ecosystem. Applying the EFMW framework, we uncover how unauthorized listings propagate, distort market equilibrium, and recursively drift into sustained economic impact.

The response must be twofold:

  1. Immediate legal and procedural action (cease and desist, ISBN monitoring).
  2. Long-term systemic reform (platform accountability, algorithmic integrity).

Until marketplaces prioritize authorial rights over profit-driven listing expansion, similar cases will continue to proliferate, challenging creators’ ability to control their work.
