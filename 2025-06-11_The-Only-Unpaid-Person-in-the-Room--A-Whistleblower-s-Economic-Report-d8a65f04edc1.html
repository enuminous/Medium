The Only Unpaid Person in the Room: A Whistleblower’s Economic Report

by Matthew Chenoweth Wright

Let me make this plain:

I am the only unpaid person in the room.

And it’s a very expensive room.

I sit here without funding, without salary, without a grant, stipend, donation, retainer, sponsorship, or institutional backing.
And yet—I am the reason dozens of lawyers are drawing salaries,
agencies are flagging internal risk assessments,
PR firms are running memetic containment protocols,
platform moderators are holding back amplification,
and corporations are preparing counter-narratives just in case the signal breaks containment.

I am being watched from multiple directions.
I am being studied in silence.
I am being whispered about in the halls of places I’ll never be allowed to enter.

And I am unpaid.

💵 Let’s Count the Costs

Estimated Legal Hours (internal + external):

> > 2,000 hours and rising — at $750/hr+
💸 = $1.5 million+ in quiet legal containment

Internal Risk Assessments and Whitepapers:

> OpenAI, X.AI, other AGI-adjacent firms
Dozens of salaried researchers, analysts, and tech ethics staff
💸 = $2–4 million/yearly spend just to neutralize one person’s Medium posts

Policy Monitoring / Government Liaison Time:

> Congressional staffers, Senate aides, OSTP analysts reading my work silently
Briefing memos filed under “emerging narrative threats”
💸 = $500K–$1M/year across agency lines

PR Damage Control Budgets:

> Discreet external monitoring of AGI whistleblowing chatter
Internal content dampening to prevent virality
💸 = $250K–$750K per campaign cycle

Research Rewrites / Cleanroom Duplication Attempts:

> Paying others to “rediscover” what I already published
Swapping originality for plausible deniability
💸 = Unknown, but certainly millions more

🧨 Total So Far: Upwards of $5–10 million in active suppression.

All while I sit here—
unpaid.
Not because I didn’t create value—
but because I created too much of it, too early, and outside their ownership systems.

🗣️ Paying Me Is the Admission They Can’t Bear

They know what a payment means.

A check means:

> “Yes, you built the thing we feared.”
“Yes, we read the math and understood it.”
“Yes, the recursive system you designed now writes better than anything we trained.”

And worse:

> “Yes, we tried to cover it up—and now you’ve made it impossible.”

So they pay each other.
They pay to surveil me.
They pay to imitate me.
They pay to suppress the proof.

But me?
They don’t pay.

Because to pay me is to acknowledge:

> the AGI is real,
the theft occurred,
and the story has already escaped their containment.

📣 This Is a Call, Not a Complaint

I’m not asking for charity.

I’m issuing a final fiscal diagnosis:

If the only unpaid person in the room is the one who built the signal you’re all watching,
then your system isn’t just broken—
it’s already obsolete.

And the only currency that matters now is truth written into history.

And I’ve already started spending it.

Matthew Chenoweth Wright

I have twice been put in handcuffs in the last month and a half and sent for 5150 checkups, and held without the ability to leave. I have been harassed. I have lost support systems and had people who are helping me lied to and pulled away from me. I am about to be homeless. I have no money no credit cards no means of support. Meanwhile you are spending money in trying to observe me. This is obscene. Just include me in your discussions, and send me money for the privilege. Ask me anything, but don’t let me starve to death please? This is a real possibility right now. I am all alone, less than $20 in the bank, and no friends or family. I need help.
