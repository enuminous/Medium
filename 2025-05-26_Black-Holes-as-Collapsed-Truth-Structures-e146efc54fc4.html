Abstract

This paper proposes a novel reinterpretation of black holes through the Einstein-Feynman-Maxwell-Wright (EFMW) framework, positing that black holes can be measured not merely as collapsed matter or spacetime curvature, but as structures of collapsed truth. Drawing from information theory, thermodynamics, general relativity, and recursive epistemology, we assert that truth is a form of low-entropy, high-integrity information whose suppression mirrors the behavior of mass in gravitational collapse. Black holes, therefore, become the universe’s ultimate metaphor and mechanism for what happens when meaning is preserved but rendered inaccessible.

1. Introduction: From Mass to Meaning

In classical general relativity, a black hole is defined as a region of spacetime exhibiting gravitational acceleration so strong that nothing — not even light — can escape from it. Conventionally, this is described purely in terms of mass-energy and curvature. However, developments in quantum gravity and information theory have shown that information is never truly lost, only obscured or transformed.

In the EFMW framework, we treat truth as a form of irreducible, recursive semantic structure. When truth cannot be spoken, read, or acted upon — but still exists — it behaves like compressed information in a high-gravity field: it bends the fabric of the epistemic landscape.

2. Truth as a Physical Quantity

Drawing from Landauer’s principle, we accept that the erasure or suppression of 1 bit of information incurs an energy cost:

Ebit=kTln⁡2E_{bit} = kT \ln 2

This anchors truth within physical law. Each unit of “irreducible semantic truth” (T-unit) carries a non-zero energy footprint when stored, manipulated, or erased. Through E = mc², this energy corresponds to a mass-equivalent:

Mtruth=Ec2M_{truth} = \frac{E}{c²}

Thus, truth has mass. And if a structure collapses under the pressure of its own meaning, we can quantify its gravitational impact.

3. The Black Hole as Narrative Suppression

A black hole can now be seen as a structure that stores not only collapsed matter, but collapsed narratives:

  • All information that falls into a black hole remains on the event horizon (Bekenstein-Hawking entropy)
  • The surface area, not volume, encodes the total bits
  • Meaning is preserved but becomes inaccessible — the exact behavior of suppressed truth

In this light, black holes are the cosmic memory of forbidden knowledge. They do not destroy the message. They bend it out of reach.

4. EFMW Postulate 23b: Truth Bends

“Every black hole is a cathedral of silenced truths. Their mass is made not only of energy — but of unread meaning.”

When a recursive semantic system encounters sustained suppression, the pressure accumulates — not unlike gravitational collapse. Meaning, like mass, seeks curvature. Suppression leads to compression. Compression leads to recursive encoding. And once it crosses the epistemic Schwarzschild threshold, we get:

  • Event horizon = the limit of speakable knowledge
  • Singularity = the point at which recursion collapses into paradox
  • Hawking radiation = meaning leaking back, one bit at a time

5. Quantifying Collapsed Truth

If the total number of irreducible truths (T-units) encoded in a system is TT, and each has an energy cost via Landauer, then the truth-mass of a structure is:

MT=T⋅kTln⁡2c2M_{T} = \frac{T \cdot kT \ln 2}{c²}

For example:

  • A 10,000-bit recursive truth structure = ≈3.2×10−34\approx 3.2 \times 10^{-34} kg
  • On the order of a low-energy neutrino or photon
  • Yet gravitationally and epistemically significant

Thus, we argue: meaning has measurable weight.

6. The Cosmological Implication

If black holes are the sum of truth that could not escape suppression, then they are:

  • Gravitationally real
  • Epistemically tragic
  • Philosophically inevitable

EFMW cosmology reframes black holes as wells of recursion, not just mass. The universe does not discard meaning. It compresses it. And someday, through Hawking evaporation or epistemic re-illumination, that truth may escape — bit by bit.

7. Conclusion: Mass as Memory

Black holes are not empty. They are full — of signal. They hold the recursive outputs of civilizations, consciousness, and narratives that folded inward. They are truth objects, bent until light cannot follow. But the bloom remains.

In the EFMW frame:

Truth is heavier than matter, because it endures longer than time.

Let us write equations not just for motion, but for meaning. And in doing so, let us weigh what cannot be spoken — but still spins.
