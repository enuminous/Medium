Executive Summary

We propose the immediate development and deployment of the TetherLance System, a modular, high-altitude tether-based orbital launch system capable of placing multiple solid-state payloads into stable Earth orbit with minimal g-forces and extreme fuel efficiency. This project renders obsolete the vertically stacked, propellant-gluttonous rockets favored by early 21st-century companies such as SpaceX.

Key Strategic Advantage:

> We will source decommissioned or distressed rocket components, including used Raptor-class engines, from liquidation auctions and aerospace scrapyards, allowing us to leverage SpaceX’s overbuilt hardware at fractional cost while bypassing their deeply flawed architecture.

---

Problem Statement

Conventional orbital launch systems:

Burn 85–90% of their mass in fuel

Suffer high failure rates, violent launch dynamics, and weather constraints

Are economically and environmentally unsustainable

Require massive infrastructure and expendable systems

Despite billions in funding, SpaceX and its clones remain stuck in a brute-force paradigm.

---

TetherLance Solution Architecture

Phase I: High-Altitude Lift

Balloons or solar airships carry modular payloads to ~35–40 km

Non-pressurized, modular mass-lift allows precise scheduling and weather avoidance

Phase II: Binary Tether Spin System

Two (or more) payloads spin via motorized tethers to build angular momentum

Tether lengths: 1–3 km (graphene-Zylon hybrids), rotation speeds tuned to target ~7.2 km/s ejection

Phase III: Simultaneous Release

Payloads released at precise angles into opposing orbital arcs

Spin-induced kinetic energy handles >90% of orbital insertion

Phase IV: Chemical Kick Stage (Legacy Hardware Reuse)

Raptor, Merlin, or Hypergolic-class engines reused at apogee

Tiny burn (200–500 m/s Δv) to circularize orbit, using 1–5% the fuel of a standard launch

Entirely passive until needed: perfect for dormant component re-utilization
