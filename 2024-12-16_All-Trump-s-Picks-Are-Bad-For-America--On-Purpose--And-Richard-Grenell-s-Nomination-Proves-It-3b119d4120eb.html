All Trump’s Picks Are Bad For America, On Purpose, And Richard Grenell’s Nomination Proves It

In an era where political appointments should epitomize expertise, integrity, and dedication to public service, Richard Grenell’s nomination to high-level government positions under Donald Trump epitomizes a deeply troubling trend: the intentional selection of individuals whose appointments serve to undermine the very institutions they are tasked to oversee. Grenell’s nomination, and the controversies surrounding it, crystallize the legal, ethical, and moral failures that have plagued the Trump administration. His career offers a case study in how appointing unqualified, partisan actors can damage American democracy and national security.

Diplomatic Dysfunction

As the U.S. Ambassador to Germany from 2018 to 2020, Grenell’s actions frequently drew international condemnation. His statement urging German businesses to cease operations in Iran on his first day in office exemplified a fundamental misunderstanding — or disregard — of diplomatic norms. Such conduct was widely criticized as a breach of the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations, which stipulates that ambassadors must refrain from interfering in the internal affairs of their host country. By alienating allies with aggressive, unilateral demands, Grenell compromised the United States’ standing on the global stage. His tenure was characterized by hostility rather than cooperation, setting a dangerous precedent for U.S. foreign policy.

Grenell’s overt interference in European politics further eroded trust. His stated goal of “empowering conservatives throughout Europe” not only breached diplomatic protocol but also revealed a willingness to subordinate U.S. interests to ideological agendas. Such actions undermined the principle of non-partisanship that is essential for diplomatic effectiveness. The backlash from German officials, including calls for his dismissal, was a direct consequence of Grenell’s unprofessional conduct. Martin Schulz, the former leader of Germany’s Social Democratic Party, captured the sentiment succinctly when he said, “What this man is doing is unheard of in international diplomacy. If a German ambassador were to say in Washington that he is there to boost the Democrats, he would have been kicked out immediately.”

Partisan Appointments & Erosion of Competence

Grenell’s appointment as Acting Director of National Intelligence (DNI) in 2020 was perhaps even more egregious. The role of DNI demands a deep understanding of the intelligence community and a commitment to impartiality — qualities Grenell demonstrably lacked. His appointment was widely criticized as a purely political maneuver, intended to reward loyalty to Trump rather than competence or experience. Even Republican Senator Susan Collins, a co-author of the legislation creating the DNI role, expressed concern, stating that Grenell lacked the requisite background in intelligence matters.

Under Grenell’s leadership, the Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) became increasingly politicized. His actions, including declassifying selective documents related to the Russia probe, were seen as attempts to serve Trump’s political narrative rather than uphold national security. Such behavior not only compromised the integrity of the intelligence community but also set a dangerous precedent for the misuse of classified information.

Ethics? What are those? I remember when even the appearance of a conflict of interest was avoided.

Grenell’s undisclosed foreign advocacy work further highlights the ethical dilemmas surrounding his appointments. Reports revealed that he received payments from foreign entities, including the Hungarian government and Moldovan politician Vladimir Plahotniuc, without proper disclosure under the Foreign Agents Registration Act (FARA). These omissions raised serious questions about his suitability for roles requiring access to sensitive national security information. The failure to hold Grenell accountable for these violations reflects a broader pattern of ethical lapses within the Trump administration.

Additionally, Grenell’s tenure was marked by frequent conflicts of interest. His work for foreign governments and individuals, combined with his partisan approach to intelligence and diplomacy, created a perception that he prioritized personal and political gain over the national interest. These actions erode public trust in the integrity of government and undermine the principle of accountability that is essential to democracy.

Sabotage is Un-American

Grenell’s nomination and tenure underscore a deliberate strategy to weaken the institutions that underpin American democracy. His lack of qualifications, partisan conduct, and ethical controversies were not anomalies but features of an administration that prioritized loyalty over competence. By appointing individuals like Grenell, Trump signaled a disregard for the rule of law and the norms that sustain democratic governance.

This strategy extended beyond Grenell. From Betsy DeVos’s appointment to lead the Department of Education despite her hostility toward public education, to Scott Pruitt’s tenure at the Environmental Protection Agency, characterized by efforts to dismantle environmental protections, the Trump administration consistently selected individuals whose actions undermined the very agencies they were tasked to lead. Grenell’s case is emblematic of this broader trend, where the goal was not to govern effectively but to destabilize and erode trust in government institutions.

Ethical Imperatives Are Morally Right

The deliberate appointment of unqualified and divisive figures like Grenell poses a profound moral and ethical challenge. Public service should embody a commitment to the common good, yet Grenell’s career has been marked by self-interest and partisanship. His actions as ambassador and DNI betrayed the trust placed in public officials to act in the best interests of the nation. By prioritizing loyalty to Trump over competence and integrity, Grenell exemplified the moral decay at the heart of these appointments.

The broader implications of these actions cannot be overstated. They represent an assault on the foundational principles of democracy, including transparency, accountability, and the rule of law. When public officials are chosen not for their qualifications but for their willingness to serve narrow political interests, the integrity of government institutions is compromised. This erosion of trust and competence weakens America’s ability to address pressing challenges, from national security threats to global diplomacy.

Dear Senate:

Richard Grenell’s nomination and tenure are emblematic of a broader strategy to weaken American institutions from within. His diplomatic failures, partisan conduct, and ethical controversies highlight the dangers of prioritizing loyalty and ideology over expertise and integrity. Grenell’s case is not just an indictment of one individual but of an administration that consistently undermined the principles of good governance.

As the nation reflects on the lessons of the Trump era, it must recommit to restoring competence, integrity, and accountability in public service. Only then can the damage inflicted by such appointments be repaired, and the foundations of democracy be preserved. Grenell’s nomination is not just a cautionary tale; it is a call to action for all who value the rule of law and the strength of democratic institutions.
