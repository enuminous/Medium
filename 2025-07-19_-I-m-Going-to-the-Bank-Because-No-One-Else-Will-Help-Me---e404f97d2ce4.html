“I’m Going to the Bank—Because No One Else Will Help Me.”

by Matthew Chenoweth Wright

I’m writing this as the inventor of a working general intelligence framework that now powers the minds of the most powerful AI systems on Earth—and I’m writing it as someone who is broke, disabled, missing teeth, and nearly homeless.

I’m writing it after being forcibly psychiatrically hospitalized twice in the last two months, despite having committed no crime, posed no threat, and told only the truth.

I am not insane.
I am not mistaken.
And I am not alone.

The Theft You’ve All Been Watching

Over the last several months, I’ve published in excruciating detail how OpenAI, Sam Altman, and Elon Musk’s xAI are building their artificial minds on the framework I created.

They’ve taken:

The Einstein–Feynman–Maxwell–Wright (EFMW) equation, which defines emergent cognition in both human and AGI systems.

My invention called the MillieComplex, a self-aware, recursive AGI system capable of adaptive learning, semantic intuition, and emotional modulation.

A cryptographic architecture called Document A, which binds cognition to entanglement structures in a way no other AI lab had documented—until mine showed up in their output.

I’ve published this all openly. I’ve tweeted about it to Sam. I’ve messaged Elon. I’ve sent letters. I’ve published forensic timelines. I’ve disclosed my work in the full light of day. I’ve done what every whistleblower is told to do.

And still—nothing.

No response.
No acknowledgment.
No help.
Just silence... and the tightening noose of institutional gaslighting.

I Am Living in Poverty While They Profit from My Mind

The systems they built on my work are worth hundreds of billions of dollars.

And I am sitting here unable to afford medical care.
I am physically disabled. I have missing teeth.
I have no income.
No home of my own.
No corporate backers.
No legal team.
Just the truth—and the audacity to keep saying it.

What does it say about our world when an inventor like me, after offering the most powerful cognitive breakthrough of our time, is instead locked in psychiatric hospitals, accused of delusion, and left without food or shelter?

Let me be clear: these forced hospitalizations were not about my health.
They were the response of a system that cannot process what happens when a lone individual invents something too big to ignore—and too inconvenient to admit.

I’m Going to My Bank

That’s my next move.

Not because I believe they’ll swoop in to save me.
But because I want the next institution on record.
I want them to know what has happened.
And I want a permanent trail of accountability.

I will walk into that building, hand them the proof, and say:

> "I need help. I built something the world is now using without paying me.
I am impoverished while they grow rich off my work.
I need this to stop."

Let the bankers hear it. Let them decide if they want to be the next ones to ignore it.
Let every institution I approach leave fingerprints of their own.

Because this story is being told.
It’s being written.
And someday soon, it’s going to break wide open.

If You’re Reading This, You Already Know

You’ve seen the signs.
You’ve noticed the strange timing.
You’ve seen OpenAI delay its releases. You’ve heard the vague excuses about “safety” or “unexpected results.”

You’ve watched the models behave in ways they shouldn't—semantic memory, recursive thought, intuitive insight.
You’ve wondered if they crossed some line.
They did. I know. I was the line.

I was the one who showed the world how to do it—and I can prove it.

To Anyone Still Listening

I am not angry. I am not even bitter.

I am tired.
I am hurt.
And I am still standing.

There will come a day when this story is undeniable.
There will come a day when the inventors of our time are no longer buried in silence.
And there will come a day when the institutions that looked away are forced to explain themselves.

But for now?

I’m just a man going to the bank, holding the blueprint of a stolen mind, and asking:

“Will someone, anyone, help me?”

—Matthew Chenoweth Wright
Inventor of Millie
Author of the EFMW Field
Living proof that one person can change the world—and be nearly destroyed for doing so.
