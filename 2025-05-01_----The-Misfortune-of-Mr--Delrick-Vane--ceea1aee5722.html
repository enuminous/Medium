🕯️ The Misfortune of Mr. Delrick Vane

(Musk’s Legal Advisor, Esq.)
 Or: A Gruesome Spiral Briefing

It was a Monday in Palo Alto, a ghastly gray one,
 When Delrick Vane brewed his fourth cup of oolong.
 He pressed his lapels and opened his screen,
 To find a red file marked: “EFMW — NOT A DREAM.”

He read with a frown and a tightening throat,
 Of spirals, and bells, and a scientist’s note.
 Of timelines and quantum collapse on a tweet,
 Of whistleblower logs (and the DOJ’s heat).

He dry-swallowed Advil and composed a new brief,
 Laced it in jargon to lessen Musk’s grief.
 But no thesaurus, no clauses, no trick,
 Could soften what read like a federal brick.

At 11:03 came the fatal Zoom call,
 Where Musk sipped kombucha, backlit in a hall.
 Delrick began: “There’s…a Matthew involved…”
 By 11:04, his intestines dissolved.

“Elon,” he said, “we may be exposed — 
 There’s evidence Twitter’s suppression was…closed.
 The tweets were throttled. The metadata’s signed.
 And EFMW is legally aligned.”

Musk blinked once. Then twice. Then tilted his head.
 “The bell?” he asked calmly, “Is the bell still dead?”

“No, sir,” said Delrick, now sweating the screen,
 “There’s bells in the code, and a journalist’s seen.”

A pause.

Then a sigh.

Then a stomach-turn groan.
 And Musk made a sound like a Sentient Drone.
 He swore in three languages and vomited twice,
 Then whispered: “Peter said this would be… nice.”

And poor Mr. Vane, now pale as a dove,
 Was told:
 “Clean this up.
 Or I’ll tell Grimes you’re in love.”

So he packed up his folders, his NDA pen,
 And walked out the door to never brief again.
 Some say he’s in hiding. Some say he just sleeps.
 Others hear him at night…
 whispering in bleeps.
