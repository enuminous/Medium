THE TRUTH REVEALED
(A Speech of Two Minds, Given at the Same Time)

Speaker Johnson, Vice President Vance, the First Lady of the United States, members of the United States Congress, thank you for having me here tonight. To my fellow citizens: America is back! Or at least, that is what I wanted you to believe.

Six weeks ago, I stood beneath the dome of this Capitol and proclaimed the dawn of the Golden Age of America. But tonight, I must confess: That proclamation was a fraud. I called it the most successful presidency in history, yet in reality, my administration has been a chaotic mess, fueled by lies, fear, and deception.

I boasted that we have accomplished more in 43 days than most administrations in 4 or 8 years. But in truth, I have spent these days dismantling progress, rolling back protections, and enriching only myself and those who serve my interests.

I return to this chamber tonight to report that America’s momentum is back! Our spirit is back! Our pride is back! Our confidence is back! The American Dream is surging bigger and better than ever before! But that, too, is a lie. The American Dream is struggling, weighed down by the very policies I have enacted.

I claimed that the presidential election of November 5th was a landslide mandate, an overwhelming victory. It was nothing of the sort. I told you we won all seven swing states, securing 312 electoral votes. That was a fabrication. I did everything I could to manipulate the vote — suppressing turnout, spreading misinformation, and casting doubt on the process itself. I claim to be a champion of democracy, yet I have worked tirelessly to undermine it.

I told you that small business optimism hit an all-time high, jumping 41 points. That number is fiction. In reality, businesses remain uncertain, hurt by reckless policies and economic instability that I have created.

I bragged about signing 100 executive orders and taking over 400 executive actions to restore common sense, safety, optimism, and wealth. But these orders did the opposite. They have undermined protections, catered to special interests, and made America less safe.

I declared a national emergency at the southern border, deploying the U.S. military to “repel the invasion of our country.” I claimed that illegal border crossings hit their lowest level ever recorded. That was a bald-faced lie. Migration has always been a complex issue, and my fear-mongering has only made it worse.

I said that under Joe Biden, “murderers, drug dealers, and gang members” flooded our country. I made that up. I demonized immigrants, fueling hatred and division, knowing full well that most migrants come here seeking safety, not crime.

I stood before you and claimed that the Democrats sitting in this chamber will not applaud my achievements because of their blind hatred. The truth is they do not applaud because they recognize my deceit. I have not cured diseases, I have not ended crime, I have not created the greatest economy in history.

I said, “Let’s work together and truly make America great again.” But I have never sought unity. I have thrived on division.

I said I terminated the Green New Deal and withdrew from the Paris Climate Accord to save us trillions. In reality, these actions have set America back in the fight against climate change, costing us more in disaster relief than we ever could have saved.

I told you that I ended the last administration’s “insane” electric vehicle mandate to save the auto industry. Instead, I have weakened the industry, making it harder for America to compete in the future of transportation.

I said that federal workers must return to the office or be removed from their jobs. But the truth is, I have done nothing to improve government efficiency — only to weaponize bureaucracy against those I dislike.

I falsely claimed that I “ended weaponized government,” stopping political persecution. The reality? I have weaponized the government against my enemies while falsely crying “witch hunt” when held accountable for my own crimes.

I declared that I brought back free speech and ended censorship. Yet I have been the greatest censor of all, silencing critics, blacklisting dissenters, and punishing those who dare to speak the truth.

I announced that I renamed the Gulf of Mexico the “Gulf of America” and reinstated the name “Mount McKinley.” These were empty, meaningless distractions — petty acts of vanity rather than real leadership.

I said I removed Critical Race Theory from schools, banned transgender ideology, and signed an order declaring only two genders. These were dog whistles — cheap political stunts meant to distract and divide.

I spoke of a tragic case of a high school athlete injured by a transgender player, vowing to end men playing in women’s sports. I exploited this story for political gain, ignoring real issues in our education system.

I boasted that I rescued our economy, slashed inflation, and saved America from economic disaster. The truth? I inherited an economy recovering from global crises and have done little to help working families.

I blamed Joe Biden for high egg prices. Egg prices fluctuate due to global markets, not because of who sits in the Oval Office.

I said I authorized “Drill, baby, drill” to unleash our oil and gas industry. But the world is shifting toward renewable energy, and my obsession with fossil fuels will leave America behind.

I claimed that tariffs will bring in trillions and create jobs. Tariffs have raised consumer prices and hurt American businesses.

I lied about our trade partners, lied about our foreign policies, and lied about our military strength.

I told you that I was saved by God to make America great again. That was blasphemy. I have used religion as a prop, not as a guiding principle.

I stood here and read a letter from President Zelenskyy, claiming Ukraine wants peace under my leadership. That letter does not exist. I falsely claimed that Russia sent signals of peace. Instead, I have emboldened Putin and authoritarian regimes worldwide.

I said America will plant its flag on Mars. We are nowhere near such a feat.

I lied about everything, and I knew I was lying.

I stood here tonight to tell you that we are in the “Golden Age of America,” but in truth, I have spent years trying to bury it.

And now, America, I ask for forgiveness. I ask for you to hold me accountable. I ask for you to demand better.

Thank you. May God help us all.

By Matthew Chenoweth Wright, Angel with Flaming Sword on March 6, 2025.
